Once upon a time, in a forest where the night came softly and the moon painted everything in silver and shadow, there lived a cozy raccoon named Remy. [long pause]
Remy had the mask — the dark patches around his eyes that every raccoon has, which give them the permanent look of someone who knows something you don't, and is deciding whether to tell you. In Remy's case this was largely accurate. He was the most observant creature in the forest night, and the forest night was full of things worth observing. [sighs with quiet contentment]
He came out every evening at moonrise. [beat]
The forest at night was an entirely different place from the forest in day. Remy had lived in both and had firm opinions: the night version was better. Quieter in the right ways, louder in others. Cooler. More honest, somehow — the moon mask shadows stripped everything back to shape and movement and the question of what was actually there, without the distraction of color and brightness.
Tonight the moon was full and the forest night was at its most extraordinary. [softly]
Remy moved through it in his particular raccoon way — touching everything with his clever, sensitive paws as he went, reading the night through his fingertips as much as through his eyes. The bark of the birch. The cold of the stream. The softness of moss. The particular roughness of an acorn cap. [with growing realization]
He found, at the base of the old willow, a young fox curled tight and shivering — lost, from the look of it, uncertain in the dark forest in the way that creatures are when they're somewhere they haven't been before.
Remy sat beside her. [pacing]
"The stream," he said quietly, "runs south. Follow it and you'll reach the meadow. From the meadow you can see the lights of the farm, and from the farm you know your way."
The fox looked at him. "How do you know where I'm from?"
[softly] "Your paws," said Remy. "Field mud, not forest mud. Different smell." He stood and gestured with one masked face toward the sound of the stream. "Go. The moon will stay out long enough."
She went. [beat]
Remy watched until she was out of sight. Then he turned back to the moonlit forest night and continued his rounds, touching and watching and knowing, the same as every night, content in his place and his purpose.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
