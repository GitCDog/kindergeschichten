Once upon a time, in the warm shallows of a coral kingdom where the water shimmered in a hundred shades of blue and the fish moved like living jewels, there lived a brave seahorse named Sebastian. [long pause]
Sebastian was small even for a seahorse — he could curl his tail around a piece of coral and disappear entirely — but he had the straightest posture of any creature in the reef, which seahorses consider a great point of pride. He drifted through his days with his chin up and his tail curled just so, surrounded by the soft pop and hiss of ocean bubbles rising from the coral below. [sighs]
The coral kingdom was home. It was beautiful. But Sebastian had begun to wonder about the deep water beyond the reef's edge — the blue that darkened and went on forever. [beat]
"Don't go past the reef," the older seahorses always said. "There are currents out there."
"There are currents in here too," Sebastian pointed out, which nobody had a good answer to.
One morning he drifted to the very edge of the coral kingdom and looked out. The water beyond was deeper, darker, and very quiet. Ocean bubbles rose even out there — slow, single bubbles drifting up from somewhere far below, as if the ocean floor was sighing. [softly]
Sebastian uncurled his tail from the last piece of coral. [with growing realization]
He drifted out.
The current caught him immediately — not savagely, but firmly, the way a river has opinions. He let it move him, keeping his chin up, his tail steady, his eyes open. [pacing]
What he saw in those deep open waters: a school of fish so silver they looked like a single moving mirror. A jellyfish the size of a cartwheel, trailing threads of purple light. A sea turtle who nodded at him as if she had been expecting him.
He drifted back to the coral kingdom as the afternoon light changed. He curled his tail around his favorite piece of coral and stayed there a long while, looking out at where he had been.
[softly] He understood now that home is sweeter when you know what lies beyond it.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
