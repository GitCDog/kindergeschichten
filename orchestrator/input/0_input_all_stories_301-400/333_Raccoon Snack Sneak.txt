The raccoon's name was Bandit, and Bandit was the best sneak in the neighborhood.

Not the only sneak — urban environments produced good sneaks across many species. The cats were excellent night sneaks but lacked Bandit's manual dexterity, which was crucial when it came to the night snack portion of the evening. The squirrels were agile but had no patience for the methodical approach. The fox who sometimes passed through was a natural but didn't apply herself consistently.

Bandit was consistent.

The forest sneak began at the edge of the garden, where she assessed the situation before moving. Lights on in the kitchen: risk factor elevated. Dog in yard: route adjustment needed. Cat on fence: manageable, as long as the cat was the neighbor's tabby and not the large orange one who took unpredictable positions on raccoon passage.

The large orange cat was not visible. Good.

Bandit moved along the wall, keeping to the shadow, her mask face absolutely invisible in the darkness. She covered ground in a rolling, silent walk — her weight transferring carefully from foot to foot in an order she had optimized over three years of urban sneaking.

The compost bin was the goal.

The compost bin lid was supposedly raccoon-proof. The manufacturer had made this claim on the label, which Bandit had assessed in daylight during a reconnaissance mission and found overconfident.

Lid off in twelve seconds.

The night snack was varied and good: apple cores, leftover rice, something that had once been a vegetable with authority and was now simply soft and flavorful. Bandit ate with the methodical contentment of an animal that had done her homework.

She replaced the lid on her way out.

Back over the wall, back through the garden, back into the dark.

Forest sneak, complete.


Bandit reached the field edge by two in the morning, the night snack satisfactorily concluded. She washed her paws in the dew on the grass — another raccoon habit that seemed to be about fastidiousness rather than actual cleaning — and assessed the return route.

Clear. Quiet. The orange cat was on a different fence tonight.

She went home through the dark with the particular satisfaction of a professional who had performed a difficult task well. The sneak was an art. Tonight it had been art.
