The brave dream dragon arrived at midnight.

He came the way dream dragons always came — not through a window, not through a door, but through the space between waking and sleeping, which is not a physical space but a real one, and which has room for more than you might expect.

His name was Cinder, and his night sky home was the interior of a dream.

Cinder was not a terrifying dragon. He was large — room-filling large, which was possible in dreams — and he was warm, the way that things with fire in them are warm, not with the dangerous heat of a flame but with the deep warmth of something that generates its own light.

His star fire was the most useful thing about him.

When the dream that Mia was having turned difficult — when the running-through-thick-air feeling arrived, or the forgotten-something-important feeling, or the being-somewhere-strange-with-no-map feeling — Cinder would appear and exhale a small controlled star fire, and the dream would improve.

Not necessarily in content. Sometimes the difficult dream continued. But with Cinder there, it was manageable.

Mia had not invented Cinder consciously. He had arrived in a dream two years ago and had kept coming back. She hadn't told anyone about him because explaining a dream dragon felt complicated.

But she had told him things she hadn't told anyone else, and he had listened with the patient attention of a large creature that had no particular schedule.

Tonight he settled at the foot of the dream, his star fire low, his eyes — each one the size of a dinner plate — regarding her with the particular warmth of something that was, without being asked, on her side.

The night sky turned slowly around them.


Cinder stayed until just before dawn, when dreams end without cause and the mind begins its return to waking.

His star fire went low as the room lightened. He did not disappear — he simply became less necessary, which was the right way for something to end.

Mia woke feeling that the night had been okay, which was the most accurate description of a night with a dream dragon.

The night sky had been good.
