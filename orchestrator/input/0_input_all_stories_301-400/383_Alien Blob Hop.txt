The alien blob's name was Glob, and Glob had arrived on the third planet from the yellow star entirely by accident.

This was not unusual for blobs. Blobs traveled by spore, which were released into space during certain phases of their life cycle and drifted until they found somewhere hospitable. Glob had been drifting for twelve thousand years before the third planet's gravity caught her, and she had arrived in a forest on the temperate northern continent and had spent three days figuring out what she was on.

The planet hop from her home world had been a very long one.

Glob was, by the standards of her species, curious. Other blobs had arrived on planets and immediately begun the process of absorption — taking in nutrients from whatever was available. Glob's approach was slower. She had spent the three days watching.

The space giggle — an emotion that blobs experienced when finding something delightfully surprising — had occurred when she realized the planet was full of mobile organisms.

On her home world, the organisms did not move. Here, they moved constantly.

The small quadruped that investigated her on the second day — sniffing, backing away, circling, sniffing again — had produced the longest space giggle Glob had experienced in twelve thousand years of travel.

She extended a small pseudopod toward it.

The quadruped backed away.

She retracted it.

The quadruped came forward.

This exchange continued for some time, to Glob's great pleasure. The quadruped — a dog, though she had no name for it — was, she began to understand, also curious about her.

Two curious organisms, encountering each other for the first time on a planet that was hospitable to both.

The planet hop had been worth it.


Glob stayed in the forest for three weeks, which was long enough to begin understanding the mobile organisms better. The dog came back twice. The first time it brought a smaller dog. The second time it came alone and was less cautious.

Glob decided the planet was a good one. The space giggle continued to occur on a daily basis.

She extended a pseudopod toward a falling leaf. The planet hop, she concluded, had been entirely worthwhile.
