The cocoa mug had been in the family for twelve years, which was a long time for a mug.

Its name — the name the family used, not a name anyone had given it formally — was Big Blue, for reasons that required no explanation. It was large. It was blue. It held more cocoa than any other mug in the house, which was the primary reason it had survived twelve years of daily use.

The warm drink it was most associated with was, of course, the cocoa: dark and sweet and made properly, which in this family meant stirring in the powder with a small amount of warm milk before adding the rest, to prevent the lumps that were the mark of an improvised cocoa and not a planned one.

The marshmallow top was the child's contribution.

Every evening cocoa — which happened in winter and in summer and in autumn and in all but the hottest spring weeks — was concluded with the addition of two marshmallows from the bag kept on the third shelf. Two, not one, not three. Two.

They went on after the cocoa was poured, while it was still hot enough to soften them at the bottom while leaving the tops intact.

The child would drink the cocoa down to the marshmallow line and then eat the marshmallows with a spoon.

This had been the ritual for eight years.

Big Blue had been present for all of it. He had held approximately two thousand cocoas. He had chips on his handle and a tea stain ring inside that no amount of washing had entirely removed.

He was full tonight.

The marshmallows were floating. The kitchen was warm and the window outside was dark and the child was sitting at the table with both hands around the mug.

Big Blue was exactly where he was supposed to be.


Big Blue was washed and returned to the cupboard, clean and ready for tomorrow. The warm drink ritual required a clean mug. This was not a stated rule; it had simply always been so.

The child was asleep by eight, which the cocoa had assisted with in the reliable way it always did. Big Blue sat in the dark cupboard, waiting for tomorrow's evening.

Forty-three cups a year, twelve years: he had held a great deal of cocoa and a great deal of evening.
