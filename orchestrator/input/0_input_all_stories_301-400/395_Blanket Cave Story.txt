The blanket cave was the oldest architecture in the house.

Not in terms of construction date — the house was forty years old, and the blanket cave had been rebuilt several hundred times over the eight years of its existence. But in terms of tradition, the blanket cave was older than the sofa it was draped over, older than the specific blanket it used (which had been replaced twice), older than the children who currently built it.

The curious blanket cave had begun with the oldest child's first attempt at making a hiding space.

The hide space started as two chairs and a sheet. Over the years it had evolved: more chairs, better attachment points discovered by trial and error, the addition of a clip that held the front edge up (creating a proper entrance), the strategic use of a cushion to block the gap at the back.

Each refinement had been passed on.

The current builders — Mia, aged ten, and Eli, aged seven — had inherited a design with three years of optimization and made a few contributions of their own. Eli had introduced the concept of the interior pillow arrangement, which greatly improved the cozy world of the cave's floor. Mia had solved the problem of the entrance flap being difficult to secure from inside.

The blanket cave was at full build as of three-fifteen.

Inside, the light was dim and orange-brown through the blanket fabric. The sound from outside was muffled. The small space held two children, two pillows, a selection of library books, and a packet of crackers.

"No adults," Eli said.

"Already stated," Mia said.

"Just confirming."

The blanket cave held them in its cloth walls, which were adequate, which was the right word for a thing built from furniture and imagination and which was, from inside, more than adequate.


The blanket cave was dismantled at five-thirty — the chairs returned, the blanket folded (imprecisely), the cushions replaced on the sofa.

Mia noted two improvements for next time: a better solution for the entrance flap, and the addition of a small lamp to make the cozy world interior brighter.

Eli ate the last cracker and considered this a full afternoon.

The hide space would return when conditions were right, which was most rainy Saturdays.
