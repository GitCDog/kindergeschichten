The brave galaxy guard's name was Shield, and Shield had been on watch since the beginning.

Not the beginning of time — that was too large. The beginning of her watch, which had begun when her civilization had developed the technology to observe the space between galaxies and had decided, rationally, that observing was useful.

The star shield was Shield's responsibility.

In practice, this meant monitoring the region of space between her galaxy and the nearest neighboring galaxy for anything that moved in unexpected ways. Rogue stars, which were ejected from their parent galaxies by gravitational interactions, moved through intergalactic space without the context of a galaxy around them — alone, dark, cold, moving in whatever direction they had been sent by the event that ejected them.

Most of them were not a threat to anything.

But tracking them was useful data. Over generations, Shield's civilization had built a map of where things were and where they were going, and the map told them things about the history of the galaxies that could not be learned any other way.

The cosmic watch was patient work.

Shield had been on watch for what would be, by human reckoning, eleven thousand years. Her kind lived long. She had seen things that took centuries to resolve — gravitational interactions between star clusters that played out in slow motion from her perspective, fast motion from a human's.

She had seen nothing that required an alarm.

This was good news. The best cosmic watch was an uneventful one.

She filed her regular report with the civilization's central record and returned her attention to the star shield region.

Stars moved in their patient orbits. The galaxy turned.

The watch continued.


The star shield region remained clear.

Shield filed her report and returned to observation. The cosmic watch was patient work, suited for those who found significance in consistency rather than event.

She had found, over eleven thousand years, that most of the universe was consistent and that this was not boring. Consistency was the foundation everything else rested on.

The galaxy turned. The watch continued.
