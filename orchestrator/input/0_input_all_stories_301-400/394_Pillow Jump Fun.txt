The rule was: no jumping on beds.

The rule had been in place for three years and had been enforced with varying levels of consistency, and its current status — a Tuesday afternoon in half-term with both parents out and a grandmother in the house who had strong views on children needing to run off energy — was effectively suspended.

The playful pillow jump began at four o'clock.

Lena had suggested it, which was typical. Her brother Sam had agreed, which was also typical. Their grandmother had said, "Take the pillows off first," which was an implied endorsement.

The bed bounce on a properly made bed was, as anyone who had investigated the physics of the matter would confirm, a genuinely useful springiness. The mattress deformed under the impact and returned most of the energy, and the result was — if you were under a certain weight and under a certain height — a genuine series of progressively larger bounces.

Sam had discovered this in a rigorous way two years ago and had the data, loosely speaking.

The soft fun of the pillow jump came from adding the pillows back into the mix.

Not on the bed — that was less efficient. But on the floor beside the bed, where they acted as a landing pad for the dismount jump — the final leap off the bed and onto the pillows, which absorbed the impact in the most satisfying way.

Their grandmother appeared in the doorway at four-fifteen.

She watched for a moment.

"One at a time," she said, and went back to the kitchen.

The bed bounce continued under the new orderly conditions until five o'clock, when tiredness and the smell of something warm cooking from downstairs brought the pillow jump fun to its natural end.


At dinner, Lena was asked how the afternoon had been.

'We did the pillow jump,' she said.

Their grandmother said nothing, which in this family's translation meant: I know, and it was fine, and you're welcome.

Sam was quiet for the whole of dinner and asleep before eight, which was the expected outcome of an afternoon that had spent energy the way a good afternoon should.
