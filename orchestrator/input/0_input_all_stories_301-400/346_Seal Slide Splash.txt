The ice hill had been formed by a pressure ridge, and it was, by the standards of the arctic coast, a modest hill. Perhaps three meters tall. A gentle slope on the north side — the friendly slide side — and a steeper drop on the south.

The seals had been using it for generations.

The ice hill slide was not a recreational activity, technically. It was an efficient way to move between the water and the higher ice surface — a shortcut born of physics and repeated use. But what was efficiency in an animal with no arms was indistinguishable from enjoyment, and the seal who used it most regularly, a young grey named Silv, had refined the experience beyond pure transport.

The water splash at the bottom was the best part.

A proper ice hill slide generated enough momentum to carry you off the ice edge at speed, and if the angle was right, you entered the water not with a vertical drop but with a long flat arc — a belly-flop, in essence, but executed at seven kilometers an hour and with a grace that belly-flops rarely achieved.

Silv had perfected the approach.

She positioned herself at the top of the hill — always the same spot, marked by the accumulated smooth patch of her repeated landings — assessed the bottom, and went.

The slide was four seconds of acceleration and one second of perfect cold.

Then the water — dark and immediate and home — and the transition from ice creature to water creature that happened in an instant, her body adjusting its whole orientation as the sea took her.

She surfaced twenty meters out and turned to look at the hill.

Another seal had already taken her spot at the top.

Silv dove into the cold and swam north, the ice slide splash still tingling at her whiskers.


Silv used the ice hill slide eleven times that morning, which was not a record but was a respectable session. By noon the sun had softened the slide's surface and the friction was wrong, so she moved to the water and swam north.

The hill would be better tomorrow, when the overnight cold refroze the surface. She would be back.
