The cave had been home to the bat colony for longer than any of the current bats could trace. The older ones had heard from their parents that their parents had been born here, which placed the colony's tenure at least four generations back and probably much longer — bats didn't keep detailed records.

What they did keep were echo maps.

The curious bat who had the most complete map of the cave was named Pip, and Pip's map existed not as a drawing or a memory in the human sense, but as a deep body-knowledge of the cave's exact geometry — the height of each chamber, the width of each passage, the location of every stalactite and pool and rough section of wall.

This knowledge was built from years of echolocation. Every pulse Pip sent out and every echo she received had contributed another data point to her understanding of the bat cave's shape. She navigated it in total darkness at high speed without error, because the echo map was precise enough to allow it.

The wing map was different from the echo map but worked with it.

The wing map was Pip's knowledge of how to fly in the cave's particular air — where the updrafts came from, where the cave breathed cold from a crack near the east wall, where the warm air from the deeper chambers rose and created a thermal she could use on the outward journey.

A researcher had spent a summer in the cave's entrance, recording bat flight paths on infrared camera. The footage showed something like choreography — multiple bats navigating the same spaces at high speed, never colliding, each one's path making way for the others without apparent communication.

But the communication was there. It was just too fast and too different to see.

Pip flew the cave that night, her echo sound mapping everything it found.


Pip returned to the colony roost at dawn, folding her wings in the particular way she had developed for the cave's ceiling texture. The echo map said: twenty-two centimeters from the wall, slight outward curve, secure grip.

The colony settled into daytime sleep around her, their wing map knowledge of the cave filling the air with a warmth of bodies and breath.

Until dusk, Pip held the map inside her — complete and available, ready for the next night.

[pause] Good night my little friend
