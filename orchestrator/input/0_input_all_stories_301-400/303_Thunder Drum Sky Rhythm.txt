Up in the highest storm cloud, surrounded by lightning and rain and the smell of charged air, there lived a playful thunder drum named Boom who was responsible for all the thunder in the region.

Boom was not a drum in the ordinary sense. He was a force of nature shaped roughly like a drum, with two great cloud-hands that struck themselves together to make the sky rhythm that everyone below called thunder.

The storm beat was his art, and he had been perfecting it for many seasons.

He had learned early that thunder was not simply noise. It was music — the music of clouds in conversation. A single sharp crack meant one thing. A long rolling boom meant another. The quick stuttering rat-a-tat of a summer storm was a fast song. The slow, heavy drumbeat of a winter storm was something more serious.

On the afternoon of the great storm, Boom was in the middle of his finest performance.

He had been building it for an hour — starting softly, a few distant rumbles to let the world below know something was coming. Then louder, the storm beat finding its tempo, the lightning playing counterpoint above him in brilliant silver lines.

A small cloud named Wisp drifted up to watch.

"How do you know what rhythm to play?" Wisp asked.

Boom struck the sky twice — a deep, resonant double beat. "I listen," he said.

"To what?"

"To everything. The wind tells me its speed. The lightning tells me its angle. The rain tells me its weight." He brought his cloud-hands together in a long rolling boom that traveled forty kilometers before it faded. "The storm has its own rhythm. I just have to find it."

Wisp was quiet for a moment, listening to the rain.

"I hear it," Wisp said finally, though it sounded surprised.

"You always could," Boom said. "You just hadn't listened before."

The storm moved on through the afternoon, Boom's sky rhythm carrying it from one end of the valley to the other. By evening, the clouds had thinned and the first stars were appearing.

Boom rested his cloud-hands and was still.

The silence after thunder has its own music too.
