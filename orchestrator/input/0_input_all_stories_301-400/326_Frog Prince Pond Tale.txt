In the reed-bordered pond at the center of the water meadow, there was a frog named Prince who had never been kissed and had no expectation of being transformed into anything.

He was a frog. He was perfectly satisfied with this.

Prince's lily crown was a joke that the other pond creatures had developed over several seasons — a reference to the large lily pad he always sat on, which happened to have a ring of smaller lily pads around it, like a natural throne. He had never requested this arrangement. The lily pads had simply grown that way, and he had found the central pad comfortable, and the name had followed.

The pond kingdom was not formally organized.

There was no government, no hierarchy, no official policy on anything. But there was an understood order — the herons arrived at dawn and the frogs went quiet, the dragonflies claimed the sunny margins in afternoon, the moorhens held the northern reeds, and everyone knew not to disturb the large carp who lived in the deepest part and had strong feelings about being disturbed.

Prince's contribution to the pond kingdom was the evening chorus.

Every dusk, when the light left the water and the air cooled, Prince would begin. A single long call — deep and resonant for a frog of his size — that served as the signal. Within minutes the pond would be full of sound: his call answered and elaborated by the other frogs, each one adding their note to the collective song.

The frog pond tale was this: every evening, something happened that hadn't been arranged by anyone. It simply emerged from the pond's many voices finding each other in the dusk.

Prince called and listened.

The pond answered.

He sat on his lily crown and felt the sound move through the water and the reeds and the cooling air.


The chorus went on for an hour and then faded, frog by frog, as each one found its sleeping position in the reeds or below the lily pads.

Prince called last and listened to the pond settle. The water was dark and still, the lily pads silver in the moonlight.

He blinked slowly. The pond kingdom was quiet and safe and exactly as it should be.

[pause] Good night my little friend
