The asteroid had been drifting since the solar system was young.

Not drifting in the way things drift on Earth — currents and winds and the casual movement of water. Drifting in the way things drift in space: along a precise orbital path determined by initial conditions four and a half billion years ago, adjusted very slightly by every gravitational interaction since, slowly curving through the inner solar system on an ellipse that repeated itself every four years.

The curious asteroid's name, in the catalog, was a number: 2019-KL4. To the astronomer who had discovered it, she was Pebble.

Pebble was approximately three kilometers across — large enough to cause consequences if she struck a planet, small enough that she would mostly vaporize on entry. She was made of chondrite — the most common asteroid type, the basic building material of the solar system, unchanged since the beginning.

She carried water ice in her interior.

This had been discovered by a spectrometer that had analyzed the light reflecting from her surface and found the signature of hydrated minerals. Somewhere inside Pebble was water that had been frozen for four and a half billion years — water that predated the Earth, that had been formed before the sun reached its current stable state.

The rock spin of her rotation was seventeen hours and twenty-two minutes, which meant the same face pointed toward the sun for that long before the other side took its turn.

Pebble passed within two million kilometers of Earth on her current orbit.

A telescope tracked her for six months as she approached and receded.

She didn't notice. Asteroids don't notice things. But she carried her ancient water through the solar system, patient and cold, on a path that repeated itself every four years until something changed it.


Pebble's next close approach would be in four years, and a mission was being proposed to send a small spacecraft to her — not to do anything, just to take a closer look at the chondrite surface and the water-bearing minerals.

The rock spin continued at seventeen hours and twenty-two minutes.

Pebble drifted on her four-billion-year orbit, indifferent and ancient, carrying her water through the solar system one loop at a time.
