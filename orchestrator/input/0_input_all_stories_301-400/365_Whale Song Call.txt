The brave whale's song traveled farther than any other sound in the ocean.

His name, if whales had names that humans could use, might have been Deep, because he spent most of his life in the deepest water of his range — a canyon that dropped three kilometers below the surface and held the cold water in which his particular songs traveled best.

The deep sea was his concert hall.

The echo call was how he found others of his kind across the ocean's vast distances. Sound in deep water, at the right depth, could travel thousands of kilometers — the ocean's own long-distance communication system, operating in frequencies below what human ears could catch without assistance.

Deep sang every day.

The songs were long — each complete song could last twenty minutes — and they were not fixed. They changed over the season and changed from year to year, which was something researchers had found remarkable. Whales within a population sang the same song, and the song evolved, and the evolution spread across the population in one direction, as if the whales were passing a melody around and each adding something.

This was music, or something enough like it that the distinction seemed pedantic.

Tonight's song began with the deep register — a long, low phrase that started below the canyon's rim and spread outward in all directions. The echo call moved through the cold water at fifteen hundred meters per second.

Somewhere, a few thousand kilometers away, another whale heard it.

She sang back.

Deep couldn't hear her response yet — it would take hours to arrive. But he knew it was coming. He sang the next phrase and the next, building the call that would be received and answered across the deep sea in the particular patience of large animals and large distances.


The response arrived eleven hours later.

Deep heard it on the next morning's singing session — faint, at the edge of his range, the echo call bent slightly by its long journey through thermoclines and distance.

But recognizable. A known voice, sending back what it had heard.

Deep sang the next phrase in the long exchange, which would continue across the months of the migration, the ocean carrying both voices in the patient dark.
