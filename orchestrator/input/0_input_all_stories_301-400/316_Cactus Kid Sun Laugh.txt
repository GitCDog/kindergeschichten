In the middle of the desert, where the heat was so consistent it had become a kind of furniture, there was a young cactus named Spike who was developing a philosophy.

The philosophy began with the sun.

The sun laugh was Spike's name for the moment each morning when the first direct light hit him and he felt the warmth spread through his green skin — a sensation that other plants in other places might have found intolerable, but which Spike had come to associate with everything good. The sun arrived. He was warm. He grew.

This seemed sufficient for a philosophy.

Spike's spike garden was the square meter of desert immediately around him, and it was more interesting than it looked. In the morning, dew collected on his spines and ran down to the soil below. In the afternoon, his shadow moved across the sand, and small things moved with it — a beetle seeking shade, once a tiny lizard who sat at the edge of his shadow and watched him with one orange eye.

The lizard seemed to share Spike's appreciation for the sun, though it dealt with the sun laugh differently, preferring to stand in it for short intervals and then retreat.

A hummingbird visited three times a week, arriving with the particular sound of a very small thing moving very fast and departing the same way. It came for the flowers Spike produced twice a year — small and yellow and lasting only three days, but filled with something the hummingbird valued enough to travel across the desert to reach.

Three days of flowers, for the rest of the year in spines and sun.

Spike had decided this was not a bad arrangement. You didn't need to flower all the time. You needed to flower when you were ready, and the rest of the time you could simply be what you were — green and spiked and warm in the sun and contributing in the particular way that you alone could contribute.

That was the whole of the philosophy.


Spike grew a new spine in spring. He always did — a tiny one, just visible at the top of his right shoulder if you looked closely.

The sun arrived and the sun laugh happened, as it always did, and Spike greeted the day the only way he knew: by being exactly himself, spiked and green and warm, in the middle of the desert that suited him perfectly.
