Once upon a time, in a flower garden so full of color and scent that even the bees sometimes had to stop and simply breathe it in, there lived a little bee named Blossom. [long pause]
Blossom was part of a very large family — her hive was home to thousands — but she had always felt that she personally was responsible for the flower garden's wellbeing. Not because anyone had told her so. Just because someone had to care, and she seemed to be the one who cared most. [sighs contentedly]
Every morning she made the same round. Lavender first — the long purple rows at the garden's eastern edge, still cool from the night. Then the roses, which required patience because of the thorns. Then the foxgloves, which were tall and tubular and required a specific approach that Blossom had spent a whole spring perfecting. Then the wild corner at the back, where the forget-me-nots and poppies grew without any human help and were all the more beautiful for it. [beat]
She knew every flower by character, not just by name. The roses were proud. The lavender was generous. The foxgloves were dramatic. The forget-me-nots were steady and reliable in the way that only quiet things can be. [softly]
One morning in late summer, she arrived at the honey hive to find it busier than usual — excited buzzing, wings fanning everywhere. [with growing realization]
"We've been awarded," said the queen, unusually animated. "Best garden honey in the county."
Blossom thought about this as she made her rounds that afternoon. Best garden honey. Which meant best flower garden. Which meant every careful, faithful, loving visit she had made to every flower through every season had added up to something.
[pacing] She landed on a foxglove — one of the dramatic ones — and collected her pollen in the unhurried way she always had.
The garden hummed around her. Bees moved in their familiar patterns. The flowers nodded in the breeze.
[softly] Blossom understood something then that is easy to forget: small, steady, daily care is not small at all. It is how beautiful things happen.
She flew home as the sun went gold, carrying the best of the flower garden with her.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
