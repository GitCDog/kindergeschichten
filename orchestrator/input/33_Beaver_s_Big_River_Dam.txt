Once upon a time, on the banks of a rushing river that foamed and churned and never seemed to stop for even a moment, there lived a brave beaver named Ben. [long pause]
Ben had strong flat teeth, a broad tail he used like a rudder, and an absolutely unwavering belief that the river needed a dam. Not for any selfish reason — the dam would make a calm pool for the ducks, shelter the fish from the worst of the flooding, and give the smaller creatures a safe place to drink without being swept away. [sighs determinedly]
The only problem was: it was a very big river. And Ben was one beaver. [beat]
He started on a Monday. He felled a birch tree — it took all morning — and dragged it to the water. The rushing river immediately swept half of it sideways. He retrieved it. He propped it. The river pushed again. Ben pushed back. [pacing]
By Wednesday he had the beginning of a framework, but it kept shifting. By Thursday his paws were sore and his back ached and he sat on the bank looking at the rushing river with an expression that even the cheerful ducks recognized as tired.
A small voice spoke beside him. [softly] "You need another pair of hands."
Ben looked around. An otter was watching from a rock, arms folded. "I've been watching you all week," the otter said. "I know where the best heavy branches are, upstream. I can get them to you faster than you can fetch them."
[with growing realization] Ben opened his mouth. Closed it again. He had been trying so hard to do it alone that he hadn't thought to ask.
"I'd be grateful," he said.
The otter called two friends. Ben called a cousin. By the weekend there were seven of them — beavers and otters working side by side on the rushing river, fitting branch to branch, sealing gaps with mud and stones. [beat]
When the wooden dam held for the first time — when the water pooled behind it, still and glassy and calm — every creature on the bank cheered.
Ben looked at the still pool. Then at his friends. "I couldn't have done it without you," he said simply.
"That's rather the point," said the otter.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
