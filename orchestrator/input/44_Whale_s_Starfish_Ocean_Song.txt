Once upon a time, in the deep and wide and endlessly moving ocean, there lived a whimsical whale named Willow. [long pause]
Willow was very large — as whales tend to be — with a voice that carried for miles through the water, a heart the size of a small car, and a song that she sang every evening as the light changed above the surface. Not all whales sang. Willow couldn't imagine not singing. [sighs musically]
Her ocean song was a rolling, rising, falling thing — notes so long they became more like feelings than sounds. The fish stopped and listened. The squid paused in their travels. Even the cold deep water seemed to warm slightly when Willow sang. [beat]
But Willow had a dream: she wanted to sing with the starfish. Everyone said this was impossible. Starfish, they pointed out, did not sing. Starfish sat on the ocean floor and were, decoratively speaking, beautiful, and that was largely the extent of their evening plans.
Willow didn't believe in impossible. [softly]
She dove down one evening to where the starfish rested on the sandy floor — dozens of them, in every color, arranged like a scattered sky. She hovered above them and began to sing. [pacing]
The song moved through the water and reached the starfish.
[with growing realization] And one by one — so slowly you almost missed it — the starfish began to glow. Each one lit from within, a pulse of soft color in time with Willow's notes. Red, orange, gold, pink — all of them breathing light in the rhythm of her ocean song.
They couldn't sing with their voices. But they sang with their light.
Willow hovered above her glowing, pulsing, starfish friends and felt something in her enormous chest that was bigger even than her song. [softly]
She had been right. It wasn't impossible at all. It just needed someone willing to ask the question.
She sang until the starfish lights faded gently into sleep, and then she rose back up through the dark water toward the surface stars.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
