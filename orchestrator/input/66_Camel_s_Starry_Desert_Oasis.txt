Once upon a time, in a desert so vast that standing in the middle of it felt like standing at the center of everything, there lived a curious camel named Caspian. [long pause]
Caspian had two humps, long eyelashes that kept out the sand, and a way of walking that made the desert feel less large — a steady, rocking, reliable gait that could carry him all day without complaint. He knew every dune by shape and smell. He knew where the hard-packed ground was and where the soft sand would swallow your hooves. He had never, however, found the desert oasis. [sighs]
Not for lack of trying. [beat]
The oasis was said to appear at the center of the largest star dunes — the great crescents of sand that shifted slowly across the desert floor. It didn't appear every night. Only on certain nights, when the star dunes aligned just so and the sky above was completely clear and the stars came down close enough to touch the tops of the dunes with their light. [softly]
Caspian had been looking for three years.
On the night he found it, he almost walked past it. [with growing realization]
He had been crossing the star dunes in the blue dark before dawn, moving by instinct and the feel of the sand underfoot, when he noticed the light. Not starlight — something warmer, something at ground level. He stopped. He looked.
Between two great star dunes, in a hollow that hadn't been there the week before, a pool caught the sky — perfectly still, perfectly round, reflecting the stars so clearly that it was impossible to tell where the water ended and the night began. Around its edge, three palm trees — real ones, green and alive — stood as if they had always been there. [pacing]
Caspian stood at the edge of the desert oasis for a long time without drinking. The water could wait. He wanted to look first.
[softly] The star dunes rose around him, quiet and enormous. The stars burned overhead and below in the still pool — the same stars, twice, the real and the reflection equally luminous.
He understood then why the oasis was so hard to find. You had to be the kind of traveler who didn't give up. The desert kept it for those who kept going.
He drank deep. The water tasted of distance and arrival.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
