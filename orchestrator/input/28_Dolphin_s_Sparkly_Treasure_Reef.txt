Once upon a time, in the warm blue-green waters of a bay ringed by golden cliffs, there lived a playful dolphin named Dash. [long pause]
Dash could leap higher than any other dolphin in the bay, swim faster than the current in a storm, and spin in the air with such grace that the seagulls above him would sometimes stop mid-flight just to watch. He was excellent at all of it. But there was one thing Dash had never done. [sighs]
He had never found the treasure reef. [beat]
Every dolphin in the bay knew the legend: a reef so deep and so beautiful that it was full of glittering things — not gold or jewels, but the real treasures of the sea: shells that sang, stones that glowed, and coral so vivid it hurt your eyes to look at it, in the best possible way. But the treasure reef was said to be so far down that the water went from blue to black before you reached it, and no one had ever come back to say they'd found it for certain.
Dash, being Dash, had decided to look. [softly]
He dove at sunrise, spinning as he went — because even serious searches were better with style. The water went from turquoise to blue to deep blue to indigo. The light from above became a small pale circle. [pacing] His clicks and calls echoed back from things he couldn't see.
And then — [with growing realization] — there.
Below him, in the last of the dim light, something shimmered. A reef so full of color and light that it seemed to hum. Shells arranged in patterns too beautiful to be accidental. Coral in every shade of pink and orange and gold. Stones that pulsed with a faint warm glow, as if they had eaten the sunlight from years ago and were only now giving it back.
Dash floated above it for a long, still moment.
[softly] Then he turned around and swam back up.
He found his friends playing at the surface and called to them in clicks and leaps and the language of pure excitement.
It took three hours, but he led all of them back down. Together. Because beautiful things, Dash had decided, should never be kept to yourself.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
