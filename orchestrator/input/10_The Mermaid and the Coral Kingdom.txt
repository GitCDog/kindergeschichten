Once upon a time, in the warmest and most beautiful part of the ocean, there lived a little mermaid named Marina. [long pause] Her tail was the color of sea glass — green with flickers of blue — and her hair floated around her like ribbons of dark seaweed in a gentle current. She lived in an underwater castle built from white shells and smooth round stones, with towers that shimmered faintly in the blue-green light. [beat] Marina loved her castle. But she had a problem. A quietly growing, worrying problem. The colorful coral garden just outside her castle walls had begun to fade. The bright pinks were turning pale. The vivid oranges were going ashy and gray. The purples were fading to nothing. The garden that had once blazed like a underwater rainbow was becoming ghostly and dull. [sighs] "What's wrong?" Marina asked a passing sea turtle, who was old and wise and had seen many things. The turtle slowed. He looked at the coral. [softly] "They need singing," he said, in his slow, thoughtful way. "Colorful coral grows best when it hears music. It has always been so." Marina looked down at the pale garden. Then she looked at her hands. [with growing realization] She had a voice. A lovely voice, actually — warm and clear, like water over smooth stones. But she had always kept it tucked inside her. Afraid it wasn't quite good enough. Afraid others might not want to hear it. She swam to the very center of the coral garden. She closed her eyes. [beat] And she sang. It started small — a soft, rolling melody, like the sound of waves at night. But it grew. It filled the water around her. It carried in every direction through the deep blue quiet. [pacing] And something wonderful began to happen. Tiny bubbles rose from the coral, one by one. The pale pink deepened — slowly at first, then all at once — into the brightest rose. The gray orange blazed back to amber, to gold. Violet crept back through the branches like ink moving through water. Fish gathered to listen. Seahorses swayed in place. An octopus settled on a rock, perfectly still, all eight arms folded neatly. Even the old sea turtle stopped and smiled his slow turtle smile. [softly] When Marina finally opened her eyes, the colorful coral was more beautiful than she had ever seen it — more beautiful than she had known it could be. She had learned something that day that she would never forget: the gifts you keep locked inside yourself aren't keeping you safe. They're keeping the world a little less bright. The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
