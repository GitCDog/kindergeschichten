Once upon a time, deep inside the walls of a very old farmhouse, there lived a tiny mouse named Marble. [long pause]
Marble had gray fur, bright black eyes, and the quietest feet in the whole world. She could cross a kitchen floor without making a single sound. She was very good at being invisible, which is a useful skill — but she had a dream that required being seen. [sighs]
Marble wanted to find the cheese castle. [beat]
The cheese castle was a legend among the mice of the farmhouse — a great round wheel of aged cheese, somewhere in the cellar, so large it had been carved over the years by the mice of generations past into a proper castle, complete with towers and a gate and a great hall where, it was said, the walls sweated golden oil and the air tasted of something ancient and wonderful.
No one Marble had spoken to had actually been there. No one had gone on a quiet adventure to find out if it was true. [softly]
Marble decided she would.
She set off one Tuesday evening, when the family upstairs was watching television and the cat was asleep in the armchair. Down through the wall. Along the pipe. Through the gap behind the water heater. Down the steps. Into the cellar. [pacing]
The cellar smelled extraordinary. Of old wood and cool stone and — yes. Something warm and sharp and golden.
Marble followed her nose. Around a stack of paint tins. Behind the old wardrobe. And then she stopped. [with growing realization]
It was real.
Not enormous — smaller than the legend, perhaps, no taller than Marble herself — but unmistakably a castle, carved with tremendous care from a great round wheel of cheese. Tiny windows. A proper gate. Walls smooth as stone, golden as afternoon.
Marble sat before it in the dark cellar for a very long time. [softly]
Then she took one small, careful bite from the corner of the outer wall — where it wouldn't show — and it tasted exactly like all the best things in the world at once.
She went home and told everyone. Next Tuesday, they all went together.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
