Once upon a time, in a forest where the winter came deep and the snow covered everything in a silence so complete it felt like a held breath, there lived a little lynx named Luna. [long pause]
Luna had tufted ears, golden eyes, and paws so large and soft they left barely any mark on the snow — which was the point. She was built for exactly this: the white forest, the cold air, the stillness. Her coat was the gray-buff of snow shadow, and when she sat still under a pine tree she was as close to invisible as a living thing can be. [sighs with the deep peace of perfect belonging]
She moved through the snowy forest like a whisper. [beat]
Not sneaking — that was not the right word for it. More like being very honest about where she was and how she moved, and the forest choosing not to mention it. Her soft paws read the snow as she went: fresh fall here, crusted ice there, the slight depression where a hare had sat sometime in the night. The snowy whisper of everything that had happened since the last fall.
Tonight she was not hunting. Tonight she was just moving. [softly]
She moved from the deep forest to the edge of the meadow, where the snow lay unbroken and smooth — a white field under a white sky — and sat at the tree line and looked out at it. In the blue pre-dawn light, the snow was luminous. It seemed to generate its own quiet glow. [with growing realization]
Luna took one step into the meadow. Her paw sank slightly, silently. She looked back at the print: soft-edged, round, the mark of her. She took another step. Another print. [pacing]
She walked in a long slow circle across the unmarked meadow, and behind her she left a ring of prints in the snow — a conversation between herself and the world, written in the only language that mattered here: presence, gentleness, the simple fact of having passed through.
[softly] At the end of the circle she stopped and looked at what she had made. A ring of soft paw prints, perfect in the blue light, already filling slowly with the gentlest of new snowfall.
Gone by morning. But real now. Completely, quietly real.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
