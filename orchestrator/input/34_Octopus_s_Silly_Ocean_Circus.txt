Once upon a time, in the deep blue waters of a warm and wonderful ocean, there lived a silly octopus named Otto. [long pause]
Otto had eight arms — which he already knew, having counted them many times — and each arm had a mind of its own. Arm One liked to wave. Arm Two liked to tap things. Arm Three was always reaching for snacks. Arms Four through Eight had various opinions that Otto was still getting to know. [sighs]
What Otto wanted more than anything was to start a circus. An ocean circus — the greatest show beneath the waves. [beat]
He spent a week making tickets out of pressed seaweed and distributed them to every creature in the reef. He built a stage from flat coral. He strung strings of bioluminescent jellyfish along the top as lights, which the jellyfish agreed to, though they found the whole arrangement somewhat undignified.
On the evening of the show, the ocean floor filled with audience. Crabs in the front row. Fish in the middle. A whale in the back who had to watch sideways to fit. [pacing]
Otto took the stage. [abruptly] Things did not go entirely as planned.
Arm Three, reaching for a snack mid-performance, knocked over the first prop. Arm One, compensating with a dramatic wave, accidentally slapped the lead crab on the head. Arms Four and Five got tangled during the grand finale spin, causing Otto to roll sideways off the stage entirely. [with growing realization]
There was a silence.
Then — the whale laughed. A sound so enormous it sent bubbles streaming to the surface. And once the whale started, everyone laughed, because that is what always happens when the whale starts.
Otto untangled himself from the coral at the bottom of the stage and looked out at his laughing, delighted audience. [softly]
He began to laugh too.
He had planned a circus. He had accidentally invented something better: the funniest show in the ocean. He took a bow with all eight arms — in eight different directions — and the crowd went wild.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
