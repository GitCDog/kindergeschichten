Once upon a time, on a rocky cliff above a cold grey sea, there lived a playful puffin named Percy. [long pause]
Percy had an orange beak so bright it looked painted on, a tuxedo of black and white feathers, and an expression of permanent mild astonishment that was simply how puffin faces are arranged and had nothing to do with how Percy actually felt — though Percy did, in fact, often feel mildly astonished. The world was full of astonishing things. [sighs with cheerful wonder]
His cliff nest was on the highest ledge — the best one, in Percy's opinion, because from there you could see the entire bay and, on clear days, another cliff far to the north where more puffins lived. He waved to them sometimes. They probably couldn't see him. He waved anyway. [beat]
But the fish adventure came first every morning.
At dawn, Percy would launch himself off the cliff nest — a moment that required commitment, because puffins are better at flying through water than through air, and the first few wing-beats always felt somewhat optimistic — and dive. [softly]
Into the cold grey sea. Down through the green-dark water. Wings folded to his sides, using them as flippers now, in the element where he was built to be. [pacing]
The ocean fish were quick. They scattered silver in every direction when he dove among them. Percy had learned, over many seasons, not to chase — but to predict. To read where the school was heading and position himself there ahead of it, beak ready, patient as a fisherman.
[with growing realization] Today he caught seven in one dive. Seven — a personal record. He surfaced into the morning air with a beak so full of fish they stuck out in a neat, improbable row.
He couldn't close his beak. He couldn't make any sound. He sat on the water for a moment, fish bristling from his bright orange beak, the cold sea lifting him gently. [beat]
He thought: I should be embarrassed. Instead he felt only triumphant.
[softly] He flew back to the cliff nest — somewhat lopsided, aerodynamically — and deposited seven fish with the pride of someone who has entirely exceeded their own expectations.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
