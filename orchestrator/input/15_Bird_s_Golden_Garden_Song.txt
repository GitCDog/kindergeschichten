Once upon a time, in an enchanted garden at the center of an ancient kingdom, there lived a singing bird named Sol. [long pause]
Sol had feathers the color of sunlight — bright gold with tips of amber — and a voice so pure and clear that flowers turned toward him when he sang, the way they turn toward the sun. Every morning he sat at the top of his golden cage and filled the garden with music. [sighs] The cage door was always open. Sol could leave whenever he liked. He just never had.
The enchanted garden was safe. It was beautiful. It was home. [beat]
But one evening, as the sun went down and the first stars appeared, Sol heard something he had never heard before. From beyond the garden wall came another voice — high and wavering and small. A bird, singing alone in the dark.
Sol tilted his head. He had never thought about what lay beyond the garden wall. [softly]
He stepped out of his golden cage. He hopped to the branch nearest the wall. He looked over.
On the other side was a little garden — scraggly and wild and wonderful — and in the middle of it sat a small brown sparrow, singing to herself in the darkness. She sang quietly, as if she wasn't sure anyone would want to listen.
"Your song is beautiful," Sol called down.
The sparrow startled. She looked up. [with growing realization] "Oh," she said. "I didn't think anyone could hear."
"I heard it from inside the enchanted garden," Sol said. "It reached me all the way through the wall."
The sparrow blinked. "You live in the enchanted garden? With the golden cage and the flowers?"
"Yes," said Sol. "Would you like to come and sing there with me tomorrow?"
[long pause] The sparrow thought about it. Then she said, very carefully, "Only if you'll come and sing here with me tonight."
Sol looked at the wild little garden beyond the wall. At the scraggly hedges and the wonky paving stones and the big dark sky above.
[pacing] He spread his golden wings. And he flew over.
They sang together until midnight, their two voices weaving together in the dark like threads of gold and brown. The stars listened. The flowers on both sides of the wall turned toward the sound.
And Sol discovered that the most beautiful songs are always sung in two parts.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
