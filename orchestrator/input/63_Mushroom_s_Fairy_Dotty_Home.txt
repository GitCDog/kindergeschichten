Once upon a time, in a damp and mossy corner of an ancient forest, there grew a magic mushroom named Mop. [long pause]
Mop was round and red with a cap covered in perfect white dots — each one exactly the right size, exactly the right distance from the next. He had grown this way since he was a spore, and he was, without any vanity about it, rather pleased with his dots. They were distinctive. [sighs with quiet satisfaction]
What Mop had not expected was a tenant. [beat]
She arrived one rainy Tuesday — a forest fairy named Pip, no taller than a thimble, with dragonfly wings and a determined expression. She stood at the base of Mop's stem, looked up at his dotty house, and said: "This will do nicely."
"I beg your pardon?" said Mop.
"Your cap," said Pip. "It's exactly the right shape. I need a home. The hawthorn I was living in has been taken over by a family of wrens." She said this without rancor — wrens needed homes too — but with the clarity of someone who had already made up her mind.
[with growing realization] Mop thought about this. "You want to live on my cap?"
"Under the edge of it," Pip said, pulling out a very small measuring string. "South-facing, good overhang, the dots will help with navigation. I'll be no trouble."
She was, in fact, some trouble. [softly]
She rearranged two of his white dots to better mark her door, which Mop objected to until he saw the result, which was actually rather elegant. She hung tiny lanterns under the cap's edge that glowed at night like a string of miniature moons. She planted a garden of miniature clover at his base, which attracted the most interesting insects. [pacing]
By the end of the first week, Mop had stopped thinking of himself as a mushroom with a problem and started thinking of himself as a dotty house with a very interesting resident.
[softly] By the end of the month, he wasn't sure what he had done before Pip arrived. He thought the forest had probably been quieter. And certainly less well-lit.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
