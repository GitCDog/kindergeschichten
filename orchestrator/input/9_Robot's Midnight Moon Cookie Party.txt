Once upon a time, at the very top of a gentle hill, there lived a friendly robot named Bolt. [long pause] Bolt's body was painted sky blue, his eyes glowed a warm golden yellow, and when he walked, he made a soft whirring sound that the birds on the hill had come to find quite comforting. He loved three things most of all: his friends, his baking, and the starry night sky. [beat] One clear evening, as Bolt stood on his doorstep looking up at the stars, he had an idea. A very good one. He would bake moon cookies — round, silver-dusted biscuits shaped just like the crescent moon — and throw a party right there under the stars for all his friends. [with growing excitement] He got to work immediately. He mixed and stirred and rolled the dough into perfect crescent shapes. He dusted each one with silver sugar that caught the light and sparkled like tiny fallen stars. The smell that came from his little oven was something between warm butter and a summer night. [softly] While the moon cookies baked, Bolt strung little paper lanterns between the trees in his garden and laid out small blankets on the grass. Then he sent invitations — a tiny folded note under every nearby door. [pacing] At midnight, they arrived: a round-eyed owl who came first, three fireflies who arrived all at once in a little glowing cluster, a hedgehog in a pointy party hat, and a friendly raccoon who brought a jar of lemonade and wore a bow tie for the occasion. They arranged themselves on the blankets. The starry night sky stretched above them, enormous and quiet and full of light. Bolt passed the moon cookies around — still warm from the oven. "That one looks like a spoon," said the owl, pointing at a cluster of stars. "That one looks like a boot," said the hedgehog. [abruptly] "That one," said Bolt, pointing carefully at a bright, curved constellation, "looks exactly like a cookie." Everyone laughed. The raccoon nearly spilled the lemonade. The fireflies blinked on and off with amusement. [beat] When the last moon cookie was gone and the stars had begun their slow drift toward morning, everyone agreed: midnight on a clear night with good friends was the finest kind of evening there was. "Same time next month?" Bolt asked, his golden eyes hopeful. "Same time next month," they all said together. The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
