Once upon a time, high in the canopy of the deepest, greenest jungle, there lived a cheerful monkey named Mango. [long pause]
Mango had long arms perfect for swinging, bright eyes perfect for spotting ripe fruit, and a grin that started somewhere around his ears and didn't stop. He spent his days swinging from vine to vine on the jungle swing — a great looping arc of braided vine that had been there so long the trees had grown around it — singing to himself and being, by most accounts, absolutely delightful. [sighs happily]
But Mango had one dream above all others: to throw the greatest banana feast the jungle had ever seen. [beat]
He started planning on a Monday. He swung from tree to tree, personally inviting every creature he met. The parrot. The three-toed sloth. The family of toucans who lived near the waterfall. The shy tapir who always said no to things and then showed up anyway. [pacing]
He spent the rest of the week collecting bananas — from the high branches, from the low ones, from the secret grove by the river where the bananas grew curved and sweet and golden as afternoon light. He piled them in a clearing and decorated the jungle swing with flowers.
On Saturday, everyone came. [with growing realization]
Even the shy tapir.
Mango had prepared: plain bananas, fried bananas, bananas wrapped in leaves, banana smoothies in coconut shells, and — his greatest achievement — a banana cake balanced on a broad flat leaf, with a little monkey-shaped piece of fruit on top.
The feast was magnificent. The parrot told stories. The toucans showed off. The sloth ate four pieces of cake with great slow contentment. The tapir, it turned out, was extremely funny once you got her talking. [softly]
Mango swung on the jungle swing above it all, looking down at his friends and his jungle and his banana cake, and felt something so large and warm inside his chest that he had no word for it.
He decided the word was probably: enough.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
