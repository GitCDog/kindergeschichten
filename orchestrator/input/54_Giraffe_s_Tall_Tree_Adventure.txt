Once upon a time, on a wide savanna where the tall trees rose above everything else and the sky seemed closer than anywhere, there lived a gentle giraffe named Grace. [long pause]
Grace was very tall. Taller than all the other giraffes. Taller, in fact, than anything on the savanna except the trees themselves, and even then it was a close thing. She had learned, over the years, to be careful about doorways — not that the savanna had many — and about where she stood during thunderstorms. She was the first to feel the rain and the last to find shelter, and she had made her peace with this. [sighs]
What Grace loved most was the leaf snacks from the very tops of the tall trees. [beat]
Not the accessible leaves — the ones every other giraffe could reach. The highest ones. The ones that caught the most sun and were therefore the sweetest, the most tender, still warm from the afternoon light. You needed to be very tall to reach them. Grace was very tall. [softly] It was, she felt, a fine arrangement.
One morning, she arrived at her favorite acacia tree to find a young giraffe standing at the base of it, neck stretched to its absolute limit, falling considerably short of even the lowest good leaves.
"May I help?" Grace asked.
The young giraffe looked up at her. "Are you going to say I'll grow into it?"
"I was going to say," Grace said, "that I know where the best leaf snacks are. Would you like to come?"
[with growing realization] She led the young giraffe to a tree she had found three seasons ago — lower-branched than the others, but with leaves of extraordinary quality, sun-warmed and sweet. Reachable, just, if you were a young giraffe with a properly extended neck and a good stretch.
[pacing] The young giraffe stretched. And reached. And got there.
The expression that crossed that young face on tasting the first leaf made Grace feel something warm and large in her long chest.
[softly] Every height, she thought, has its right tree. You just need someone who knows where to look.
They ate together in the afternoon light, the tall trees swaying gently above them, the savanna wide and golden all around.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
