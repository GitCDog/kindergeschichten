Once upon a time, in a damp green clearing in the heart of a very old wood, there grew a tiny toadstool named Tom. [long pause]
Tom was red-capped and white-dotted and stood exactly as tall as a robin's knee, which he considered a perfectly dignified height. He had grown up in the clearing alongside eleven other toadstools, all of them arranged in a perfect circle — a fairy ring — which the wood's creatures gave a wide berth, for reasons that varied depending on who you asked. [sighs]
Tom knew the real reason. He lived there, after all. [beat]
The fairy ring was a mushroom house — his house — and it was the most remarkable address in the entire wood. Because on certain nights, when the moon was full and the dew was heavy and the wood was absolutely still, the ring came alive.
It happened slowly. First a warmth in the ground beneath the toadstools — not heat, but a kind of hum, like a note played very far away. Then a faint light around the base of each toadstool, greenish and soft. Then, if you were paying attention — if you were very still and very quiet and genuinely curious — the ring began to shine. [softly]
Tom had watched it happen his whole life. He knew it better than anything. [with growing realization]
What he hadn't known, until this particular full moon, was that he could be part of it.
He had always considered himself an observer. A toadstool who watched the fairy ring from the inside, which was unusual but still — watching.
But this night, as the hum began and the light rose around his roots, Tom felt it move through him too. Up through his stem, into his cap, out through the tips of his white dots. His own small, particular light. [pacing]
He glowed. Not much. But enough.
And the ring, complete now with every toadstool giving its share, shone brighter than it ever had — a perfect circle of warm light in the dark wood, a mushroom house lit from within, a fairy ring home that held twelve small lights and knew what to do with all of them.
[softly] Tom glowed on until dawn, and the wood was a little less dark for it.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
