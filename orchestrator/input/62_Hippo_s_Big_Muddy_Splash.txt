Once upon a time, in a wide muddy river that wound through the heart of a hot and golden savanna, there lived a little hippo named Hugo. [long pause]
Hugo was not the smallest hippo in the river — there was one slightly smaller named Dotty — but he was the youngest who was allowed to splash. Splashing, in the river, was serious business. Done wrong, it disturbed the fish, annoyed the crocodiles, and sent water up the banks in ways that the older hippos considered inconsiderate. [sighs]
Done right, however, splashing fun was the finest thing in the world. [beat]
Hugo had been studying technique all summer. He had watched the way the elder hippos moved — how they let their whole body drop into the muddy river at once, creating a single tremendous wave rather than a series of messy little ones. He had practiced in the shallows, where the splash was smaller and the consequences less significant. He had developed, he believed, a theory.
Today he was going to test it. [softly]
He waded to the deep center of the muddy river, where the brown water came up to his chin and the current ran cool against his sides. The older hippos watched from the banks with the air of creatures prepared to be either impressed or disappointed.
Hugo took a breath. He lowered himself very slowly — nose, shoulders, back — until only his eyes were above water. [with growing realization] He felt the river all around him. The pull of it. The weight of it.
And then he jumped. [abruptly]
Upward and outward with every ounce of his considerable self, leaving the water entirely for one extraordinary moment — and landing back with a BOOM that sent a wave rolling both ways down the river, lapping at both banks, rocking the reeds, startling a colony of egrets into white flight. [pacing]
The muddy river settled. Rings spread to every bank.
Hugo surfaced, blinking mud from his eyes.
[softly] The elder hippos were quiet. Then the oldest one — a great grey mountain of a hippo named Bernard — raised himself slightly from the water and clapped his enormous jaws together three times. In the river, this was the highest possible praise.
Hugo sank back into the muddy river up to his happy ears.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
