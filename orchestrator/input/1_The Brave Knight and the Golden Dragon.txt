Once upon a time [long pause], in a faraway kingdom filled with rolling green hills, colorful flowers, and a sparkling river that sang soft songs, there lived a brave young knight named Sir Elias. [softly] Sir Elias was known throughout the land for his kind heart and his special sword.
The sword was not like ordinary swords. It was made of shining silver with a handle decorated with sparkling gems. [with growing realization] Most importantly, it glowed with a warm, golden light whenever courage and kindness were needed. [beat]
One bright and sunny morning [sighs], the peaceful village was filled with worried voices. Villagers rushed to the castle gates. “A golden dragon has been seen near the dark forest!” they shouted. “What if it’s scary? What if it breathes fire?”
The king looked concerned. He turned to Sir Elias [checks watch]. “Brave knight, will you go and see what this dragon wants? We must protect our home.”
Sir Elias smiled bravely. [deadpan] “Of course, Your Majesty. A knight’s duty is to help those in need… even if they have scales and wings.” He picked up his glowing sword, put on his shiny armor, and set off on his loyal horse toward the forest. [pacing]
As he rode through the meadows [softly], the wind whispered through the trees and birds sang cheerful tunes. But as he got closer to the forest, the trees grew taller and the light became dappled. [long pause] Suddenly [abruptly], he heard a deep rumbling sound coming from a clearing by the lake.
There sat the golden dragon! Its scales glittered like a thousand treasures in the sunlight. But the dragon didn’t look fierce. It looked lonely and a bit sad, with its head resting on its paws.
Sir Elias got off his horse slowly. He held his sword high, and it began to glow softly. “Hello there, great dragon,” he said [softly]. “I am Sir Elias. I come not to fight, but to talk.”
The dragon lifted its large head and blinked its big, kind eyes. “My name is Goldie,” the dragon replied [sarcastically]. “I didn’t mean to scare anyone. I flew here from my home in the misty mountains because I lost my special shining stone. It helps me feel calm and sleep peacefully. Without it, I’m so tired.” [sighs]
[with growing realization] Sir Elias lowered his sword. “A shining stone? My sword glows when something important is near. Maybe we can find it together!”
Goldie smiled a toothy but friendly smile. “You would help me? Even though I’m a dragon?”
“Of course!” said the knight. [rolls eyes] “Bravery is about friendship too.”
Together, the brave knight and the golden dragon searched the forest. They looked under large rocks, behind ancient oak trees, and near the whispering river. The sword glowed brighter and brighter as they got closer.
[beat] “Look! It’s glowing so strongly now,” Sir Elias said with excitement.
They came to a hollow log hidden behind some bushes. [abruptly] There it was—the beautiful shining stone, sparkling just like Goldie’s scales!
Goldie picked it up gently with one claw. “Thank you, Sir Elias! You are the bravest and kindest knight I’ve ever met.” The dragon hugged the stone close and looked much happier.
To thank his new friend, Goldie offered to fly Sir Elias back to the village. As they soared high above the trees [long pause], the wind rushed past them and the kingdom looked like a beautiful painting below. The villagers cheered when they saw the knight and the dragon waving down at them.
From that day forward, Goldie became a friend to the entire kingdom. He helped water the gardens with his gentle breath and told the children wonderful bedtime stories about the misty mountains. Sir Elias and his glowing sword were always ready for new adventures with his friend.
And so [softly], the brave knight and the golden dragon taught everyone that sometimes the best treasures are new friendships. [long pause] Sweet dreams, little one. The end.
