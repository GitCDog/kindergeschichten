Once upon a time, in a forest where the autumn light fell golden through the leaves and the air smelled of pine and earth and something sweetly warm, there lived a sleepy bear named Bruno. [long pause]
Bruno was the sleepiest bear in the whole forest — and that, in a forest full of bears, was saying something. He yawned through breakfast. He dozed through lunch. He napped twice between dinner and bedtime. But before his long winter sleep began, there was one thing Bruno needed. [sighs] Honey.
Not just any honey. The honey from the old linden tree at the top of Clover Hill — dark amber honey, thick as syrup, that tasted of every summer flower at once. Bruno had dreamed about it all year. [beat]
The climb to the honey tree was long. The path wound upward through the dreamy meadow — a wide, soft sweep of grass and wildflowers that always seemed to shimmer slightly in the afternoon light, as if it was slightly more beautiful than a meadow had any right to be. Bruno stopped in the middle of it and lay down. [softly] Just for a moment.
He was asleep within seconds.
He dreamed of warm days and cool streams and the particular sound the wind made through the tops of very tall trees. [beat] When he woke, the light was golden-pink — late afternoon — and a small bee was sitting on his nose, looking at him calmly.
"You're going the right direction," said the bee. "The honey tree is just at the top."
Bruno blinked slowly. "How did you know where I was going?"
[with growing realization] "We always know," said the bee, and flew up the hill.
Bruno followed.
The honey was everything he had remembered. Dark and warm and sweet, thick on his tongue, tasting of summer and patience and the particular satisfaction of having earned something. [pacing] He ate until he was full, licked his paws clean, and sat at the top of Clover Hill as the sun went down, looking out over the dreamy meadow below.
Then he ambled home to his den, curled up in his favorite corner, pulled his paws around his nose — and slept the most wonderful sleep of his entire life.
Some journeys, he dreamed, are worth every slow, sleepy step.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
