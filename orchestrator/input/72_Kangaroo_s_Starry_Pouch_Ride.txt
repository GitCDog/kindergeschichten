Once upon a time, on the wide red plains of Australia where the sunsets turned everything pink and gold and the nights were full of stars, there lived a kind kangaroo named Kira. [long pause]
Kira had strong back legs, a long tail for balance, and a pouch that was, at this particular moment, occupied. Her joey — a tiny, bright-eyed baby named Bo — had been living in that pouch for four months, and was at the stage where he wanted to look at everything, all the time, and never miss a single thing. [sighs with fond exhaustion]
Tonight, Bo wanted to see the stars. [beat]
"Mama," said Bo, his small head poking out of the pouch, nose twitching. "I want to see all of them."
Kira looked up at the sky. It was a very clear night — the kind that Australia does particularly well — and the stars were so thick they seemed to lean down. "That's a lot of stars," she said.
"I know," said Bo. "That's why I want to see them."
Kira smiled. [softly] She hopped.
Not a running hop — a slow, deliberate, looking hop. Each time she landed, she paused for a moment, and Bo could look at one part of the sky properly before they moved to the next. The Southern Cross. The Milky Way — a smear of light so dense it looked like spilled cream. The bright eye of Jupiter hanging low on the horizon.
[with growing realization] "What's that one?" Bo asked, pointing a tiny paw at a cluster of blue-white stars.
"That's the Pleiades," said Kira. "Seven sisters. Our people have known their names for a very long time."
"Did they look at them from a pouch too?"
[pacing] Kira laughed — a soft, warm sound that carried across the plains. "Perhaps the very first time," she said.
Bo turned this over in his small mind. He looked at the seven sisters. He thought about all the joeys, across all the long years, who had looked at the same stars from the same safe place. [softly]
He tucked himself a little deeper into the pouch. The stars moved slowly overhead on their ancient paths.
He was asleep before Kira had taken ten more steps.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
