Once upon a time, at the very top of the tallest beanstalk in the world, there lived a jolly giant named Gus. [long pause]
Gus was enormous — so enormous that when he sneezed, all the clouds shifted to the left and the weathermen below had to start over. His feet were the size of rowing boats. His laugh shook the beanstalk from top to bottom. But Gus had a problem. [sighs] He was desperately, terribly ticklish.
This made life in the beanstalk village very complicated.
The beanstalk village was a cheerful little town built into the stalk itself — tiny wooden houses with window boxes, a bakery that smelled of gingerbread, and a school where the children learned to climb. Everyone there was very fond of Gus. But they had learned to be careful. [beat]
If you bumped his elbow — BOOM. Gus would collapse into giggles and the whole stalk would sway like a ship in a storm.
If a butterfly landed on his nose — BOOM. Down would come Gus, laughing so hard his eyes watered, and every pot of geraniums on the high street would topple sideways. [with growing realization]
"The ticklish clouds are the worst," Gus told the mayor apologetically, after a particularly fluffy cloud had drifted into his armpit and sent him rolling sideways across the sky.
"What if," said the mayor thoughtfully, "instead of trying to avoid the tickling — we made it part of the show?"
[long pause] Gus blinked his enormous eyes.
That weekend, the beanstalk village held its very first Tickle Festival. Gus lay on his back in the sky — carefully, so as not to squash anything — and let the ticklish clouds drift wherever they liked. Every time he laughed, the stalk shook, and all the villagers held on and laughed too, bouncing up and down like the best carnival ride anyone had ever been on. [pacing]
Children shrieked with delight. The baker dropped his hat and never even noticed. The mayor laughed so hard she hiccupped for an hour.
[softly] Gus laughed most of all.
Being different, he decided — even gloriously, inconveniently, enormously ticklish — could be the greatest gift of all, if you found the right people to share it with.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
