Once upon a time, in a forest so old that the trees had names and the paths remembered every creature that had ever walked them, there lived a gentle deer named Dara. [long pause]
Dara moved through the forest quietly — which is how deer prefer to do most things. Her hooves barely made a sound on the soft earth. Her great brown eyes took everything in without rushing. She had lived in this forest her whole life and loved every inch of it: every mossy hollow, every shaft of morning light, every place where two branches crossed and made a frame for the sky. [sighs softly]
But her favorite thing of all was the ancient oak at the very center of the forest. It was the oldest tree anyone knew — wider around than ten deer standing nose to tail, its bark thick and grooved as a map of everywhere. The whispering wind moved differently in its branches than anywhere else in the forest. [beat]
Other creatures walked past it every day. Few of them stopped.
Dara stopped every time.
She would press her soft nose against the bark and listen. [softly] The wind through the ancient oak made a sound unlike any other — not quite music, not quite words, but something in between. Something that felt, if you were very still, like being told: you are exactly where you are meant to be.
One autumn evening, a young fawn from the northern part of the forest found Dara standing there, eyes closed, nose to bark.
"What are you doing?" the fawn asked.
"Listening," said Dara.
[with growing realization] "To what?"
Dara was quiet for a moment. "To the forest," she said finally. "It has things to say, if you're patient enough to hear them."
The fawn stood beside her. [long pause] She pressed her own small nose against the ancient bark.
The whispering wind moved through the oak above them. The last leaves of autumn drifted down.
[pacing] After a while, the fawn let out a long, slow breath — the kind you let out when something tight inside you finally loosens.
"Oh," she said quietly. "I hear it."
Dara smiled. The greatest gifts, she had always known, are not things you keep. They are things you pass on.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
