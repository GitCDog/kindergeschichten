Once upon a time, in a meadow at the edge of a quiet wood, there lived a brave butterfly named Belle. [long pause]
Belle had wings of deep amber and velvet black — broad and strong, with a single spot of blue on each hindwing, like two small windows onto a summer sky. She flew well. She flew far. She was not afraid of wind or rain or long distances. [sighs]
But Belle carried something that not many creatures knew about. [beat]
She remembered being a caterpillar.
Not as an embarrassment — she had been an excellent caterpillar, thorough and determined and very good at finding the right leaves. But she remembered what the chrysalis had felt like from the inside. The darkness. The not-knowing. The complete inability to see what was happening or whether anything good would come of it at all. [softly]
That had taken more courage than any flight she had ever made.
She met another caterpillar on a warm afternoon — small, green, clinging to the underside of a blackthorn leaf with an expression of concentrated worry. [with growing realization]
"I know what you're thinking," Belle said, landing gently nearby.
The caterpillar looked up. "You can't," he said. "You're a butterfly. You've never —"
"I was a caterpillar for two months," Belle said. "I built my own chrysalis, right here on this blackthorn, three summers ago."
The caterpillar stared. He looked at her wings. Her wings of light, amber and black and blue. [pacing]
"Was it frightening?" he asked. "Going in?"
"Yes," said Belle, without hesitation. "It was completely dark. I couldn't see anything. I didn't know if I was doing it right. I just had to trust that what was happening inside me knew what it was doing, even when I couldn't feel it." [beat]
The caterpillar was quiet for a moment. "And then?"
[softly] Belle spread her wings slowly — the full amber span of them, the blue spots catching the light. "And then," she said.
The caterpillar looked at her wings for a long time.
Then he turned back to his leaf and began to eat with new purpose.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
