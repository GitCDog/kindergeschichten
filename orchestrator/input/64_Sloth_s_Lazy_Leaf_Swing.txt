Once upon a time, in a rainforest so green and layered and alive that it seemed to breathe, there lived a sweet sloth named Sable. [long pause]
Sable moved through life at the speed of deep contentment. She hung from her branch with three-toed ease, her fur the gray-green of old moss, her face wearing the particular expression that all sloths wear — not sad, not sleepy exactly, but utterly, profoundly at peace with the pace of things. [sighs very, very slowly]
Her slow swing was her favorite place in all the world. [beat]
It was a vine — thick and curved — that hung between two cecropia trees at just the right angle, long enough that when the wind came through the canopy it would move, very slightly, back and forth. Sable had found it three years ago and hadn't moved far from it since. She ate the cecropia leaves within arm's reach. She slept there. She watched the rainforest do everything the rainforest did, from her slow swing, at her own pace.
Other creatures found this puzzling. [softly]
"Don't you want to go somewhere?" asked a howler monkey, swinging past at speed.
"I am somewhere," said Sable.
"Don't you get bored?" asked a toucan.
[with growing realization] Sable considered this. It took a moment. "Bored," she said finally, "is what happens when you don't notice what's already here."
She looked at the light on the leaves. She looked at the particular way a raindrop traveled down a cecropia leaf to its tip and hesitated there before letting go. She looked at the patch of sky between the canopy and the tree fern below, through which, if you were patient, you could sometimes see a harpy eagle in the distance, unhurried as a cloud. [pacing]
The howler monkey was already gone. The toucan had found something more immediately interesting.
[softly] Sable hung on her slow swing and watched the leaf hammock of the rainforest around her — the layered green world, still and busy at once — and felt, as she always felt, that she was exactly where everything was happening.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
