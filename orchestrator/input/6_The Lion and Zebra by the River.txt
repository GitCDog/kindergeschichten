Once upon a time, by the wide and glittering river that wound through the heart of the savanna, a lion named Kofi sat alone on a flat rock. [long pause] His golden mane drooped. His great paws were folded in front of him. His tail lay still. He was the kind of sad that sits very quietly inside you and doesn't make a sound. [beat] A zebra named Zuri came to the river to drink. She spotted Kofi on his rock and paused — because lions and zebras did not, as a rule, stop to chat. But Zuri had a kind heart, and she could see that the lion was hurting. [softly] She walked a little closer. "Why do you look so sad?" she asked carefully. Kofi sighed — a long, low sound like wind across the grass. [sadly] "I lost my ball in the river. My cub gave it to me before he left on a long journey, and the river took it away. It's gone." Zuri looked at the river. It was wide and it moved fast — but there, caught between two stones near the far bank, something bobbed. Something small and orange. [with growing realization] "I can get it," said Zuri. Before Kofi could say a word, she waded in. Zebras, it turned out, were excellent swimmers. She crossed the current steadily, reached the two stones, and nuzzled the little ball free with her nose. Then she carried it back and dropped it gently at Kofi's enormous paws. [beat] Kofi looked at it for a long moment. His eyes, when he looked up, were full of something bright. "Thank you," he said quietly. "I didn't know you could be so brave." [softly] "And I didn't know lions could love something so small," Zuri said. They both laughed then — a deep rumbling lion laugh and a bright, musical zebra whinny — and the sound rose up from the flat rock and drifted out across the river like a song. [beat] After that day, the animals of the savanna grew used to an unusual sight: a lion and a zebra sitting side by side on the flat rock by the river, watching the water go by, their shadows long and peaceful in the evening light. Some friendships begin in the strangest of places. And some of those are the very best ones of all. The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
