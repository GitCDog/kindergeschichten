Once upon a time, on a rocky mountainside where the wind was always blowing and the clouds came and went like slow thoughts, there lived a brave baby dragon named Spark. [long pause]
Spark was four months old, which in dragon years was very young indeed. His scales were still coming in properly — more iridescent than his parents' deep green, catching the light in the shifting way of new things. His wings were larger than his body thought they should be, and he tripped over them occasionally when walking on the ground, which he found embarrassing. [sighs]
Today was the day of his first flight. [beat]
His mother and father stood on the ledge above him, watching. They had done this with their other offspring — stood on the ledge, watched, been ready — and they had learned that being ready was all a parent could do. The flying part a dragon had to work out alone.
Spark stood at the edge of the rocky outcrop and looked down. The valley fell away below him, a long green drop to the forest floor. The clouds were right at his level — not above him as they usually were, but there, within reach, drifting past the mountain's face in slow gray-white masses. [softly]
His cloud castle. That's what he had called them to himself — those clouds at mountain height that he had watched from below all his life, thinking: one day I will be up there.
He spread his wings. They caught the updraft immediately — a strong, steady push from below — and he felt what he had felt in his dreams but never woken life: the potential in them. The readiness. [with growing realization]
He stepped off the ledge.
[long pause]
He fell for exactly two seconds before his wings understood what to do. [abruptly]
And then he wasn't falling anymore.
He was inside the cloud. Cool and white and vast around him, no ground, no mountain, nothing but soft rushing gray-white and the wind in his face and the extraordinary feeling of being held up by something invisible and entirely trustworthy. [pacing]
He flew through the cloud and out the other side into clear blue sky. The mountain was behind him. The valley was below. The horizon went on forever.
[softly] Spark hovered there for one long, astonished moment. Then he turned and flew back — for now, just for now — because his parents were waiting.
But he had been to the cloud castle. And he was never going to be afraid of the ledge again.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
