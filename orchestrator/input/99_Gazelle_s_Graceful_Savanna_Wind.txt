Once upon a time, on a savanna so wide and golden that standing at its edge felt like standing at the beginning of the world, there lived a gentle gazelle named Grace. [long pause]
Grace was slender and precise, with horns that curved like questions and eyes the warm brown of still water. She moved the way the savanna wind moved — fluidly, without apparent effort, covering ground in a way that looked less like running and more like being carried by something invisible. [sighs with a peace that comes from knowing exactly who you are]
She ran every morning. Not from anything. Not toward anything in particular. Just because running was what her body was made for, and she had decided long ago that ignoring what you were made for was a waste of something important. [beat]
The savanna wind was her companion.
It came from the south in the early morning, low and cool and smelling of distant rain. Grace would feel it against her face as she ran — first against her, then beside her as she built speed, then behind her as she really opened up — and at that last stage, when the wind was behind her and her hooves barely seemed to touch the ground, something extraordinary happened. [softly]
She and the wind became the same thing.
Not metaphorically. Physically. The same speed, the same direction, the same long forward lean. The savanna around her became a blur of gold and green. Her graceful run was no longer something she was doing — it was something she was. [with growing realization]
Other gazelles sometimes ran with her, for a while. But they would slow, and she would keep going, and that was all right. Some things you do alongside others, and some things you do in the particular company of the wind and the wide empty world.
[pacing] She ran the full length of the savanna that morning — from the acacia line in the east to the kopjes in the west — and turned, and ran back.
When she stopped, finally, she stood in the morning sun with her sides rising and falling and the savanna wind cooling her.
[softly] She thought about nothing except this: the ground, the air, the run, the wind. The perfect completeness of a morning spent being exactly, entirely yourself.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
