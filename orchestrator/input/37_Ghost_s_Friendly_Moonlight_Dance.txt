Once upon a time, in the attic of a tall and crooked house at the end of a lane, there lived a friendly ghost named Gilbert. [long pause]
Gilbert had been in the attic for a very long time — long enough that he had read every book in the old trunk three times, organized all the buttons in the biscuit tin by color, and become genuinely fond of the spiders who kept him company in the rafters. He was not a frightening ghost. He was, if anything, slightly too cheerful for a haunted attic. [sighs contentedly]
But Gilbert had one great wish: to dance. [beat]
He had watched from the attic window as the moonlight danced on the garden below — how it moved across the old flagstones, how it turned the birdbath to silver, how it made the whole garden look like a ballroom waiting for someone to use it. [softly]
Gilbert floated down through the ceiling on the first clear night of autumn.
He drifted into the garden. The moonlight fell on him and through him — because that is the nature of ghosts — making shapes on the flagstones that shifted as he moved. [with growing realization] And to his astonishment, the shadows he made were beautiful. Shapes that no solid person could make. Shapes that swirled and billowed and caught the light in ways that were entirely, wonderfully his own.
He began to dance.
He danced among the flower beds. He swirled around the old apple tree. He waltzed the full length of the garden path and back. [pacing] The moonlight moved with him, obedient as a partner, throwing his ghost-light everywhere.
The hedgehog who lived under the hedge came out and watched. Then the fox peered over the wall. Then an owl landed on the fence post, large-eyed and attentive.
Gilbert danced until the moon began to set, and his audience watched in complete and comfortable silence — the kind of silence that is actually full of something.
[softly] As the first gray of morning came, Gilbert floated back up through the ceiling to his attic.
He had discovered something important: you don't need to be solid to make something beautiful. You just need to show up and move.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
