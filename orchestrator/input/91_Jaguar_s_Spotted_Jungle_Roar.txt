Once upon a time, deep in a jungle so dense and ancient that the sunlight had to work hard to reach the ground, there lived a jolly jaguar named Jasper. [long pause]
Jasper had a magnificent coat — deep gold with black rosette spots, each one slightly different from the next, like a map of somewhere complicated and interesting. He was large, and fast, and could climb trees that most cats wouldn't attempt. He was also, for a jaguar, extremely sociable. [sighs with the ease of a creature comfortable in its own skin]
He loved a good roar party. [beat]
A roar party was simple: you gathered in the clearing at the jungle's heart, and you roared. No agenda. No competition. Just the collective, magnificent sound of large animals doing what large animals do best, echoing off the trees and up through the canopy and out over the spotted jungle into the open air above.
Jasper had started the tradition three years ago and had not regretted it once.
The invitations went out on Monday — scratches on the bark of certain trees that every jungle creature knew how to read. By Thursday evening the clearing was full. [softly]
Two jaguars. A puma who said she was just passing through and then stayed for the whole thing. Four howler monkeys who needed no encouragement whatsoever. A tapir who had an unexpectedly resonant voice and was always surprised by this. A harpy eagle who didn't roar but contributed a scream of such quality that everyone agreed it counted. [pacing]
They roared together as the sun went down. The spotted jungle absorbed the sound and sent it back. The trees shook slightly — not from fear, but from something more like recognition.
[with growing realization] Jasper stood in the middle of it all, his spotted coat catching the last of the light, his own great roar rolling out from his chest like something that had been waiting there all week.
This, he thought — this exact sound, this exact moment, these exact friends in this exact clearing — this is what the jungle is for. [softly]
The roar party went on until the stars came out. No one wanted to be the first to stop.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
