Once upon a time, in a meadow where the clover grew thick and the rabbit holes went deep and warm into the earth, there lived a brave bunny named Bramble. [long pause]
Bramble had grey fur, a white tail, and ears that were independently mobile — one could go left while the other pointed forward, which was very useful for listening in two directions at once. She was considered the boldest bunny in the warren, which was either a great compliment or a mild warning, depending on who you asked. [sighs]
And Bramble had heard a story. [beat]
Deep in the hillside, past the briar patch and through the long rabbit hole that smelled of ancient earth and old roots, there was said to be a carrot castle — a real castle, built from the largest, brightest carrots ever grown, by rabbits in the old days when things like that apparently happened. No one from the current warren had been there. The rabbit hole that led to it had not been used in many seasons.
Bramble decided Tuesday was as good a day as any. [softly]
She found the old hole behind the elder tree, narrower than the others, half-hidden by roots. She flattened her ears and went in.
The tunnel wound for longer than she expected — down, then level, then gently up, through layers of earth that smelled of clay and time. [pacing] Her eyes adjusted. Her whiskers mapped the walls on either side. Her brave bunny heart thumped at a moderately elevated rate that she chose to interpret as excitement rather than nerves.
Then the tunnel opened up. [with growing realization]
The carrot castle was smaller than the legend suggested — the old rabbits had apparently been modest in their architecture — but it was absolutely, unmistakably real. Walls of packed earth reinforced with the dried remains of enormous carrots, orange still showing at the corners. A doorway made from two great curved roots. A floor swept clean by someone, some time ago. In the middle, on a low shelf of compressed earth: three perfect carrots, fresh as this morning, as though someone had left them. [beat]
Bramble sat in the carrot castle for a long time. She ate one carrot slowly, in the old underground quiet.
[softly] Then she took the other two home for her friends.
Some quests end in legend. Some end in something even better: something real, and good, and meant to be shared.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
