Once upon a time, in a house where the radiators ticked warmly and the windowsills were wide enough to sleep on, there lived a cozy cat named Luna. [long pause]
Luna was grey as a rain cloud with four white paws and whiskers so long and precise they seemed designed for a purpose more important than mere navigation. She spent her days in the careful rotation that cats have perfected over millennia: a nap in the morning sun patch, a light investigation of any new smell, afternoon attendance at the window, early evening grooming, and then — the most important part — the moon yarn. [sighs with deep domestic satisfaction]
The moon yarn was Luna's name for the particular time just before sleep. [beat]
It began when the house went quiet and the moonlight came in through the bedroom curtains and fell, in a long pale stripe, across the floor. Luna would sit in that stripe of moonlight and wait. And then — always, without fail — the yarn would come.
Not a real ball of yarn. Something else entirely. [softly]
A dream, half-begun before sleep arrived — a long, luminous thread of something that floated in the moonlight and needed chasing. Luna would follow it with her eyes, then with one careful paw, then the other. [with growing realization] It moved like light does when it shifts — there and not there, clear and then gone, leading her in slow spirals across the moonlit floor.
The whisker dream always came in the middle of this. Luna would feel her whiskers — so sensitive, so precisely tuned to the world — begin to read the moonlit air around her the way her paws read a warm surface. The air was full of information, even at night. Especially at night. [pacing]
She would follow the moon yarn until her eyes grew heavy. Until the pale stripe of moonlight had moved, as it always did, to a different part of the floor. Until the whisker dream gave way to something deeper and quieter and full of the particular warmth of a sleeping house in the middle of the night.
[softly] She would curl then — nose to tail, paws tucked, whiskers still — and the last thing she knew before morning was the moonlight moving slowly across her fur like a hand.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
