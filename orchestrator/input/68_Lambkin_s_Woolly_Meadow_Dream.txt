Once upon a time, in a meadow where the grass was soft and the sky was enormous and the days moved slowly in the best possible way, there lived a little lambkin named Pip. [long pause]
Pip was new to the world — only a few weeks old — and everything in it was still astonishing to him. The way the grass felt between his hooves. The sound the wind made in his ears. The smell of the meadow after rain, which he had decided was the finest smell in existence. [sighs with the wonder of someone encountering things for the first time]
But most astonishing of all were the woolly clouds. [beat]
They looked like him. That was the thing. Or — he looked like them. Great white billowing shapes, thick and soft and rounded, moving slowly across the wide blue sky above the meadow in the unhurried way that reminded Pip, very much, of the way the older sheep moved around the field. [softly]
He spent whole afternoons looking up at them.
His mother watched him from nearby — a patient, warm presence — and let him look. [with growing realization]
"What are you thinking?" she asked one afternoon.
Pip was quiet for a moment. The woolly clouds moved. One of them looked like a lamb. It really did — four short legs, a round fluffy body, a small rounded head.
"That one looks like me," Pip said.
His mother looked up. [long pause] "Maybe," she said, "it is you. Your dream self. Looking down to check that everything is all right."
Pip thought about this. He waved one small hoof at the cloud lamb. [pacing]
The woolly cloud moved on — slowly, gently, as clouds do — but not before it seemed to shift, slightly, in a way that might have been a wave back. Or might have been the wind.
[softly] That evening Pip fell asleep in the meadow grass before he even made it home, curled in a small warm circle, his wool still warm from the sun. He dreamed of a meadow seen from above — green and soft and wide, with one small white shape in the middle of it, looking up.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
