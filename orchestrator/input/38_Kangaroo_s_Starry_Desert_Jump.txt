Once upon a time, in the wide red desert where the days were blazing hot and the nights were cold and crystal clear, there lived a little kangaroo named Joey. [long pause]
Joey had strong back legs built for jumping, a pouch he wasn't quite big enough to fill yet, and a habit of looking up at the night sky with an expression of complete wonder. Every evening, when the desert cooled and the stars appeared one by one, Joey would sit very still on a flat red rock and count them. [sighs contentedly]
He had never counted them all the way to the end. He wasn't sure there was an end. [beat]
One evening, his grandmother sat beside him on the rock. She was very old and very wise and smelled comfortingly of warm dust and eucalyptus.
"What are you looking for?" she asked.
"The desert stars," Joey said. "I'm trying to count them all."
His grandmother was quiet for a moment. [softly] "Your grandfather used to say," she said, "that each one is a jump waiting to happen. That the desert stars are just destinations you haven't reached yet."
Joey looked at the stars with new eyes. [with growing realization] Each one a jump. Each one a somewhere-to-go.
He stood up on his flat red rock. He crouched low on his powerful legs, the way he had been practicing all summer. And then he jumped — higher than he had ever jumped before — reaching up toward the desert stars with everything he had.
He didn't touch them, of course. [pacing] But for one extraordinary moment at the top of his jump, the stars seemed very close indeed. Close enough to feel their light on his paws.
He landed back on the red rock, breathing hard, heart full.
[softly] His grandmother smiled. "Same jump tomorrow?"
"Higher tomorrow," said Joey.
And above the wide red desert, the stars shimmered on — patient and waiting, as they always are, for those who keep reaching.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
