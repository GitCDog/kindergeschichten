Once upon a time, in a kingdom where the mountains were always covered in mist and the rivers ran cold and clear, there lived a brave knight named Edmund. [long pause]
Edmund had the shiniest armor in the whole kingdom — his breastplate gleamed, his visor shone, and his boots could be seen from three fields away on a sunny day. He had won many tournaments. He had never, however, faced a dragon. [sighs] Until today.
The dragon had been seen near the northern pass. The king had asked for a volunteer. Edmund had stepped forward before anyone else even had the chance.
He rode his horse to the northern pass with his lance held high. [beat] The horse, it should be noted, was considerably less enthusiastic than Edmund.
They found the dragon in a valley below the pass — an enormous creature with dark green scales and wings like storm clouds. Edmund raised his lance. He opened his mouth to shout something heroic.
[softly] And then he noticed something odd.
The dragon was not looking at him. It was looking at its own foot, which was wedged between two large boulders, its face twisted in an expression that Edmund could only describe as embarrassed.
"Are you... stuck?" Edmund asked.
The dragon closed its eyes. [abruptly] "Yes," it said, with great dignity. "I landed badly."
Edmund lowered his lance. He climbed down from his horse. He walked slowly around the boulders, examining the situation. [pacing] The dragon watched him with one enormous eye, unsure what to expect.
"If I push from this side," Edmund said, more to himself than to the dragon, "and you pull —"
"That might work," the dragon said carefully.
It took twenty minutes, three attempts, and one moment where Edmund's shining armor got thoroughly dented. But eventually, with a great grinding of stone, the dragon's foot came free.
[with growing realization] It stretched its wings. It looked down at Edmund — small, dented, covered in dust.
"You could have walked away," the dragon said.
"Yes," said Edmund.
"Why didn't you?"
Edmund polished a small scuff off his breastplate. [beat] "Because it was the right thing to do," he said simply.
The dragon nodded slowly. It lowered its great head in a bow. And it breathed — not fire, but a warm, golden gust that polished Edmund's armor back to its original shine.
Some battles, Edmund rode home thinking, are won without a single swing of a sword.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
