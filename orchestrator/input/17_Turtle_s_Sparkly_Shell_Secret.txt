Once upon a time, on a beach where the ocean waves rolled in one after another like sleepy sighs, there lived a shy turtle named Tilly. [long pause]
Tilly had a shell that was perfectly ordinary — smooth, brown, a little dull. She had always felt a bit embarrassed about it. All the other creatures at the beach had something beautiful: the hermit crab had a swirling pink home, the starfish gleamed red and gold, and the jellyfish floated past like living lanterns. [sighs] Tilly's shell was just... brown.
So Tilly spent most of her time tucked inside it, waiting for the other animals to pass before she came out. [beat]
One afternoon, a particularly large ocean wave rolled in — larger than usual — and when it pulled back, it left behind something on the sand right in front of Tilly's hiding spot. Something small and round and gleaming.
A shiny shell. [softly] The most beautiful shell Tilly had ever seen. It caught the light in every direction — purple, pink, gold, silver — shifting like a tiny rainbow with every movement.
Tilly crept out slowly and picked it up. She turned it over in her flippers. It was perfect.
For a moment she thought: I could keep this. No one would know.
But then she heard a small voice nearby — a hermit crab, searching the sand frantically, muttering to himself. [with growing realization] "My shell, my shell — the wave took it — I can't find my shell anywhere —"
Tilly looked at the shiny shell in her flippers. She looked at the hermit crab. [long pause]
She walked over and held it out.
The hermit crab froze. His tiny eyes went wide. "That's mine!" he gasped. "How did you find it?"
"The wave left it for me to find," Tilly said simply. "But it was yours."
The hermit crab looked at Tilly's plain brown shell. Then he reached into a pouch at his side and pulled out something small — a perfect round pearl, glowing softly pink. "For you," he said. "To carry."
[pacing] Tilly tucked the pearl into the edge of her shell. In the afternoon light, it glowed against the brown in the most beautiful way.
She learned that day that kindness has a way of making everything shimmer.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
