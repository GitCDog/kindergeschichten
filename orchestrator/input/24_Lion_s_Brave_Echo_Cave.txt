Once upon a time, on the golden savanna where the grass rippled like a warm sea and the sunsets turned everything amber and rose, there lived a brave lion named Leo. [long pause]
Leo was young — his mane was still coming in, more fluff than fur — and he was brave in the way young lions are: loudly, and a little bit nervously. He boasted about his bravery at every opportunity. He practiced his roar each morning until the zebras politely moved to the other side of the hill. [sighs]
But there was one place Leo had never gone. The courage cave. [beat]
It sat at the base of a rocky cliff at the edge of the savanna — a dark opening in the stone, deep enough that you couldn't see the back of it. Strange echoes came out of it. Every animal on the savanna had a reason not to go inside. Leo had sixteen reasons. He had counted.
"A truly brave lion," said his grandmother one evening, "doesn't need to announce his bravery. He just goes."
[softly] Leo thought about that for a long time.
The next morning, before anyone was awake, he walked to the courage cave alone. He stood at the entrance. He looked in. It was very dark. [beat]
He took one step inside. Then another. His paws were quieter than he expected. The ground was cool and smooth under his feet.
And then he roared — not to frighten anything, but because the urge came up from somewhere deep in his chest and he couldn't stop it. [abruptly]
The roaring echo came back.
But it didn't sound frightening. It sounded — [with growing realization] magnificent. His roar multiplied ten times over, filling the cave, bouncing off every wall, rolling back out through the entrance and across the whole savanna.
Leo stood in the middle of the roaring echo and felt something shift inside him.
Real bravery, he understood then, wasn't performing for anyone. It was walking into the dark place and finding out what you're made of. [pacing]
He walked back out into the golden morning, sat down in the grass, and didn't say a word about it to anyone.
He didn't need to.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
