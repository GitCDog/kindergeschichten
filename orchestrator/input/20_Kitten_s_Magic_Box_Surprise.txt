Once upon a time, in a warm kitchen that always smelled of tuna and contentment, there lived a curious kitten named Pip. [long pause]
Pip had enormous eyes — the kind that noticed everything — and a tail that never quite knew what to do with itself. He had explored every corner of the house: the sock drawer, the space under the sofa, the back of the wardrobe where an old tennis ball had been quietly rolling around since before Pip was born. [sighs] He had investigated all of it. Thoroughly.
But there was one thing he had never investigated. The mysterious box. [beat]
It had appeared on the kitchen table three days ago. It was brown cardboard. It had air holes. It made, occasionally, a very faint rustling sound. Pip's tail had not stopped twitching since it arrived.
He approached it for the fourteenth time that morning. He sat in front of it. He stared at it. [softly]
The box rustled.
Pip jumped backwards so fast he knocked over his food bowl. [abruptly]
"It's all right," said a small voice from inside the box. "I'm just finding a comfortable position."
Pip stared. His ears rotated like satellite dishes. [with growing realization] "...You can talk?"
"Can't everyone?" said the voice.
"Not really," said Pip. "What are you?"
"A yarn ball," said the voice pleasantly.
Pip blinked very slowly. This seemed unlikely. He had interacted with yarn balls before and they had never said anything. "What kind of yarn ball talks?"
"The magical kind," said the voice. "Also the kind that needs someone to open this box, because it has been three days and I am finding it quite stuffy."
[pacing] Pip batted the lid with one paw. Then again. On the third attempt it tipped open — and out tumbled the most extraordinary ball of yarn Pip had ever seen. It was every color at once, shifting as it rolled, and it glowed faintly with a warm golden light.
It bounced once. It looked at Pip. Pip looked at it.
"Chase me," it said.
[beat] Pip was already running.
They played until dinnertime — through the kitchen, up the stairs, around the sofa twice, and back through the kitchen again. Pip had never been so happy in his entire life.
Some surprises, he decided breathlessly, are absolutely worth the wait.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
