Once upon a time, in a jungle where every shade of green existed somewhere and the light changed every hour, there lived a curious chameleon named Cosmo. [long pause]
Cosmo could change color — that was simply what chameleons did — but Cosmo had elevated it to something approaching an art form. Not just green to brown to grey, but precise, deliberate, exact. He could match the dappled shade of a fig leaf in afternoon light. He could replicate the exact russet of a fallen leaf in two seconds flat. He could become, if he concentrated, very nearly invisible. [sighs with professional satisfaction]
He had played leaf hide every day of his life. [beat]
The game was simple: find the perfect leaf, match it perfectly, and see how long before someone noticed you were there. Cosmo had once gone undetected for four hours while sitting on a branch twelve centimetres from a very observant hummingbird.
But today something different happened.
A small lizard — young, bright-eyed, new to the jungle — spotted him immediately. [with growing realization]
Cosmo froze. He was certain his color match was perfect.
"I can see you," said the small lizard.
"Impossible," said Cosmo.
"Your eye moved," said the lizard. "You matched the leaf exactly. But your eye moved and eyes don't move on leaves."
[long pause] Cosmo thought about this. In all his years of color change, he had never considered his eyes.
"Teach me," said the small lizard. "How do you do it?"
[pacing] Cosmo looked at the lizard — who was a bright, unbothered green, making no effort to hide at all, perfectly visible and apparently unconcerned. He thought about all the hours he had spent invisible.
"Why do you want to hide?" Cosmo asked.
The lizard blinked. "I don't," he said. "I want to find. If I learn how you see the leaves so perfectly, maybe I can find things others miss."
[softly] Cosmo had never thought about his skill that way before. All that seeing — used not to disappear, but to discover.
He climbed down from his branch. "All right," he said. "Let me show you how to look."
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
