Once upon a time, high in a eucalyptus tree that grew on a gentle hillside overlooking a wide Australian valley, there lived a kind koala named Kit. [long pause]
Kit had thick grey fur, a broad black nose, and round ears that gave her a permanent air of mild surprise. She spent most of her days wedged in the fork of her favorite eucalyptus branch — the dreamy branch, she called it — where the bark was smooth and slightly curved, as though it had been shaped over many years specifically to fit one small koala perfectly. [sighs deeply and contentedly]
Kit was a great believer in rest. Not laziness — rest. There is a difference, and Kit could explain it at length, though she rarely had energy to do so. [beat]
She knew every sound the eucalyptus tree made. The way it creaked in the morning when the sun hit the eastern side and the wood expanded. The soft rustle of the leaves — silvery, fragrant — when the breeze came up the hillside. The distant sounds of the valley below: birds, wind, the occasional vehicle on the far road. [softly]
She knew these sounds so well that when something was different, she noticed.
One afternoon, on the dreamy branch, she heard a very small sound she had not heard before. Below her, in the hollow of the lower trunk, a soft scratching. Then stillness. Then scratching again. [with growing realization]
She descended, slowly and carefully as koalas do, and looked into the hollow.
A possum — young, bewildered, with enormous eyes — blinked back at her. "I got separated," it said. "I don't know this tree."
Kit looked at the possum. She looked at the hollow, which was large enough for two. [pacing]
"This is a good tree," she said. "Very steady. The branch on the left side is the best for sleeping. You can stay until you know where you're going."
She climbed back up to the dreamy branch. Below her, the possum settled into the hollow. [softly]
The eucalyptus tree creaked gently in the afternoon sun. Kit closed her eyes. Kindness, she thought drowsily, costs so little and means so much.
She was asleep within seconds.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
