Once upon a time, on a hillside where the thyme grew low and the afternoons lasted a long time, there lived a tiny tortoise named Theodore. [long pause]
Theodore was young, as tortoises go — only twelve years old, which in tortoise terms was still very much the beginning. His shell was smooth and domed and a bright young green-brown, and he moved through the world at a pace that suited him perfectly: slow enough to see everything, fast enough to get where he was going eventually. [sighs with the contentment of someone who has never been in a rush]
His ancient shell was the most interesting thing about him, though he didn't know it yet. [beat]
He met an old tortoise one afternoon — ancient in the way that tortoises become, worn and wise, her shell patterned with the rings and marks of a very long life. She was sitting in a patch of sun on a flat stone, apparently doing nothing, which in tortoises can be the most productive state of all.
"How old are you?" Theodore asked. [softly]
"Older than the oak on the south slope," said the old tortoise. "Older than the wall at the bottom of the hill. I was here when the wall was built, and I watched."
Theodore looked at her shell. It told a story in its marks — a long scrape on one side where a journey had been hard, a patch of unusual color where something had happened that he couldn't name, the deep rings of many seasons counted.
[with growing realization] "Does your shell remember everything?" he asked.
"Everything," she said. "A shell is a diary. You've barely started yours."
Theodore looked down at his own smooth young shell. So few marks. So few stories yet. [pacing]
"Does that make you sad?" he asked. "Having so many marks?"
The old tortoise took a very long time to answer. [beat] "Each mark," she said finally, "is a day I was here. I would not trade one."
She settled further into her patch of sun. Theodore sat beside her and looked out at the hillside — at the thyme, the wall, the old oak — and thought about the long slow journey ahead of him, and felt, for the first time, exactly how much there was to look forward to.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
