Among all the foods in the kingdom of the kitchen, none shone quite as magnificently as a perfectly made sweet potato fry — and Goldie was determined to be exactly that. [pause] She had been cut from a large, glorious sweet potato that morning, and now she sat with her fellow fries on a baking sheet, about to begin the greatest transformation of her life.

"Where are we going?" asked the fry next to her nervously.

"On a golden quest," said Goldie, who had heard stories from older fries and knew that the oven was the beginning of everything.

The heat was extraordinary. It rose around them like a warm, invisible tide, and Goldie felt herself changing — the outside of her growing firm and golden and crispy, while inside she became soft and sweet and perfectly tender. [softly] The sugars in her began to caramelize, turning from pale orange to deep, gleaming gold.

She felt magnificent. She felt like herself — her very best self — for the first time.

When the oven door opened and a wave of cool kitchen air rushed in, Goldie looked down at her own surface and gasped. She was the color of a sunset. She was perfectly golden, curved and crispy, and she smelled of warmth and sweet earth and something almost like honey.

[pause] The cook lifted the baking tray and set it on the counter, and the family gathered around. A small child reached for Goldie first — which, she felt, was entirely correct.

"This one's perfect," the child declared.

Goldie's crispy quest had been brief, but it had been magnificent. She had gone into the oven plain and come out golden. She had set out to become the best version of herself, and the heat and time had made it happen.

[softly] Other fries finished their quest around her. Some were crispier, some softer, but each one was warm and golden and exactly right in their own particular way. Together they disappeared, plate by plate, into the happy quiet of an evening that smelled of warmth and comfort and the very best kind of food.
