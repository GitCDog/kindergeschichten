On the evening when the kitchen first started smelling of warm things and cinnamon, a magic sweet potato named Sable understood that her time had come. [long pause]

She had been waiting in the vegetable basket for exactly the right moment, and this, clearly, was it.

The cozy oven had been warming since afternoon, ticking softly as ovens do, sending small waves of heat out through the kitchen like a gentle invitation. The window glass had fogged in the corners. A cat was asleep on the lowest shelf of the bookcase, arranged in the neat oval of a creature who has solved the problem of the cold evening entirely. [sighs]

Sable went in at five o'clock.

The cozy oven closed around her with a soft click, and the world went warm.

There is a particular quality to the darkness inside a warm oven that Sable could not easily describe to the potatoes back in the basket. It was not a frightening darkness. It was more the darkness of being held — of being surrounded on all sides by something that meant well. [beat]

She felt herself beginning to change.

Her outside grew drier and her inside grew sweeter, and the warmth went all the way through her in a long, slow process that was not at all uncomfortable. It was rather like gradually becoming more completely herself, she thought. Like the warmth was finding everything she had always been and drawing it closer to the surface. [softly]

Outside, the kitchen window showed the first stars appearing over the garden. The cat rearranged itself slightly without waking up. The smell in the kitchen intensified into something that made anyone nearby feel, without quite knowing why, that everything was going to be all right.

At six o'clock the oven door opened.

Sable emerged into the warm glow of the kitchen light, steaming gently, smelling of herself and of the cozy oven and of the particular sweetness that only comes from being patient and staying still and letting heat do its work. [with growing realization]

She was placed on the table with care.

Outside the kitchen window the garden was dark and the stars were fully out. Inside everything was golden and warm and smelled of good things. The cat opened one eye, considered the situation, and closed it again with evident satisfaction.

The evening settled in around the warm kitchen like a blanket drawn close.

[pause] Good night my little friend
