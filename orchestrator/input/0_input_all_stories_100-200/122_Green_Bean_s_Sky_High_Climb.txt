The climbing vine had been growing since the first warm week of summer, winding itself up the old bamboo pole at the garden's edge. Most of the garden watched it politely from a distance. [long pause]

Not Benny. Benny was a playful green bean, and the moment he saw the climbing vine, he knew exactly what he wanted to do.

He wanted to climb it.

"That seems very high," said a nearby pea, looking up.

"That's rather the point," said Benny cheerfully.

He started at the bottom on a Tuesday morning, when the sun was still low and the air was cool. He wrapped himself around the vine and began to pull upward, a little at a time. The vine was strong and rough and smelled of summer. Above him the sky was a clean pale blue that went on for a very long way. [sighs]

The first third was easy. He could still see the ground clearly, still hear the familiar sounds of the garden below — the hum of bees, the small conversations of the lettuces, the occasional deep thought of the squash. It felt good to be moving.

The second third was harder. The wind found him more easily up here, pushing gently from different directions as if testing his grip. His arms ached pleasantly in the way that comes from doing something real. He paused once to look down and felt a small dizzying thrill at how far below the garden floor had gotten. [beat]

The last third felt like flying.

Not because he left the vine — he held on carefully, as a sensible green bean should — but because up here the sky was everywhere and the world below looked completely different. Small and organized and patterned, like a quilt someone had laid out very deliberately. The rows of vegetables, the garden paths, the gate. [softly]

He could see a pond he had never known existed, glinting silver behind the far hedge. He could see fields stretching away toward something blue that might have been hills. He could see a sky reach that the ground could not offer.

He stayed up there until the afternoon clouds came in, then climbed back down slowly, the right way.

"Well?" said the pea.

"Worth it," said Benny. "Completely worth it." [with growing realization]

The garden was quiet now in the evening light, and the climbing vine swayed gently in the last of the breeze, still pointing upward, still patient, still waiting.

[pause] Good night my little friend
