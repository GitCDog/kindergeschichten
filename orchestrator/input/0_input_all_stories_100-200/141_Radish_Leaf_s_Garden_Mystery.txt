The morning dew had barely dried when a curious radish leaf named Ria noticed something strange. [pause] Right there in the middle of the garden path, a tiny trail of crumbs curved between the flower pots and disappeared under the old wooden fence.

"Something was here last night," Ria whispered, her green edges trembling with excitement. She loved nothing more than a good garden mystery.

She began following the trail carefully. First past the strawberry patch, then around the lavender bush, and finally down into the soft, shadowed corner of the garden she called the Hidden Bugs Quarter.

It was a place where beetles wore coats of shimmering armor, where caterpillars read tiny books, and where fireflies kept lanterns lit even during the day. Most plants were too shy to visit. But Ria was curious.

"Excuse me," she called softly. "Does anyone know about the trail of crumbs on the path?"

A small beetle named Bruno looked up from polishing his shell. [softly] "Ah, you must be looking for Milo," he said. "He had a midnight snack and made a bit of a mess, I'm afraid."

"Milo?" asked Ria.

"A baby snail," said Bruno kindly. "He is new here and very afraid of the big garden. He only moves at night when no one can see him."

Ria felt a wave of warmth in her leafy heart. She asked Bruno where Milo lived, and he pointed to a small curl beneath a mossy stone.

There, tucked inside his swirly shell, was Milo. His eyes went wide when he saw Ria.

"I'm sorry about the crumbs," he said in a tiny voice. "I didn't mean to start a garden mystery."

Ria laughed softly. [pause] "Don't be sorry at all. You led me to the most wonderful hidden bugs I've ever met. And now I've made new friends too."

Milo slowly stretched out and smiled. For the first time, he felt brave enough to explore in the daylight.

Together, Ria and Milo wandered back along the garden path. The hidden bugs waved from behind their mossy rocks, and the whole garden seemed to glow a little warmer than before.
