Tilly was a tiny turnip leaf who had never wandered very far from the patch where she grew. [pause] But one quiet morning, she noticed something she had somehow never seen before — a winding garden path made of smooth stepping stones, curving away through the lavender and disappearing around the corner of the old garden wall.

Where did it go?

She was small. She was soft. The soft soil beneath her was comfortable and familiar. There were many good reasons to stay.

But there was one very good reason to go: she was curious.

[softly] So Tilly let the morning breeze give her a gentle push, and she began to move along the garden path.

The stepping stones were smooth and warm from the early sun. On the first stone, a family of tiny ants marched past carrying crumbs in a perfectly organized line. She stepped aside to let them through and felt very polite.

On the second stone, a dewdrop was balancing perfectly on a spider's thread strung between two lavender stems. Tilly held her breath so as not to disturb it. She had never seen anything so delicate.

On the third stone, she found a note — or what looked like a note. It was a curled piece of bark with a small curved line that she decided meant "keep going."

[pause] She kept going.

Around the corner, the garden path opened into a small hidden garden within the garden — a circle of tall sunflowers with their heads bent together as if sharing a secret, and at the center of them, a smooth flat stone warm enough to nap on.

A bee dozed there in the afternoon warmth.

Tilly settled beside the bee and felt the soft soil of a place she had never known existed, yet which felt perfectly, quietly, wonderfully right.

She stayed until the light turned golden, then made her way back along the garden path, touching each warm stone one more time.

[softly] She went to sleep that night knowing that the world was larger and more beautiful than she had imagined, and that sometimes the best thing a small leaf can do is follow the path she has never taken before.
