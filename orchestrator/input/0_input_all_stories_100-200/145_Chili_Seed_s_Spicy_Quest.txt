On the highest shelf of the seed pantry, behind the basil and the parsley, there sat a brave chili seed named Carlos. [pause] He was small and dark and full of fire — though he had never had the chance to show it.

His older brothers and sisters had been planted long ago and had grown into tall, fiery chili plants that everyone in the garden admired. But Carlos was late, forgotten on the shelf, dreaming every night of his great spicy quest.

One hot summer morning, a child with muddy boots and a small trowel discovered him behind the seed jars. "Oh! One more seed!" she cried, and carried him outside.

The sun was blazing. A heat wave had settled over the garden like a heavy warm blanket, and most of the other plants were drooping sleepily in the shade. But Carlos loved it. [softly] He pushed his roots deep into the warm soil and stretched his first tiny leaves up toward the blazing sky.

"This is exactly what I needed," he said to himself, and he meant it with every fiber of his small, fiery heart.

The summer was long and hot, and Carlos grew fiercer and prouder with each passing week. The heat wave that made other plants wilt only made him stronger. His stem grew thick, his leaves grew bold, and one morning — tiny flowers appeared. Then tiny peppers.

[pause] And those peppers grew red and magnificent and hot, curving upward like little flames reaching for the sky.

The day the cook came to pick his first pepper, Carlos stood perfectly still and proud. He had begun his spicy quest late. He had spent months waiting on a dusty shelf. But he had made it — and he had grown more gloriously than anyone expected.

The cook held the pepper up and whistled softly. "This is the finest chili of the whole season."

Carlos swelled with quiet pride. His quest had not been one of rushing or racing. It had been one of waiting patiently, and then growing fiercely when the moment finally came.

[softly] The brave chili seed had become exactly what he always knew he would be — bold, bright, and beautifully, perfectly spicy.
