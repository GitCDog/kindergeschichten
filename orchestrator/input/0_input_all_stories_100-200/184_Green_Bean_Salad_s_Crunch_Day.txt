Gracie the green bean salad felt the cool refrigerator air exchange with the warm kitchen, felt herself lifted and carried to the counter, and stretched in her bowl with the particular pleasure of someone who knows they are about to be very much appreciated.

She was a summer bowl composition of rare intelligence. Fresh green beans — still just slightly crunchy from the blanching, not soft, not raw, but exactly in between — tossed with thin slivers of red onion and toasted almonds and the kind of bright lemon dressing that made everything it touched taste more itself. "I am," said Gracie pleasantly. "I have very good texture."
