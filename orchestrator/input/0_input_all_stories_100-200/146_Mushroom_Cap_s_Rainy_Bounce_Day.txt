On the morning of the great rainstorm, a playful mushroom cap named Moppet woke up happier than she had ever felt before. [pause] Rain! Real, splashing, thundering rain, bouncing off her wide brown cap like tiny dancers on a drumhead.

Most of the garden creatures had scurried under rocks and leaves. The earthworms dove deeper. The beetles closed up their shells. But Moppet stood in the rainy forest clearing and laughed with delight.

"This is the most wonderful thing," she said, tilting her cap to catch a big fat raindrop and watch it roll to the edge and plop to the ground below.

A tiny frog named Fred hopped beside her on a mossy stone. He wore a look of complete skepticism. "You enjoy this?" he said, staring at the downpour.

"It's my favorite kind of bounce fun," said Moppet. "Watch!"

A raindrop fell. It hit her cap, bounced up, caught another drop, and the two of them splashed together into a puddle below. Moppet giggled. [softly] Fred watched this with one raised eyebrow, then — very slowly — he began to smile.

"May I try?" he asked.

"Climb up!" said Moppet.

Fred settled himself on the edge of her cap. The next raindrop hit, and the bounce sent a delightful shiver through both of them. Fred's eyes went wide. He let out a sound that was halfway between a ribbit and a laugh.

[pause] Soon he was hopping from Moppet's cap to a wide leaf, to a curling fern, to a mossy stone, bouncing through the rainy forest in a great zigzag of joy.

Other small creatures crept out from their hiding spots to watch. Then, one by one, they joined in — a hedgehog rolling happily through a puddle, two beetles sliding down a leaf-slide, even a shy snail who stretched out his neck to feel the rain.

By afternoon, when the storm gentled to a soft drizzle, the whole clearing had been turned into the most wonderful rainy bounce park any forest creature had ever seen.

[softly] Moppet settled back with a happy sigh. Her cap was full of little waterdrops that glittered like tiny stars. She closed her eyes as the last soft raindrops pattered gently on her wide, happy head.
