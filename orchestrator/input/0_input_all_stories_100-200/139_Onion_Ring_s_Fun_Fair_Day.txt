The fun fair came to town every year in the last week of summer, and every year the residents of the kitchen argued about who would go first. [long pause]

This year, a silly onion ring named Otto had solved the problem by arriving at the fun fair at opening time, before the argument had even begun, which he felt was an elegant solution.

The fun fair was exactly as he'd left it the year before, which was part of what made it a fun fair and not just a fair: it was reliably, dependably, aggressively fun. There were rides that spun. There were games that almost made sense. There was a tent full of golden crunch smells that hit you from twenty feet away and made your decisions for you. [sighs]

Otto went to the golden crunch tent first.

He collected a paper cone of fried things that smelled of everything right about summer, and he walked through the fun fair eating them in a leisurely way, surveying the options.

The ring toss was going badly for everyone, as ring tosses always do, but with a cheerful sort of futility that was its own entertainment. The wheel of fortune was spinning with great enthusiasm. A small stage in the corner had music that was three different keys at once and did not seem to be bothered about this. [beat]

Otto went on the ride that spun you sideways, which was his favorite, and emerged from it feeling pleasantly rearranged, his onion ring shape slightly less precise than it had been before but his general happiness substantially improved. [softly]

He tried the ring toss three times and got one ring to briefly touch a bottle before falling off, which he counted.

He watched the wheel of fortune for a while without betting.

He got a second paper cone of golden crunch from the tent, because some decisions benefit from being made twice.

By late afternoon the fun fair was crowded and loud and full of the particular kind of noise that means everyone is having a good time simultaneously, which is a different quality of noise from most noises. [with growing realization]

Otto stood in the middle of it all, slightly sticky from the golden crunch, slightly dizzy from the spinning ride, surrounded by the full height of summer's last week, and felt that this, specifically, was what a fun fair day should be.

The lights came on as the evening came down, turning the whole fair gold, and Otto stayed until they did.
