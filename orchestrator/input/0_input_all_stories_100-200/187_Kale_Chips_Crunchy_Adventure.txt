The kale chips had come out of the oven with a sound like a very gentle round of applause. [pause] Kira the kale chip was among them — thin and dark and magnificently crispy, curled at the edges where the heat had worked its magic, with a light dusting of sea salt that glittered like tiny diamonds.

She had not always been confident about her destiny. When she had still been a flat, slightly leathery kale leaf, the oven had seemed very hot and very uncertain. But the cook had been kind and precise — the right temperature, the right time, the right amount of olive oil worked into every fold of her leaf.

And now she was this: a crispy snack of extraordinary character.

The healthy crunch was her gift. Not the hollow crunch of an empty thing, but the deep, satisfying crunch of something that had been transformed — leafy and green still at heart, but light as air and perfectly, delicately crisp on the surface. [softly] A chip that happened to also be a vegetable, which was something entirely wonderful.

She was set out in a small bowl and immediately surrounded by curious hands. A child picked her up between two fingers and regarded her with professional skepticism.

"It looks like a leaf," the child said.

"It is a leaf," the cook agreed. "A very crunchy one."

[pause] The child bit into her. The crunch rang out clearly. The child's eyes widened. She finished the rest in one bite, reached for another, and then with her mouth still full said, "These are actually really good."

Kira felt something she hadn't expected — pride. Not in being eaten, exactly, but in having surprised someone. In being the thing people didn't expect to like and then couldn't stop eating. In being, when it came right down to it, a healthy crunch adventure in a small bowl on an ordinary afternoon.

[softly] By the time the bowl was empty, the skeptic was the most enthusiastic convert.
