In the corner of the raised garden bed, where the soil was dark and fine and smelled of good rain, there existed a tiny kingdom that most of the garden did not know about. [long pause]

It had been built by Bram — a brave broccoli sprout who had arrived in the bed three weeks ago and immediately begun organizing his immediate surroundings with great seriousness and a green shield fashioned from a fallen leaf.

The tiny kingdom was small, as kingdoms go. It occupied roughly four square inches of garden bed. Its borders were marked by a circle of small pebbles. Its treasury was a collection of particularly interesting seeds Bram had gathered and stored under a flat stone. Its motto, which Bram had worked out himself, was: Small and Steady. [sighs]

Other residents of the raised bed had mixed feelings about the tiny kingdom.

The radishes thought it was charming. The carrots were politely indifferent. One very old garlic bulb, who had been in the bed for several seasons, observed it with an expression of philosophical acceptance.

"You're building a kingdom?" the garlic asked.

"A mini kingdom," said Bram, which he felt was an important distinction. Not large and complicated, but small and carefully maintained. [beat]

The mini kingdom's main activity was defense — but not of the sword-and-battle variety. Bram's green shield was for keeping things out that shouldn't be in: the cabbage moth who laid eggs on the vegetables, the slugs who moved in at night, the occasionally overenthusiastic bird who pecked at seedlings. Bram was watchful and patient, and so far the tiny kingdom had remained undisturbed. [softly]

In the evenings, when the garden was quiet and the air was cool, Bram would stand at the pebble border of his tiny kingdom and look out at the larger garden — the rows of things growing, the walls, the sky above — and feel a particular calm that he couldn't quite name. [with growing realization]

It was the calm of someone who knows exactly where they stand, and what they're standing for, and that even a very small thing can matter if it's tended with care.

The garden bed settled around him in the dimming light, rich and dark and full of things growing quietly in every direction.

The tiny kingdom was at peace.

[pause] Good night my little friend
