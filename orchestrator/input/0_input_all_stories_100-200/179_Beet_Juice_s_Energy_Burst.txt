In the tall glass on the breakfast table, something remarkable was happening. [pause] Beet juice — dark ruby red, almost luminous — was shimmering in the morning light, and at its very heart, a spark of extraordinary energy was building.

Bruno the beet juice had been pressed early that morning from the finest, most deeply red beets in the whole garden. He carried everything those beets had stored up over a whole summer — rain and soil and sunlight and deep root energy, all of it compressed and concentrated into his vivid, glowing red.

He was, if he said so himself, electric.

"Good morning," said the orange juice in the pitcher beside him, pleasantly. It was a calm, sweet sort of juice, warm and familiar.

"GOOD MORNING," said Bruno, with rather more enthusiasm. [softly] The orange juice blinked.

Bruno couldn't help it. He was full of an energy burst that needed somewhere to go. He could feel it in every molecule — a bright, bouncing, red-sparkle readiness that was the natural state of a very good beet pressed at the height of its power.

[pause] When the tall glass was lifted and tilted, Bruno felt the energy pass through the air in a rushing red wave, and then he was inside the person who had drunk him, spreading warmth and vivid ruby color through everything he touched.

It was his favorite moment. The energy burst, finally released, traveling outward through veins and warmth and breath — the whole day suddenly brighter, the morning more awake, the world more possible.

The person set down the glass and stretched their arms wide, feeling a morning energy they couldn't quite explain but were grateful for.

[softly] Bruno swirled contentedly in his last corner of the glass. He had given his red sparkle to the morning, and the morning had opened up wide and bright and full of possibility in return.
