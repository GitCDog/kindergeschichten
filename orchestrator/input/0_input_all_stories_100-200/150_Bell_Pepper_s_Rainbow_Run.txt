[pause] There was Rojo, a red slice who always ran fastest. And Marigold, an orange slice who never stopped smiling. And Indigo, a deep purple slice who hopped lightly on her seeds like springs.

The Rainbow Road was the most famous path in the whole vegetable market — a long, winding color run through seven stations, each painted a different color of the rainbow. Every participant had to touch every station and bring back a ribbon to show they had run the whole colorful course.

"Ready?" called the carrot judge.

"Ready!" shouted the pepper slices.

And they were off. [softly] Bella ran with everything she had. Her green stem glinted in the morning sunlight as she raced through the red station, then the orange, then the brilliant yellow. She could feel her colors brightening with each station, as if the road were painting her even more beautiful as she ran.

At the green station, she paused for just a moment to wave at a small tomato watching from the fence. The little tomato's face lit up and it waved back with both arms. [pause] Bella ran on, feeling lighter than before.

By the time she reached the violet and purple stations, she was laughing out loud with joy — not because she was in first place (she wasn't), and not because she was winning (she would finish fourth), but because the rainbow road itself was so unbelievably beautiful.

At the finish line, all the pepper slices came together, panting and glowing and happy. Their ribbons were different colors, but when they held them up side by side, they made a rainbow.

"That was the most wonderful color run," said Bella breathlessly.

Rojo nodded. Marigold beamed. Indigo laughed. And the whole market cheered for the bright, bold, beautiful slices who had run the whole road together.
