The day the great orange river began to flow through the kitchen was the most exciting day the fruit bowl had ever witnessed. [pause] Carrot juice — bright, vivid, and tremendously cheerful — poured from the juicer in a rushing stream that filled a tall glass pitcher and turned the whole counter orange-gold.

At the front of that flowing river was a tiny drop of carrot juice named Ori, who had been waiting all his life for exactly this moment.

The liquid world of the juicer had been dark and tumbling. But now: light! Space! A whole glass pitcher to explore, with smooth curved walls and a ceiling of swirling bubbles at the top, and the warm kitchen light making every molecule of him glow like a sunset.

"This is magnificent," Ori breathed.

Around him, the orange river churned and settled. Other drops gathered, pooling their light together until the whole pitcher glowed with a deep, warm, perfectly orange warmth. [softly] A straw appeared from above. Ori held very still.

Then he was lifted — swooped upward and through the narrow straw in a great rushing surge — and suddenly he was somewhere else entirely: inside a cool, shadowed glass, with ice cubes towering around him like crystal mountains.

"Hello," said an ice cube pleasantly.

"Hello," said Ori, a little breathlessly.

[pause] He explored the glass thoroughly. He pressed against the cool curved walls and felt the condensation gathering on the outside. He rode a small eddy around a large ice cube and discovered the way the light shifted through the ice, making rainbow patterns on the inside of the glass.

When the glass was lifted and tilted, Ori felt a great surge of the orange river, and for one perfect moment he was flying — a small drop of bright orange light moving through cool, open air toward the wide world beyond.

[softly] He landed softly, having completed his journey from root to glass in a great, glowing, orange-river rush. He had been a carrot, and then a river, and then light itself, bright and warm and perfectly at peace.
