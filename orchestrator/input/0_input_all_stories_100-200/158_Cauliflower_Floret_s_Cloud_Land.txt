Clara the cauliflower floret had always felt that she looked like a cloud — and she had decided this was the finest thing in the world to look like. [pause] She was white and soft and perfectly rounded at the top, and when she looked at herself reflected in a puddle after the rain, she was sure she could see a small, fluffy cumulus cloud looking back at her.

"Someday I will visit Cloud Land," she told the other florets.

They were politely unconvinced. Clouds were quite far away.

But Clara kept the dream. And one misty autumn morning, her wish came true — in a way.

The morning fog rolled in thick and low over the garden, and when it settled around the cauliflower patch, Clara suddenly found herself surrounded on all sides by soft, drifting white. [softly] She could not see the fence or the bean poles or even the row of cabbages two feet away. There was only mist — cool, gentle, and quiet as a held breath.

"I am in Cloud Land," she whispered.

She reached out her small frilly edges and felt the mist move around her. It was cool and damp and softer than anything she had ever touched. [pause] Small droplets gathered on her white surface, making her glitter like the inside of a cloud when the sun touched it at dawn.

A fellow floret named Finn crept close through the fog. "Clara? Is that you?"

"It is," she said. "We are in Cloud Land. Be very still and feel it."

Finn was quiet. Then: "It feels like being inside something gentle."

"Yes," said Clara. "Exactly like that."

They stayed in the soft cloud for a long time, neither of them speaking, both of them listening to the quiet that fog makes when it has settled perfectly around you.

When the sun burned through at last and the mist dissolved, the garden came back piece by piece — the fence, the beans, the cabbages — until Clara stood again in the clear light, still glittering with tiny drops.

[softly] She had visited Cloud Land after all. It had been exactly as wonderful as she had imagined, and gentler, and closer than she had ever dreamed.
