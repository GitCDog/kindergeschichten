Deep in the crunchy forest at the far edge of the garden, where the leaves had gone dry and the pathways crackled pleasantly underfoot, a curious celery named Celia was following a snack trail. [long pause]

The snack trail was not like other trails. It was made of breadcrumbs and seed pods and the occasional dropped cracker, and it wound through the crunchy forest in a direction that seemed to always be slightly ahead of wherever Celia was standing.

She had found the beginning of it that morning, near the compost heap, and had been following it ever since with the focused attention of someone who suspects a very good ending. [sighs]

The crunchy forest was cool and pleasant to walk through. The dried leaves made satisfying sounds. The light came through in pieces, dappled and shifting, and everything smelled of autumn and good things slowly becoming something else. Celia munched thoughtfully as she went, making her own small crunch sounds to match the ones beneath her feet.

She was, she thought, extremely well-suited to this environment. [beat]

Halfway along the snack trail she found a clearing where three beetles were eating an oat biscuit in a cooperative and organized way. They looked up at her without alarm.

"Following the trail?" asked the largest beetle.

"I am," said Celia.

"Good trail," said the beetle, and went back to the biscuit.

Celia continued. The snack trail curved around a large mossy log, dipped into a small hollow that smelled wonderfully of pine, and then rose back up to where the crunchy forest opened onto a small sun-warmed hilltop. [softly]

There, at the end of the trail, was a flat stone with a small selection of seeds and dried fruit arranged on it as if by someone thoughtful. A note beside them read: Well done. This is for you.

Celia sat on the hilltop and ate the seeds and dried fruit and looked out over the crunchy forest spread below her, all its dry-leaf colors and quiet shadows and tangled paths. [with growing realization]

She didn't know who had left the trail, or who had left the ending, and she decided that not knowing was fine. Some trails are worth following simply for the pleasure of the following.

The sun was low now, and the crunchy forest was settling into evening. Somewhere below, a leaf fell with a small, satisfied crunch.
