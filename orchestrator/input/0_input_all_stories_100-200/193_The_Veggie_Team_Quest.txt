The color team had been assembled by the end of the farmers' market morning, and it was a very fine team indeed. [pause] Red peppers, orange carrots, yellow corn, green zucchini, purple eggplant — five vegetables of five different colors, all chosen specifically for a healthy quest that the cook had set out to accomplish.

The quest was a rainbow dinner. One dish of every color, each vegetable roasted to its best, served together on the long platter that only came out for special occasions.

"We should introduce ourselves," said Rosamund the red pepper, who was used to taking charge.

"I'm Carlos the carrot," said the orange one. "Second from the right, usually."

"Cornelius. Corn." The yellow one waved a cob.

"Zara," said the zucchini, simply.

"And I'm Edgar," said the eggplant, with considerable dignity. [softly] Edgar the eggplant always had considerable dignity. It was one of his defining qualities.

The oven was hot and ready. One by one, the veggie team went in — each to their own section of the roasting pan, each cut and seasoned with the same good olive oil and sea salt. The heat moved through the pan in waves, browning edges and caramelizing sugars and deepening colors.

[pause] When they emerged, they were magnificent. Rosamund was blistered and sweet. Carlos was golden at the edges. Cornelius had caramelized to a deep, nutty gold. Zara was soft and tender. Edgar was rich and silky all the way through.

Together on the long platter, they were a rainbow — vivid and warm and abundantly vegetable.

[softly] The family stood around the table and looked at the color team before anyone reached for a serving spoon. Even the youngest, who had strong opinions about eggplant, seemed moved.

The healthy quest had been accomplished, and it was gloriously, colorfully, complete.
