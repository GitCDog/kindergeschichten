Inside the pumpkin there was a seed named Pip, and Pip had the most enormous dreams of anyone in the garden. [long pause]

The other seeds knew about this. They could hear Pip's giant dream in the way that small sounds carry in quiet spaces — a sort of rumbling warmth that passed through the pumpkin walls at night, soft and vast, full of unfinished growing.

"What are you dreaming about?" one of the other seeds asked one morning.

"Growing," said Pip simply.

"How big?"

Pip considered. "Very." [sighs]

The curious pumpkin seed had been inside the pumpkin for a whole season, which is long enough to develop very specific ideas about the world. Pip knew about the smell of the vine that connected the pumpkin to the earth. Pip knew about rain — the sound of it, the way it changed the smell of everything. Pip knew about warmth and its source, and cold and its meaning. [beat]

But Pip had never grown. Not yet.

The growing journey, Pip understood, would begin with darkness and dampness and a slow uncoiling that would take more effort than anything yet attempted. Then would come upward movement through soil, then light — actual light, not the filtered orange glow of the pumpkin interior but real, blue-sky, wide-open light.

And after that: growing. [softly]

Pip lay in the pumpkin and dreamed about all of it in great, patient detail. The roots that would anchor. The leaves that would catch light. The vines that would reach and trail and find things. The eventual pumpkin — enormous, orange, perfectly round — that would be Pip's own someday.

In the dream the giant dream took up the whole garden and most of the field beyond it. In the dream Pip was vast and rooted and full of more seeds who would dream their own growing journeys in the dark.

A seed's dream is not impatience. It is practice. [with growing realization]

Every night Pip dreamed the whole growing journey from beginning to end, getting ready. Every morning Pip waited with the calm certainty of something that knows exactly what it will become, and is willing to take the time required.

Outside the pumpkin, the garden turned slowly toward winter. Inside, the giant dream continued, warm and patient and full of all the summer to come.

[pause] Good night my little friend
