The great summer salad bowl was the happening place of the whole kitchen, and everyone in it knew it. [pause] Slices of cool cucumber, rings of red onion, fat cherry tomatoes, and springs of fresh dill had all gathered in a wide ceramic bowl for what could only be described as the most refreshing party of the season.

Cleo the cucumber slice had arrived first and chosen her spot near the left curve of the bowl, which got the best light and had the most room. She was thin and elegant and pleasantly cool, and she had been looking forward to this all week.

"Summer fun has officially begun," she announced to no one and everyone.

The tomatoes rolled in with their usual exuberance, bumping cheerfully into each other and leaving little red kiss marks on the sides of the bowl. The dill arrived last, fashionably late, and spread itself across the top of the salad with a flourish that smelled of anise and warm fields. [softly] The cool mix settled and sighed contentedly.

A simple dressing arrived — just oil and vinegar and a pinch of something bright — and it moved through the bowl like a summer breeze, coating everyone lightly, making them shine.

"That is genuinely perfect," said Cleo, feeling the cool dressing settle over her.

[pause] The bowl was set on the porch table, and for one golden hour it sat in the warm summer light, the cucumber slices cool and refreshing, the tomatoes vivid and sweet, the dill nodding in the soft afternoon air.

The family came out in their summer clothes and sat around the table. Children reached into the bowl with small hands. Someone laughed. A glass of cold water was poured. A bee considered joining the party and thought better of it.

[softly] By the time the bowl was empty, the afternoon had stretched long and easy toward evening, and Cleo felt — with the particular satisfaction of a cool cucumber in summer — that this had been exactly the right kind of day.
