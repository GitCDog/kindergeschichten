The rolling hills at the edge of Food Land were nothing like the flat garden beds that Lola the brave lettuce wrap had grown up in. They curved and dipped in every direction, and the paths between them went up and over and around in ways that were difficult to predict but turned out, once you were actually on them, to be rather logical. [long pause]

Lola had arrived in Food Land on a foggy morning with a packed lunch and no specific destination, which was her preferred way of traveling.

The first hill she climbed was gentle. The second was steeper. The third was the kind of hill that makes you stop at the top purely out of the physical necessity of breathing, and then you look around and find that stopping was the right thing to do, because the view from the top of a hill you've worked to climb is always better than the view from the bottom. [sighs]

Below her, Food Land spread out in all its variety: orchards in the valley, grain fields on the next slope, a river that caught the fog and held it low along its banks. The rolling hills went on in every direction, each one a slightly different shade of green. [beat]

She ate half her packed lunch at the top of the third hill, sitting on a grass-covered rock, watching a pair of birds work their way up the thermal above the next ridge.

The afternoon she spent walking the paths between the rolling hills, which wound through them rather than over them, and which were entirely different from the ridge-top paths: shaded, cooler, with the hills rising on either side like walls, and small unexpected features — a spring, a grove of old trees, a stone bridge over a stream she hadn't known was there. [softly]

Food Land kept surprising her. This was the thing about rolling hills, she decided — their shape meant you could never see everything at once. There was always more on the other side of the next one, and the exploring never felt complete, which was not a frustrating feeling but rather a promising one. [with growing realization]

She camped at the edge of a field as the fog came back in the evening, wrapping itself around the rolling hills with the comfortable certainty of something that knows it belongs here.

Food Land settled in around her, full and various and impossibly worth exploring.

[pause] Good night my little friend
