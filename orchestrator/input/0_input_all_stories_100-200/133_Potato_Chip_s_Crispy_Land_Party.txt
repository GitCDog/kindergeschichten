The great party of Crispy Land had been scheduled for three days, which in Crispy Land terms was an enormous amount of planning time. [long pause]

Crispy Land occupied the warmest corner of the kitchen counter, where the afternoon sun came through the window and made everything on the counter a little drier and more golden than it needed to be. It was here that a playful potato chip named Chip had decided to throw the finest snack friends party the counter had ever seen.

The guest list was the envy of the kitchen.

There was a pretzel, slightly salty, with a philosophical bent. There were three crackers of different sizes who always arrived as a set and spoke in unison, which made conversation interesting. There was a single rice cake who said very little but took up a reasonable amount of space and was universally liked. [sighs]

And there were the snack friends — a varied and delightful group of light crispy things who knew how to have a good time.

Chip had organized three events for the party.

The first was the crunch-off, which was exactly what it sounded like: a competition for the most satisfying crunch sound, judged by the pretzel, who had excellent ears. The crackers won, but only technically, as they had an unfair acoustic advantage from their geometric design. [beat]

The second event was the stack challenge: building the tallest stable tower using only snack friends, without leaning against anything. This ended at four layers when the pretzel attempted to add himself and miscalculated his own density.

The third event was the talking competition, which was really just everyone being at a party and talking, but Chip had given it a name to make it feel official. This was the best event. [softly]

They talked about flavor. They talked about crispiness and what made one thing crispier than another. They talked about the nature of Crispy Land and whether it was a place or a feeling. The pretzel said it was clearly a state of mind. The rice cake nodded thoughtfully and said nothing. [with growing realization]

The afternoon sun moved off the counter as evening came, and the party wound down in the gentle way of good parties, gradually and without anyone quite deciding it was over.

Chip sat on the corner of the counter afterwards, in the last of the warm light, surrounded by the pleasant crumb-evidence of a party well attended.

[pause] Good night my little friend
