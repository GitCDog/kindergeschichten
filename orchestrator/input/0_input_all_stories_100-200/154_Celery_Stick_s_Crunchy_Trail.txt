Nobody in the snack basket had ever walked as far as Cedric the celery stick intended to walk on that fine crisp morning. [pause] He had a snack trail to follow — a winding path of crumbs and seeds and colorful bits of vegetables that someone (he suspected the carrot, who was always organizing things) had laid out through the whole kitchen.

Cedric loved a good crunch path. He loved the satisfying sound of his own steps on the cool tile, the way the light caught the grooves in his pale green surface, and the particular feeling of being both useful and adventurous at the same time.

He started at the peanut butter jar and followed the trail around the fruit bowl, past the bread board, and along the edge of the window where the sunlight came in warm and bright. [softly] At each stopping point, there was something wonderful — a small dried raisin, a sunflower seed, a tiny square of cheddar.

He was not alone on the trail. A small cauliflower floret named Cleo was following at a polite distance, her little head bobbing with each careful step.

"What are you looking for?" she called out.

"I'm not looking for anything," said Cedric. "I'm just following the crunch path to see where it goes."

[pause] This seemed like such an excellent reason that Cleo immediately joined him, and they walked side by side through the kitchen and out to the back step, where the snack trail ended at a small round plate left in the warm afternoon sun.

On the plate was a note (written in crumbs, very cleverly): "You made it! Enjoy the view."

They sat on the back step and looked out at the garden. A dog napped in a patch of sun. A bird sang somewhere in the high branches. The afternoon was golden and quiet and completely, perfectly still.

[softly] Cedric had set out looking for a crunch path, and instead he had found the best kind of adventure — the kind that ends somewhere warm and beautiful, with a new friend beside you and nowhere you need to be.
