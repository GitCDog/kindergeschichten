The old red pot had been simmering all afternoon, and by evening the kitchen was wrapped in the most wonderful smell — tomato soup, deep and warm and cozy, ready for the cold night outside. [pause] Inside the pot, a happy tomato soup named Tara swirled contentedly, having spent a long, beautiful day becoming exactly herself.

She had begun as a collection of separate things — tomatoes and onions and a clove of garlic, a sprig of thyme and a splash of something golden. But hours of gentle heat had changed everything. The separate things had dissolved and merged and deepened until there was only one unified, perfectly harmonious soup, rich and red and full of warmth.

"I used to be so many different things," Tara thought, swirling slowly beneath the ladle. "Now I am just one very good thing."

Outside the kitchen window, a cold wind was moving through the dark streets. Inside, the lights were low and warm, and a child had pulled a chair close to the table where the soup bowls were waiting. [softly] The child's hair was still damp from a bath, and she wore pajamas patterned with small moons.

When Tara was ladled into the bowl, she spread herself wide and settled into a perfect, steaming circle of warmth. The child leaned close and breathed in.

"It smells like a cozy night," the child said.

[pause] Tara thought this was exactly right. She was a cozy night made into food — all the warmth and softness and slow, comfortable settling of an evening with nowhere to go and nothing to do but be together in a warm kitchen with soup.

The first spoonful was very quiet. Then the child said, "Can I have more?" and the cook smiled and said yes, and Tara heard the ladle dip again in the pot behind her.

[softly] Outside the wind went on. Inside, the warm bowl emptied slowly, and the kitchen was cozy, and the small moony pajamas glowed in the low light, and the tomato soup had done exactly what it was made to do.
