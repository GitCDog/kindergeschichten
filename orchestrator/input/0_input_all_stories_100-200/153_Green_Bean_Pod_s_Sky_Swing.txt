High above the vegetable garden, a playful green bean pod named Benny had discovered the greatest secret of the climbing plants: the sky swing. [pause] It wasn't a real swing, exactly. It was the way the wind caught the long, curling tendrils of the bean vine and set them swaying in wide, looping arcs — high enough that Benny could see all the way to the edge of the field.

He had found it by accident on a windy afternoon, when he had been playing on the upper vines and felt the whole plant lift and sway in a great whoooosh of summer air.

"I'm flying!" he had shouted, though no one below could hear him.

Now he went up every afternoon to ride the sky swing. He had told his friends, but most of them were too comfortable on the lower vines to bother climbing. All except for Grace, a slender bean pod who had a natural talent for heights. [softly] She had spotted Benny swinging one day and immediately started climbing.

"How do you do it?" she called up to him.

"Just hold on and trust the vine!" Benny called back.

She reached the top, wrapped her thin stems around the highest tendril, and the wind came — just in time. The vine arced upward and outward in a long, swooping sky play that made her gasp. Then laugh. Then whoop with pure, uncontrollable joy.

[pause] By the end of the week, the high vine had become the gathering spot for all the bravest bean pods in the garden. They swung in turns, sometimes two at a time, sometimes in a long chain of green, swaying like a living garland against the blue summer sky.

The tomatoes watched from below with mild interest. The sunflowers nodded approvingly. The old squash plant said nothing, but her leaves rustled in a way that might have been a smile.

[softly] When evening came and the wind died down, Benny settled in the highest crook of the vine and looked out at the garden below. Everything looked smaller from up here, and quieter, and terribly, beautifully peaceful.

He closed his eyes as the vine swayed gently in the last breath of the summer breeze.
