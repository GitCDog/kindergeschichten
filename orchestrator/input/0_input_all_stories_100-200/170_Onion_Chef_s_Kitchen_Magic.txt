Chef Obi the onion had one rule in his kitchen: no crying allowed. [pause] This was, as everyone agreed, an extraordinary rule for an onion to make, given that onions were the primary cause of kitchen tears. But Chef Obi had spent years mastering his tearless magic, and he was very proud of it.

The secret, he had discovered, was warmth. When you kept your onion very cold before cutting, and your knife very sharp, and your movements very swift — the tears never came. The kitchen fun remained just that: fun.

He had shared this technique with every cook who had ever worked in his kitchen, and they had all been amazed. [softly] It was such a small thing, this tearless magic, and yet it changed the whole experience of cooking. Instead of the cook leaning away with streaming eyes, they leaned in. They watched the onion rings soften and caramelize. They smelled the deep, sweet transformation. They stayed present for the whole beautiful process.

Chef Obi loved the kitchen most when it was like this — everyone engaged, everything working, the smell of softening onions rising warm and sweet and golden.

[pause] "You are the most remarkable vegetable in the kitchen," said a young leek who had just arrived for his first shift.

"Every vegetable is remarkable," said Chef Obi, without looking up from his chopping. "Onions are simply misunderstood. People think we are for crying. We are actually for sweetness." He gestured at the golden, caramelized rings in the pan. "Everything difficult becomes sweet eventually, if you give it enough warmth and time."

The young leek stood very still, thinking about this.

Chef Obi smiled and added another handful of rings to the pan. The kitchen filled with a warm, sweet, onion-golden smell.

[softly] And not one person in that kitchen was crying — because the tearless magic was working perfectly, and the kitchen was full of nothing but warmth and the very best kind of fun.
