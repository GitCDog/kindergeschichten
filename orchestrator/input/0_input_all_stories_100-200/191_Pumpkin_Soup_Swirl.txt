[pause] Petra the pumpkin soup was the color of a deep sunset — rich orange-amber, impossibly smooth, with a swirl of cream spiraled across the top and a sprinkle of toasted pumpkin seeds that rattled gently when the bowl was set down.

She had been roasted first, which was the secret. Not boiled — roasted, until the flesh caramelized and concentrated and the whole kitchen filled with that particular autumn bowl smell that was warm and sweet and slightly nutty. Then blended, then simmered with stock and spices until she became this: a deep, perfect, autumn-orange swirl.

The cream on top was cold. The soup beneath was hot. When the first spoon moved through, the two combined into something new — swirled together in a small spiral, pale cream and deep orange moving against each other in the most beautiful way. A child at the table asked permission to make the swirl herself.
