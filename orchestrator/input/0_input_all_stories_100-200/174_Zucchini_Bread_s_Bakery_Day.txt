The smell of zucchini bread fresh from the oven was different from other smells. It was warm, obviously, but there was something else in it — something green and fresh hiding underneath the sweetness, like a garden tucked inside a dessert.

"That's me," thought Ziggy proudly. "That's the zucchini part."

She sat on the cooling rack while the bakery town woke up around her. The croissants were piled in their basket, buttery and flaky. The sourdough loaves stood in tidy rows, serious and dignified. The cinnamon rolls spiraled magnificently in their pan, glistening with icing. [softly] Everyone smelled wonderful, but Ziggy felt that she smelled best — not because she was prouder than the others, but because she was also secretly a vegetable, which gave her a particular depth no other baked good could claim.
