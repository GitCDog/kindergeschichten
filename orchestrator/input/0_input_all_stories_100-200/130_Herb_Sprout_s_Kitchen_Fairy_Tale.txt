The kitchen fairy arrived, as kitchen fairies always do, on the smell of something good. [long pause]

A tiny herb sprout named Sage was the first to notice her. Sage was growing in a small pot on the kitchen windowsill, and from that position could see most of what happened in the kitchen — the morning coffee, the afternoon baking, the evening simmering of things that took time and patience. The kitchen fairy arrived between lunch and dinner, on a Thursday, carried in on a wave of aroma magic that rolled off the pot of tomato soup on the stove.

She was small and smelled strongly of rosemary, which Sage found reassuring.

"I heard there were herbs here," she said, settling onto the edge of Sage's pot and looking around the kitchen with the attentive interest of a professional. [sighs]

"There are several of us," said Sage.

"Good. Good kitchens need them." She nodded at the basil on the far end of the windowsill, the thyme in its narrow pot beside the stove, the small bunch of dried lavender hanging from the cabinet. "Each one changes the kitchen's aroma magic. You know that, yes?" [beat]

Sage had known this in a general way but hadn't thought about it specifically.

"The basil makes people happy," the kitchen fairy continued. "The thyme makes them feel like things are under control. The lavender, which strictly speaking isn't edible and is there for decoration, still affects the room — it makes people slow down a little."

"And me?" said Sage.

The kitchen fairy looked at Sage the way someone looks at a thing they find genuinely pleasing.

"You make people feel wise," she said. "Even when they're not. You make them feel like they knew all along what they were doing." [softly]

Sage thought about this for the rest of the afternoon, which was mostly quiet. The soup simmered. The kitchen smelled of tomatoes and herbs. The fairy had gone, but her visit left something behind in the air — a heightened awareness of smell, of warmth, of the small useful magic that happens every evening in a kitchen where someone is cooking carefully. [with growing realization]

Outside the window the evening came on, and the first lamps of the street lit up, and the kitchen became the warmest room in the house.

Sage stood on the windowsill in the aroma magic and grew just a little, reaching toward the light.

[pause] Good night my little friend
