The cool pond was at the far end of the garden, behind the bean rows and the old willow, and on hot summer days it was the most pleasant place in the whole garden to be. [long pause]

A cheerful cucumber slice named Cleo had discovered it on a Tuesday in late July and had been returning every chance she got.

The cool pond was perfectly round, fed by a small spring that kept its temperature steady even on the hottest afternoons. Water reeds grew at its edges. A family of water beetles had established a complex and apparently organized society along the eastern bank. At the center, the water was so still on windless days that it reflected the sky exactly, and you could lie on the bank and look at the reflected clouds moving through it and feel like the whole world had turned pleasantly upside down. [sighs]

Cleo had a favorite spot — a flat stone jutting over the water's edge that caught the morning shadow and stayed cool all day. She would arrange herself there after her mornings in the heat of the cucumber patch and feel the coolness come up through the stone into her, steady and reliable.

Water fun, she had discovered, did not require doing very much. [beat]

Sometimes she watched the water beetles. They moved with surprising speed and intention, their legs making tiny dimples on the surface of the cool pond that spread out in perfect rings and then disappeared. She had once spent an entire afternoon counting how long the rings lasted — three seconds, usually, sometimes four if the air was very still. [softly]

Sometimes she floated the idea of getting in, and then didn't, and was content with the idea.

Sometimes she just sat on the flat stone with the cool seeping up through her and the reflected sky moving in the pond and the dragonflies making their impossible turns above the water, and felt that this — this specific arrangement of elements — was a kind of water fun that didn't need to be explained. [with growing realization]

The cool pond held its stillness all day and into the evening, when the first bats came out and skimmed the surface for insects, and the sky it reflected turned pink and then orange and then the deep blue of almost-dark.

Cleo stayed until the stars appeared in the water below her, and then she went home through the cooling garden, feeling thoroughly and properly cool.
