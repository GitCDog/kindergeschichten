Every spring, the garden held a contest called the Tall Jump — and every spring, Arnie the asparagus tip entered it with high hopes and a very springy stem. [pause] Asparagus plants were famous for their leaping ability, but Arnie's particular style of jumping — a sort of spiraling, twisting, upward rocket — was admired throughout the whole garden.

The problem was the spring wind.

The spring wind was wild and unpredictable, and it came whooshing through the garden at the worst possible moments — usually right when someone was at the top of their jump. Last year it had sent Arnie sailing sideways into the bean trellis. The year before, it had spun him backward into the compost pile.

This year, Arnie had a plan. [softly] Instead of fighting the spring wind, he would use it.

He spent two weeks watching the wind move through the garden. He learned which direction it came from in the morning, how it shifted by midday, how it calmed in the late afternoon and then kicked up again just before sunset. He listened to the way it moved through the tall grass and bent the flower stems.

On the day of the Tall Jump, Arnie stood very still and waited. The other contestants jumped when the judge called them. Some did very well. [pause] But when Arnie's turn came, a strong spring wind began to build from the west.

Everyone expected him to wait for it to pass. But instead, he bent his stem like a bow, counted three heartbeats, and then — at the exact moment the wind swept through — he leaped.

The wind caught him. Instead of throwing him sideways, it lifted him. He rode it like a wave, spiraling upward in a long, tall arc that drew gasps from every corner of the garden.

When he came down, gently, into the soft earth, the whole garden was silent for one beautiful second.

Then erupted into cheering.

[softly] Arnie stood up straight, dusted off his tip, and smiled up at the sky — where the spring wind was already moving on to its next adventure, having given him exactly the gift he needed.
