On the morning that Kira the kale leaf decided to go on a healthy quest, the super garden was still covered in a silver layer of dew that made everything look freshly polished. [long pause]

Kira had made a list. This was the kind of thing she did — the little kale leaf was methodical, and she believed that the best adventures were the ones with some structure behind them.

The list had five items on it:
Find the strongest thing in the garden.
Find the most colorful thing.
Find the thing that had been there the longest.
Find the thing that had been there the shortest.
And then find the one thing that connected all of these. [sighs]

She started at sunrise.

The strongest thing, she decided after an hour of careful assessment, was the old apple tree at the garden's corner, which had been there so long that its roots had lifted the stones of the garden path in several places. It simply stood and grew and produced apples regardless of what the weather thought about this plan. [beat]

The most colorful was a row of Swiss chard whose leaves ranged from deep red to electric yellow to a green so vivid it almost seemed to hum. Kira stood in front of it for a while, just looking.

The oldest was a rosemary bush that everyone in the garden agreed had been there longer than memory. It smelled sharply of everything and nothing had ever managed to kill it.

The newest was a small bean sprout that had come up only yesterday, pale and uncertain, holding its first two leaves out to the air as if testing the temperature.

Kira sat in the middle of the super garden and looked at her list and thought about the connecting thing. [softly]

The apple tree grew. The chard grew. The rosemary grew. The bean sprout was growing right now.

The one thing that connected all of them — the strongest, the most colorful, the oldest, and the newest — was growth. The patient, persistent, unassuming act of growing. [with growing realization]

She added a sixth item to her list: Be part of this.

Then she put the list away and stood in the morning sun with her arms spread wide, doing exactly that.

The super garden hummed quietly around her, full of everything growing in every direction.
