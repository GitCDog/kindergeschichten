The invitation had arrived at noon: the royal feast would be held that evening, and the eggplant dish had been chosen to sit at the center of the table. [pause] This was not a small honor. The center of the feast table was the position of greatest distinction, surrounded by all the other magnificent dishes of the evening.

Eddie the eggplant dish had spent the afternoon being prepared with great care. He had been sliced and roasted and layered with the richest, most fragrant ingredients the kitchen had to offer — garlic and herbs and cheese and a deep red sauce that had been simmering for hours.

When he was finally carried to the table and set in his place of honor, he looked out at the assembled feast and felt something very close to royal himself. [softly] On his left, a golden roasted chicken gleamed. On his right, a bowl of herbed potatoes steamed contentedly. And around the edges of the table, salads and breads and little dishes of beautiful things sparkled in the candlelight.

"Are you ready?" whispered the chicken.

"More than ready," said Eddie quietly.

The family arrived and took their seats. The feast time began. Spoons and serving forks moved in the warm light, and one by one the dishes contributed their best to the great meal. [pause] When the spoon finally dipped into Eddie, he felt the heat rise around him and the cheese pull apart in long, fragrant strings, and something in him swelled with pride and satisfaction.

The woman at the head of the table took the first bite and closed her eyes. "This is extraordinary," she said.

Eddie couldn't have agreed more.

[softly] By the end of the royal feast, the dishes had given everything they had, and the table was quiet and warm and full of happy people who had eaten well. Eddie sat empty but deeply contented at the center of it all, having been exactly what a royal feast needs: something rich and generous and completely, wonderfully itself.
