The windy hill behind the garden was not a place most vegetables went by choice. It was exposed and gusty and the grass there was always being pushed sideways in a way that suggested the wind had somewhere else to be and was not inclined to slow down. [long pause]

This was precisely why a curious carrot named Cato went there every chance he got.

He had his first leafy kite on a Wednesday in early autumn, made from a large plane-tree leaf attached to a length of garden twine that he'd borrowed without asking and fully intended to return. It was not an aeronautical masterpiece. But the windy hill was forgiving. [sighs]

The kite went up.

Not gracefully at first — it spun and dipped and twice folded itself in a way that suggested it had reconsidered the whole enterprise — but the wind on the windy hill was strong and consistent and eventually the leafy kite caught it properly and went up, tugging at the twine, pulling Cato's arms upward with a feeling that was part effort and part flying. [beat]

He ran with it.

Down the slope of the windy hill and up again, the leafy kite streaming behind him, the wind in his carrot top, his feet finding the grass with the random certainty of someone moving too fast to think but too happy to care. The hill rolled away on all sides, and the sky above was that particular autumn blue that has almost no clouds and goes on forever.

He ran until his legs wanted to stop, and then he walked, letting the kite do its work above him. [softly]

Below, through the gap in the hill, he could see the garden — the neat rows of vegetables, the paths, the gate with its paint fading in the autumn light. It looked quiet and orderly from up here, which it mostly was, and beautiful in the way that familiar things become beautiful when seen from a new angle. [with growing realization]

He stayed on the windy hill until the light began to go golden, then walked back down the slope to the garden with the leafy kite tucked under his arm, his carrot top wind-tousled and his face warm from the afternoon.

The hill stayed windy behind him all the way down, blowing toward somewhere he hadn't yet been.

[pause] Good night my little friend
