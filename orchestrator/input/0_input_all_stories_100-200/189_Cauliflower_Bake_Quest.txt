The cheesy top was what everyone noticed first, but inside, Carlton the cauliflower bake knew, was where the real adventure lived. [pause] He had been assembled with great deliberation — fat florets of cauliflower laid in a wide dish, covered in a cheese sauce of startling depth and richness, then dotted with breadcrumbs and more cheese and sent into the oven for his transformation.

The oven quest was what Carlton thought of it as. Forty-five minutes at high heat, during which everything changed.

The cauliflower — pale and firm when it went in — became soft and tender, yielding to a spoon but not collapsing. The cheese sauce darkened at the edges into something deep and nutty. The breadcrumbs on top absorbed the butter and crisped to gold. And on the very top, an extraordinary thing happened: a crust formed — bubbly and bronzed and crackled, the most magnificent cheesy top Carlton had ever produced. [softly] He had outdone himself.

When the oven door opened, steam poured out. The family in the kitchen inhaled in unison.

"That is extraordinary," said someone.

Carlton agreed, without any embarrassment.

[pause] The first spoonful went deep — through the golden cheesy crust, through the rich sauce, down to the tender cauliflower beneath. When it came back up, it was layered: crust and cheese and floret all at once, every element transformed from its raw state into something completely unified and magnificent.

The child who received the first serving stared at it for a moment, surprised that cauliflower — cauliflower! — could look and smell like this.

[softly] Carlton waited.

The child ate.

Their eyes closed for exactly one second.

Carlton had completed his oven quest, and he had done it beautifully — turning something humble into something extraordinary, one golden, cheesy, gloriously crustled bake at a time.
