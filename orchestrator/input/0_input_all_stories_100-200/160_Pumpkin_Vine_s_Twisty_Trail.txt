Nobody in the garden could twist and turn the way Vinnie the pumpkin vine could. [pause] While the other plants grew mostly straight — the beans up their poles, the carrots down in tidy rows — Vinnie spiraled and looped and doubled back on himself in the most wonderfully unpredictable way imaginable.

"You should really try to grow more neatly," said the trellis one morning.

"I am growing perfectly neatly," said Vinnie cheerfully. "Neatly for me."

His twisty trail had become something of a landmark. Garden visitors always stopped to admire the way his tendrils curled around fence posts and flowerpot edges and even — once, famously — around the leg of the old garden chair, which he had claimed with great ceremony. [softly] The growth secret, Vinnie knew, was not trying to be straight. It was paying attention to what you found as you grew.

Every twist in his vine had a reason. Here, he had turned to find better sunlight. There, he had curved around a cluster of stones to reach the moister soil beyond. At that great looping section near the compost heap, he had grown in a wide circle simply because the smells were extraordinary and he hadn't wanted to leave.

[pause] A young bean seedling watched him one morning with something between admiration and confusion.

"Don't you ever get lost?" the seedling asked.

"Constantly," said Vinnie happily. "That is the whole point."

The seedling thought about this for a very long time.

"What if you grow somewhere and then can't find your way back?"

"Back to where?" said Vinnie. "Wherever I am is exactly where I'm supposed to be. I just keep going." He paused and looked down the length of his long, looping, magnificently twisty self. "And look what I found along the way."

He was right. All along his trail, small flowers had bloomed. Beetles had made homes in his curled tendrils. Three young pumpkins hung in his curves like orange lanterns. [softly] His twisty trail had become the most interesting, most lively, most perfectly inhabited vine in the whole garden.

The trellis said nothing more about neatness.
