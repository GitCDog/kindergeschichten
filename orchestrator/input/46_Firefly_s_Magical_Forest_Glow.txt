Once upon a time, in a forest where the nights were very dark and very quiet and the fireflies were the only lights that moved, there lived a tiny firefly named Flick. [long pause]
Flick had wings like pressed glass and a lantern in his tail that glowed a warm, steady gold. He was one of hundreds of fireflies in the forest, and every evening they rose from the long grass like a slow explosion of sparks, filling the dark spaces between the trees with floating light. [sighs with quiet wonder]
But Flick had a dream that the other fireflies thought was slightly unusual. [beat]
He wanted to build something with his light. Not just float and glow — really build something. A forest glow that stayed in one place, that others could find, that meant: here, in the dark, something beautiful is waiting.
He chose the old hollow oak at the center of the forest. Three nights he worked — circling it slowly, landing on the bark, lifting off again — tracing the same path over and over until the oak seemed to hold a memory of his light. [softly]
On the fourth night, he asked the others to come.
[with growing realization] Forty-seven fireflies landed on the hollow oak — on the bark, in the opening of the hollow, along the great roots at the base — and lit up together. Not floating, not drifting. Still.
The forest glowed.
A warm, golden, breathing glow that reached the path twenty meters away and lit it clearly. A moth found her way home by it. A lost hedgehog followed it back to familiar ground. An owl, hunting in silence, paused on a branch and looked at it with what appeared to be appreciation. [pacing]
Flick hovered before the glowing oak and felt his own small lantern burning more brightly than usual.
[softly] Any light matters, he thought. But a light that helps someone find their way — that is the finest kind of all.
The fireflies glowed on until dawn, and the forest was not dark that night.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
