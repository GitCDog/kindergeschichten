Once upon a time, in the deep and midnight-blue part of the ocean where the light from the surface barely reached, there lived a jolly jellyfish named Juno. [long pause]
Juno was round as a moon — a pale, luminous, pulsing moon — with sixteen tentacles that trailed below her like the long fronds of a living chandelier. She glowed. Not brightly — not in a way that announced itself — but steadily, in the soft way of something that has its own light source and has made peace with that. [sighs with oceanic contentment]
What Juno loved most was dancing. [beat]
Not that she moved fast. Jellyfish don't do fast. But the pulse — the rhythmic open-and-close of her bell, the drift and sway of her tentacles — this Juno had refined into something so beautiful that other deep-sea creatures would slow their own journeys just to pass through her vicinity.
She had discovered, over many years, that her tentacles did something remarkable in motion. [softly]
When she danced — when she pulsed in long, slow, deliberate rhythms rather than her usual efficient swimming pulse — each tentacle left a faint trail of light in the dark water. Ocean glow, the deep-sea creatures called it. Bioluminescence, said the scientists, but Juno didn't know that word and didn't need it. She just knew that when she moved with intention, the water lit up behind her like she was writing in light. [with growing realization]
She began each night's tentacle dance at the hour when the water above her darkened completely and the deep was at its most absolute.
She pulsed. She turned. Her sixteen tentacles wove through the water in long, looping curves. [pacing] The ocean glow spread — green-blue, then gold, then a blue so pale it was almost white — flowering outward from her dance in patterns that disappeared slowly, like breath on cold air.
Fish came to watch. A deep-sea lanternfish hovered at the edge of the light, adding its own small glow. An anglerfish turned off its terrible lure and watched, for once, in simple appreciation.
[softly] Juno danced until the light above her began to return. Then she folded her tentacles gently and drifted up through the water, carrying her glow with her, all the way home to the warm surface world.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
