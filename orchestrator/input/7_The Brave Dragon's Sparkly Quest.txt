Once upon a time, deep inside a magical forest where the trees whispered secrets and the mushrooms glowed softly at night, there lived a brave little dragon named Ember. [long pause] Ember was only as tall as a garden fence — quite small, as dragons go. His wings were still a little wrinkly, and when he tried to breathe fire, it came out more like a warm sneeze. But his heart was as big as a mountain, and everyone in the magical forest knew it. [beat] One morning, the forest creatures came running. A bluebird arrived first, wings flapping in a panic. Then a family of rabbits. Then a very out-of-breath hedgehog. "The shiny treasure is gone!" they cried. "The crystal that keeps the forest's magic alive — it's disappeared! Without it, everything will start to fade!" Ember puffed up his small chest. "I'll find it," he said. [softly] Some of the larger animals exchanged glances. He was very small, after all. But Ember had already turned and walked bravely into the deep of the magical forest. He searched through tunnels of twisted roots that smelled of old earth and adventure. He crossed bridges made of vines that swayed over rushing streams. He squeezed past waterfalls that sparkled like handfuls of stars. [pacing] Everywhere he went, the forest seemed to hum quietly around him, as if cheering him on. And then, at the very heart of the forest — in the oldest, quietest clearing of all — he found it. The shiny treasure, a crystal as large as his head, tangled deep inside a rosebush that had grown around it while everyone was sleeping. [with growing realization] Ember thought carefully. His claws were too big. His wings would knock everything over. [beat] But his breath — his small, warm, gentle breath — that was just right. He leaned in close to the thorns and breathed — just a little flame, no bigger than a candle — warming the branches until they uncurled slowly, like fingers opening. One by one the thorns pulled back. And Ember lifted the shiny treasure free. The whole magical forest seemed to exhale at once. Flowers bloomed in seconds. Birds sang as if they had been holding their breath for days. The trees swayed their great branches like arms raised in celebration. [softly] "Our brave little dragon!" the creatures cheered. Ember blushed so hard that small sparks flew from his nose. "It wasn't so hard," he said modestly. But everyone there knew the truth. Being brave doesn't mean being the biggest. It means trying anyway. The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
