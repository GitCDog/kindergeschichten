Once upon a time, in the hollow of a very old oak tree at the edge of a sleeping town, there lived a smart owl named Oliver. [long pause]
Oliver wore a pair of small round spectacles and kept his feathers extremely neat. He was the keeper of the night library — a remarkable collection of books tucked into every nook and cranny of the old oak, their spines glowing faintly in the darkness so you could always find what you were looking for. [sighs contentedly]
The night library was only open between midnight and dawn. Those were the quiet hours, Oliver maintained, when the best reading happened. [beat]
Every night, creatures from all across the wood came to visit. The fox borrowed adventure stories. The hedgehog liked poetry. The family of field mice had a standing reservation for the picture books in the lower hollow. [softly]
But the star books were different.
The star books were kept in the very highest branch of the oak — a special shelf that caught the moonlight — and they were the rarest books in the collection. Each one was written on pages made of pressed starlight, and each one contained the true story of a different constellation. [with growing realization] No one had ever checked them out, because everyone assumed they were too precious to touch.
One night, a small hedgehog named Nell climbed all the way to the top branch and stood before the star books with her paws clasped together. "Please," she said to Oliver. "May I read one? Just for one night?"
Oliver adjusted his spectacles. He looked at Nell. He looked at the star books. [long pause]
"Books," he said finally, "are for reading. That is their entire purpose."
He lifted down the nearest star book carefully — the story of the Great Bear — and placed it in Nell's waiting paws.
[pacing] She read it all night, sitting in the highest branch, the real stars moving slowly overhead. When the dawn began to come, she closed the book gently and looked up at the sky.
The Great Bear constellation shimmered once — as if it knew it had been understood.
[softly] Oliver smiled from behind his spectacles. Knowledge, he had always believed, grows when it is shared.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
