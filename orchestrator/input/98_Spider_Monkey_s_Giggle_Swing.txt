Once upon a time, in a jungle so tall that the canopy felt like a second sky, there lived a silly spider monkey named Spin. [long pause]
Spin had a tail so strong and so long and so capable that it functioned as a fifth limb, and he used all five of his limbs constantly, in all directions, at the same time. He moved through the canopy the way a thought moves through a mind: quickly, freely, apparently without plan but always ending up exactly where he meant to be. [sighs with breezy confidence]
He had a swinging tail — not the tail itself, which was attached to him, but a vine he had claimed at the highest point of the tallest cecropia tree — and he had perfected what he called the Giggle Swing. [beat]
The Giggle Swing was a technique, not just a vine. You grabbed the vine. You ran three steps along the branch. You launched — not just with your hands but with your tail wrapped around the vine too, your whole long spider monkey self extended, and you swung in the widest arc the canopy allowed, out over the open air where you could see the whole jungle below, and at the apex — [softly]
At the apex, you let go of everything except the joy of it.
The giggle was involuntary. Every creature who tried the Giggle Swing made the sound. The jungle giggle — a helpless, surprised, delighted sound that rose up through the canopy and made the birds look up and the sloths open their eyes. [with growing realization]
Spin had taught half the jungle to do it.
He taught the small howler monkey who was afraid of heights. (She giggled so hard she forgot to be afraid.) He taught the toucan, who substituted a laugh for a giggle but the principle was the same. He taught three younger spider monkeys who already had the swing but not the letting-go. [pacing]
"The letting-go is the whole thing," Spin told them. "The swing is just getting you there."
[softly] He demonstrated once more — five limbs, one vine, the wide arc, the open air, the apex — and the jungle giggle came up out of him like it always did, surprised and whole and impossible to hold back.
Below, a dozen creatures in the canopy made the sound along with him without quite meaning to.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
