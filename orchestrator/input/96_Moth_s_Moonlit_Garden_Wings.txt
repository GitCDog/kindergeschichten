Once upon a time, in a garden that was beautiful by day but extraordinary by night, there lived a magic moth named Mira. [long pause]
Mira had wings the color of old parchment — a warm, dusty cream — with two eye-spots of deep brown and gold that looked, in certain lights, like something that had been watching the world for a very long time. She flew only after dark, which suited her perfectly. Darkness was where she came into her own. [sighs softly]
Her moonlight wings were her secret. [beat]
They looked ordinary enough in the lamplight — subtle, soft, the kind of wings you might overlook. But under moonlight — full moonlight, the real, direct, silver light of a clear night — they changed. The parchment color became luminous. The eye-spots brightened to gold. The dusty cream of her underwings caught the moonlight and held it and gave it back in a glow that was entirely her own. [softly]
Mira spent every moonlit night in the night garden.
She had mapped it completely — every night-blooming flower, every patch of pale moonflower, every white petunia that glowed like a small lamp in the dark. She knew which flowers opened only after midnight and which released their scent only in the last hours before dawn. [with growing realization]
She flew among them, her moonlight wings gathering the silver light, trailing it through the garden in long soft arcs.
Other night creatures watched. An owl from the fence post. A hedgehog from beneath the lavender. A fox who had stopped, unexpectedly, on the garden wall and forgotten to keep going. [pacing]
Mira didn't perform for them. She simply flew the way she always flew — fully, attentively, belonging entirely to the night and to the moonlit garden that was her home. But they watched anyway, because there are some things so entirely themselves that you can't look away.
[softly] When the moon went down and the garden darkened, Mira folded her wings and rested on the inside of a broad foxglove leaf. Her moonlight wings, ordinary again, tucked quietly around her.
She slept. And dreamed of silver and gardens and the particular peace of moving through the night with purpose.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
