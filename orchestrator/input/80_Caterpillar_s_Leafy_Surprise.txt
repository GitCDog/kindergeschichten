Once upon a time, in a garden hedge where the leaves grew thick and overlapping and the light came through in a thousand small green patches, there lived a curious caterpillar named Clem. [long pause]
Clem was green — the exact green of the leaves he lived on, which was either remarkable camouflage or a coincidence that Clem preferred to think of as fate. He had sixteen legs, excellent grip, and a curiosity that was, for a caterpillar, quite exceptional. He wanted to know what was on the other side of everything. [sighs with restless wonder]
The leaf tunnel had begun as an accident. [beat]
He had been eating — as caterpillars do, methodically, working across a large ivy leaf — when he noticed that the leaf overlapped with the next one, and that one with the next, and that if he kept going, following the overlap, he was moving through a tunnel of layered leaves, green light filtering in from above and below, the whole world reduced to this one long green corridor. [softly]
He kept going.
The leaf tunnel went further than he had expected. Around the curve of the hedge. Up through a tangle of stems. Down the other side where the ivy met the wall and the leaves grew darker and larger. [with growing realization]
And then, at the end — light. Not green leaf-light but open, golden, full afternoon light flooding through a gap in the hedge.
Clem pushed through. [pacing]
He came out on the other side of the hedge into a garden he had never seen. Completely different from his own — wider, with a pond and a fruit tree and the smell of something warm and flowering that he didn't have a name for. A blackbird sat on the edge of the pond, looked at him, and went back to its bath without concern.
Clem sat at the end of his leaf tunnel and looked at the new garden for a long time. [softly]
Then he turned around and went back through to tell the others.
Adventure, he thought, is usually one leaf-length further than you thought you could go.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
