Once upon a time, at the base of a great oak tree where the leaf litter was deep and the light came down in long afternoon shafts, there lived a brave beetle named Bruno. [long pause]
Bruno had a shell — shiny armor, he called it — that was deep green-black and caught the light like polished metal. He was smaller than most creatures he encountered, which he had long since decided was irrelevant. What mattered was not size but courage and persistence, and Bruno had both in considerable supply. [sighs with dignity]
He had appointed himself knight of the oak tree. [beat]
No one had asked him to do this. It had simply seemed necessary. The oak tree was ancient and important and had no one specifically looking after its interests at ground level — and ground level, Bruno had found, was where most of the interesting and important things happened.
His duties were considerable. He moved fallen bark that blocked the path between the roots. He escorted lost worms to better soil. He kept watch at the eastern root, where once a boot had nearly come down on a family of woodlice, and he intended never to let that happen on his watch again. [softly]
The great adventure came on an October morning when a leaf — one enormous oak leaf, dry and crackling and twice Bruno's size — blew across the clearing in a gust of wind and trapped a small beetle, even smaller than Bruno, underneath it.
Bruno ran. [with growing realization]
The wind was pulling the leaf around in circles. He ran underneath it into the moving dark and found the small beetle — frozen with confusion, legs in the air. Bruno grabbed one leg with his strong jaw. He pulled. The leaf lifted in another gust. Bruno ran with the small beetle into the clear, and they both tumbled into the deep leaf litter and lay there, catching their breath. [pacing]
The small beetle looked up at Bruno's shiny armor with enormous eyes. "Who are you?"
Bruno straightened. He made sure his armor was presentable. [softly] "Knight of the oak tree," he said.
The small beetle considered this. "Can I be one too?"
Bruno looked at her. He thought about the eastern root, and the western side of the clearing that he had been meaning to patrol for weeks, and how much easier it would be with two.
"Yes," he said. "Come. I'll show you the territory."
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
