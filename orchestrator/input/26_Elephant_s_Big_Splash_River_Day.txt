Once upon a time, in a jungle so lush and green it seemed to hum with life, there lived a little elephant named Ellie. [long pause]
Ellie was the smallest elephant in her family — her ears were too big for her head and her trunk didn't always do what she told it to — but she had the most enormous, joyful spirit of anyone in the herd. And what she loved most in all the world was the jungle river. [sighs happily]
The jungle river was wide and brown and cool, winding through the trees with a cheerful burbling sound. Every afternoon, the herd walked down to the bank for a drink and a splash. [beat]
But today, Ellie had a plan. She had spent the whole morning practicing, and she was ready to show everyone the most spectacular splash the jungle river had ever seen.
She backed up. She took a running start — which, for a small elephant, involved a lot of ear-flapping. She galloped down the bank — [pacing] trunks up, ears out, all four legs going like drumsticks — and launched herself into the air.
[abruptly] She hit the water like a small, enthusiastic boulder.
The splash was enormous. Water flew up in a great shining curtain and caught the jungle light in a hundred tiny rainbows. It landed on the crocodiles on the far bank, who looked startled. It rained down on a family of monkeys in the trees, who whooped with surprise. It doused Ellie's grandmother, who had been standing a sensible distance away, from trunk to tail.
[long pause] There was a silence.
Then Ellie's grandmother — dripping, dignified, and extremely wet — began to laugh. A deep, rolling elephant laugh that shook the trees.
And then everyone was laughing. And then everyone was splashing. [with growing realization] Even the crocodiles eventually crept in, looking slightly embarrassed but undeniably pleased.
By the end of the afternoon the jungle river was a carnival of splashing fun, all because one small elephant had been brave enough to leap.
Ellie floated on her back and looked up at the sky and thought: sometimes the best thing you can do is jump in with everything you've got.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
