Once upon a time, in a jungle where the trees grew so tall that their tops disappeared into mist and the ground was always cool and green, there lived a gentle gorilla named George. [long pause]
George was very large — the largest creature in his family — with broad shoulders, kind eyes, and hands that could crack open a coconut as easily as you might open a book. He was quiet in the way that large gentle creatures often are, as though they have decided that the world is loud enough without their help. [sighs softly]
George loved two things above all: his family, and bananas. [beat]
The banana bunch at the heart of their territory grew thick and golden, and every morning George would find the ripest ones — the ones that had gone from green to yellow to the deep gold that meant perfect — and bring them back to where his family sat in the dappled morning light.
He didn't announce this. He just arrived with an armful and set them down, and the family ate, and that was that. [softly]
But today he found something unexpected when he reached the banana grove. Sitting in the middle of it, looking thoroughly lost and considerably smaller than everything around him, was a young monkey — not a gorilla, not his family, just a young vervet monkey who had clearly come a very long way from wherever he belonged.
The monkey looked up at George. George was very large. George looked down at the monkey. [with growing realization]
The monkey burst into tears.
George sat down — which took a moment, given his size — and was very still. The monkey cried. George waited. When the monkey had finished, George reached into the banana bunch and selected the most perfectly ripe one — deep gold, fragrant, completely soft — and held it out. [pacing]
The monkey took it. He ate it. He stopped crying.
George found the rest of the monkey's family an hour later — they had been looking everywhere — and walked the small one back to them. The family surrounded the young monkey with a great deal of noise and reassurance, the way families do.
[softly] George turned and walked back through the jungle to his own family, carrying the rest of the banana bunch. He set them down. He sat. His family leaned against him in the dappled light.
A jungle hug, he thought, doesn't always use arms.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
