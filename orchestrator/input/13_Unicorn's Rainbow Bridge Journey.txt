Once upon a time, high above the clouds where the air was soft and cool and smelled faintly of rain, there lived a magic unicorn named Iris. [long pause]
 
Her coat was pure white — the kind of white that seems to glow from the inside. Her horn shimmered with every color of the rainbow, shifting from rose to gold to deep violet depending on how the light fell. She lived in a cloud castle: a billowing, pillowy palace with towers of silver mist and floors as soft and yielding as freshly fallen snow. [softly]
 
Iris loved her cloud castle very much. But what she loved most was the rainbow bridge.
 
The rainbow bridge curved in a long, glowing arc from the base of her castle all the way down to the world below — striped in red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet. Her friends from the meadows and forests could walk up it any time they liked, and she could glide down it to visit them. It was the most beautiful thing she had ever seen. [beat]
 
One morning, Iris woke and looked out of her mist window — and stopped.
 
[with growing alarm] The rainbow bridge had gone gray. Every stripe, every color, every shimmer — gone. Just a pale, dull arc, like a bridge made of ash.
 
"What happened?" she whispered.
 
She went to the oldest cloud in the castle — a great, billowing cumulus named Nimbus, who had been there longer than anyone could remember.
 
[slowly] "The bridge needs a wish to light it again," Nimbus said. "But not any wish. It must be a wish made entirely for someone else."
 
Iris stood very still. [long pause] She thought of her friend Benny — a small rabbit in the meadow below who had once told her, shyly, that his greatest dream was to see the world from up high. She had never been able to give him that.
 
She walked to the bridge. She closed her eyes. She pointed her glowing horn at the gray arch.
 
[softly] "I wish," she said, "that Benny could see the world from up here."
 
Light poured from her horn like warm water. Color flooded back into the bridge — red first, then orange, then yellow, cascading down all seven stripes until the whole arc blazed brighter than it ever had before. [pacing]
 
And at the very bottom of the rainbow bridge, a small rabbit appeared. He blinked. He looked up. His mouth fell open.
 
Iris galloped down to meet him. They stood together in the middle of the bridge, surrounded by color, looking out at the whole wide glittering world.
 
"How?" Benny whispered.
 
Iris smiled. "A wish for a friend," she said, "is the strongest magic there is."
 
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
