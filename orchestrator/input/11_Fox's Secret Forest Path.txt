Once upon a time, in a forest where the trees grew tall and close and the leaves whispered to each other all day long, there lived a clever fox named Felix. [long pause] Felix knew the whispering woods better than anyone. He knew where the sweetest berries clustered in late summer. He knew where the stream sang loudest after rain. He knew which roots to step over and which mossy patches were too soft to trust. [beat] And he knew about the hidden path. It was a trail so narrow, so twisty, so tucked beneath low branches and behind curtains of hanging fern, that no one else had ever noticed it. It wound through the oldest part of the forest, past a ring of ancient mossy stones, and came out — quite suddenly — on a hilltop so high that you could see the entire valley spread below, glittering in the sun. [softly] Felix had never shown it to anyone. It was his special place. His secret. He went there when he needed to think, or when the world felt too loud, and every time he arrived at the top he felt as if the view had been put there just for him. [beat] But one autumn morning, he found a small mouse sitting at the entrance to the whispering woods, crying into her tiny paws. "I'm lost," she sniffled. "I've been walking since morning and I don't know where I am." [sadly] Felix looked at her. Then he looked toward the forest, toward the place where the hidden path began — a place only he had ever walked. [long pause] He made a decision. "Come with me," he said quietly. He led her along the hidden path. She gasped at every turn — at the moss-covered stones, at the smell of the earth, at the way the light fell through the trees in long golden shafts. [pacing] When they stepped out onto the hilltop and the whole valley opened up below them, the mouse went completely still. [softly] "Oh," she breathed. "Oh, it's the most beautiful thing I have ever seen." Felix sat beside her. And he noticed something curious: the view looked even more beautiful than it always had. Bigger, somehow. Warmer. [with growing realization] He had always thought sharing the path would make it smaller. Instead it had made it larger. After that, Felix brought many friends to the hidden path. And the whispering woods were never quite as quiet — or quite as lonely — as they had been before. The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
