Once upon a time, in a cave behind a waterfall at the edge of a warm forest, there lived a brave bat named Bruno. [long pause]
Bruno had night wings so wide and so thin they were nearly transparent — fine as paper, strong as leather — and ears that could hear a moth's heartbeat from ten metres away. He slept all day, hanging upside down in the dark cave with his wings folded around him like a small, neat cloak, and at dusk he flew. [sighs with the contentment of a creature perfectly suited to its life]
But tonight was not an ordinary night. Tonight Bruno was going to find the star fruit. [beat]
Star fruit grew only at the very top of the tallest fig tree in the forest — so high that even the monkeys rarely bothered — and it ripened only on nights when the stars were very bright and very close. The older bats spoke of it as something extraordinary: a fruit so sweet and so strange that one bite tasted like flying in the dark and knowing exactly where you are.
Bruno left the cave at the first blue moment of dusk, his night wings catching the cooling air. [softly]
He navigated by sound — sending clicks into the darkness and listening to what came back. Trees. Gaps. The warm column of air above the open meadow. The sudden complex echo of the tall fig tree, enormous and detailed. [pacing]
He climbed in spirals, echolocating as he went, higher and higher through the branching dark. Other bats didn't come this high. The air was cooler. The stars, above the canopy, were very bright indeed.
And there — at the absolute topmost branch, catching the starlight on its five-pointed shape — the star fruit. [with growing realization]
Bruno landed beside it. He folded his night wings. He looked at it for a moment in the starlight.
Then he took one careful bite.
[softly] It tasted like the first night of flying. Like the feeling of hearing the world in the dark and understanding exactly where everything is. Like being perfectly, exactly what you were made to be.
He ate it slowly, alone at the top of the world, the stars around him like old friends.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
