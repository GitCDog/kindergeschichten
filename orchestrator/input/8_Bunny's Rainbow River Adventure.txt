Once upon a time, in a meadow full of clover and morning dew, there lived a curious bunny named Clover. [long pause] Clover had long floppy ears that twitched at every new sound and a nose that never — not even for one single moment — stopped sniffing for adventure. She had explored every corner of the meadow, every tunnel beneath the hedgerow, every patch of tall grass by the fence. [sighs] She had begun to wonder if she had seen everything there was to see. And then, one morning, she heard something she had never heard before. A gentle musical bubbling — like a song made entirely of water. [softly] It seemed to come from somewhere beyond the tall grass, over the mossy hill, past the big beech tree. Clover's ears shot straight up. Her nose went into overdrive. [beat] She hopped. She hopped through the tall grass. She hopped over the mossy hill. She hopped around the big beech tree. And then she stopped — and her mouth fell open. [with growing realization] A rainbow river. An actual rainbow river, flowing in glowing stripes — red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and violet — each color rolling beside the others in perfect, sparkling order. "Oh my whiskers," Clover breathed. "Beautiful, isn't it?" said a voice below her. She looked down. Along the riverbank stood rows of talking flowers — daisies and tulips and little bellflowers, all nodding at her in a friendly way. "We drink from it every day," said a cheerful buttercup. "That's why we're so wonderfully colorful." "Can I try some?" Clover asked. [knowingly] "Of course," said an elderly violet. "But only one color at a time, dear. Mixed together, they give you hiccups for a week." Clover chose blue — her very favorite. She leaned down and took one careful, tiny sip. [softly] It tasted like blueberries, and summer rain, and something she couldn't quite name — something that made her feel warm all the way to the tips of her ears. She spent the whole afternoon by the rainbow river, chatting with the talking flowers and watching the colors flow. The flowers told her stories. She told them stories back. [pacing] They laughed together as the sun moved slowly across the sky. When she finally hopped home at sunset, her fur shimmered faintly blue — a secret glow that lasted until bedtime. [beat] She curled up in her burrow, ears folded gently, and thought: curiosity is the very best kind of adventure. Because you never, ever know what you might find. The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
