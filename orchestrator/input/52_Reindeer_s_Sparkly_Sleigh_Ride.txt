Once upon a time, far in the north where the sky was dark for months at a time and the cold was the kind that makes your breath visible, there lived a magic reindeer named Aurora. [long pause]
Aurora had a coat the color of warm caramel and antlers that branched like winter trees. Around her neck hung a set of sleigh bells — small, silver, and very old — that had been in her family so long that no one quite remembered how they had come to be magical. Only that they were. [sighs softly]
When Aurora shook her head and the sleigh bells rang, the northern lights appeared. [beat]
Not always — only on the coldest, clearest nights, when the sky was black as ink and the stars were as sharp as broken glass. On those nights, if Aurora stood in the snow and tossed her head and let the bells ring out across the ice, the sky would answer: sheets of green and violet and white light, rippling overhead like curtains in a great silent wind.
She had been doing this since she was a fawn. She had never once grown tired of it. [softly]
On the longest night of the year, Aurora pulled the sleigh — she was very good at this, the bells always ringing bright and clear in the cold air — across a sky that was blue-black and blazing with stars. Below her, the world was white and still. Frozen lakes. Silent forests. The occasional warm square of a lit window, where someone was still awake.
[with growing realization] She wondered sometimes about those warm windows. About what it looked like from inside, looking up — whether the northern lights seemed as vast and astonishing from below as they did from up here where she made them.
She dipped low over one small house at the edge of a frozen lake, the sleigh bells ringing merrily in the dark. [pacing]
Inside the house, a small child pressed their nose to the cold glass of the window and looked up.
The northern lights blazed green and violet across the whole sky.
[softly] The child's mouth opened into a perfect O of wonder.
Aurora smiled her reindeer smile and flew on, the bells singing in the endless cold night.
Some magic, she thought, is made better by knowing someone is watching.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
