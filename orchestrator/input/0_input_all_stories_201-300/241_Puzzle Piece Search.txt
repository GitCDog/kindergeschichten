In the wide shallow box that lived under the coffee table, there were five hundred puzzle pieces, and one of them was missing. The missing piece's name was Pip, and she had been missing for three days.

Pip was a curious puzzle piece — sky-blue on one side, with two interlocking tabs and two sockets, perfectly shaped to fit in the upper right section of a picture of a lighthouse by the sea. Without her, the puzzle could not be completed. A ragged hole in the upper right corner stared up at the ceiling, waiting.

Pip herself was wedged under the sofa cushion, and she had been on quite a journey to get there.

On the first day, a cat had batted her off the table. On the second day, she had been kicked by a passing sock. On the third day, a bouncy ball had rolled into her and nudged her under the cushion's edge, where she now rested in the dark and the quiet.

She could hear, faintly, the sound of the family working on the puzzle above.

"It must be here somewhere," said a voice.

"Maybe it's under the sofa," said a smaller voice.

There was a bumping sound, and then a hand pushed under the sofa cushion and found her. Pip felt herself lifted up, carried across the room, and pressed gently — click — into her waiting spot.

The lighthouse puzzle was complete.

Pip looked at the picture she was now part of. The lighthouse stood tall on a rocky point, its beam sweeping out over painted waves. A small boat bobbed in the distance. She was the sky piece — the bit of blue above the lighthouse's light.

She had seemed so small alone under the cushion. But here, as part of the whole, she understood her exact shape. Her two tabs fitted perfectly into two spaces, and two sockets held two other pieces in place. She was the fit search made real — the missing spot found, the picture made whole.

"There," said the smaller voice with deep satisfaction.

The family leaned back and looked at the finished puzzle together.

Five hundred pieces, all in the right place. Including one small curious sky-blue piece who had taken three days to come home.
