Inside an old explorer's jacket pocket, there lived a magic compass named True. True had a brass body, a cracked glass face, and a needle that always, always pointed north — no matter what.

One misty morning, a young girl named Petra found the jacket in her grandmother's attic. She slipped her hand into the pocket and felt True's cool smooth case. When she held True up, the needle swung and settled, pointing steadily toward the far wall.

"North," Petra whispered, feeling a tingle she could not explain.

She decided then and there that she would follow it.

The hidden map tucked into the jacket's inner pocket showed a trail through the old forest behind Grandmother's house, winding past a stream and up a rocky hill. Petra had never gone that far alone. But True felt solid and certain in her hand, and that made all the difference.

Into the forest she went. The trees were tall and quiet, their roots knotted above the ground like sleeping giants' fingers. True's needle held perfectly steady, guiding Petra along the north quest route as though it had been waiting years to show someone this path.

At the stream, Petra stepped across on mossy stones without getting her boots wet — something she had never managed before. Up the rocky hill she climbed, her breath making small clouds in the cool morning air.

At the very top, she stopped and stared.

Spread out below her was the whole valley — the village, Grandmother's house, the winding silver river, and beyond it all, blue hills rolling away into the distance. She had not known the world was so large and so beautiful from up here.

True's needle held perfectly north, steady as a heartbeat.

Petra sat down on a flat rock and drew the view in her notebook, every hill and rooftop and tree. She did not know what she had expected to find at the top of the hidden map's trail — treasure, perhaps, or a secret door. But this was better.

The magic compass had not led her to gold. It had led her to the kind of view that stays inside you forever.

She tucked True safely back into the jacket pocket and began the walk home, already planning when she would come back.
