Off the coast where the water turned from green to blue, a little dolphin named Dip was having the best day of the season.

The conditions were perfect: a long offshore swell running from the southwest, clean and glassy, and a school of mackerel moving at exactly the right depth to be interesting without requiring too much effort. The sea play had begun before dawn, when Dip had found the swell and realized its potential.

The water jump was her specialty.

Dip would ride the swell forward on the wave's face, building speed without swimming — the wave doing the work, her body reading the pressure and adjusting continuously — and then at the moment of maximum speed she would angle up, leave the water, and arc through the air.

For three seconds she was entirely airborne.

She had never managed to think, in those three seconds, about anything useful. She had tried. But the air and the light and the sensation of being a sea creature briefly in the sky always emptied her mind of everything except the immediate fact of it.

Then the sea play resumed — back into the water, banking hard to the left to avoid the mackerel school, surfing the next wave forward.

Other dolphins had joined her through the morning. Sea play was communal by nature — one dolphin finding something good and others following, drawn by the sounds and movements of joy.

By midmorning there were eleven of them in the swell.

A boat of whale-watchers had come alongside and stayed for two hours, the humans at the rail with their cameras and their exclamations. Dip had investigated the boat's bow wave, which was worth surfing if you positioned yourself exactly right.

The mackerel moved on in the afternoon.

The swell softened.

Dip made one final water jump — lower, slower, but still true — and dove back to the deep cool below, where the sea play quieted into rest.


Dip descended into the cooler deep and slowed. The sea play of the morning had burned through her morning energy in the best possible way. She breathed easily and let the current carry her a little, resting the way dolphins rest — moving, always, but loosely. Not directing, just going.

Above her, the surface shimmered with the light she had left and returned to.

[pause] Good night my little friend
