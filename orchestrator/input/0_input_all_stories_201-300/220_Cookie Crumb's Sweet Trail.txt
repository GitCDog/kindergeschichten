Under the kitchen table, in the space between the chair legs, there was a whole world that most people never noticed. A cheerful cookie crumb named Nibble lived there, along with a small community of other crumbs, seeds, and dust specks who had all fallen from the great table above.

Nibble was round and golden, with a faint smell of cinnamon and sugar. He had arrived that morning from a particularly excellent shortbread cookie, and he was still warm from the oven when he landed on the bakery floor — as the crumbs called the kitchen tiles.

The sweet trail stretched from the table all the way to the kitchen mat. It was a road of crumbs, and Nibble decided to walk its entire length. He set off in the morning light, nodding at a sesame seed, stepping carefully past a dried raisin who was sleeping, and pausing to admire a sugar crystal that sparkled like a tiny diamond.

At the halfway point, there was great excitement. A long, waggling nose appeared at floor level — the family's dog, sniffing along the sweet trail with tremendous enthusiasm. Nibble rolled quickly behind the chair leg. The dog's nose passed over him with a great [snuffle] and then moved on, somewhat disappointed.

"That was exciting," said Nibble.

"That happens every afternoon," said the raisin, who had woken up.

Nibble laughed and continued on his way. At the end of the sweet trail, he arrived at the kitchen mat. From here he could see the whole kitchen — the tall cabinets, the bright window, the mixing bowl still out from the morning's baking.

He sat down contentedly on the mat and breathed in the warm, sugary air. He was a cookie crumb — small and sweet and temporary — but the world looked very large and full of wonders from down here.

The afternoon light stretched golden across the tiles, and Nibble settled in for the end of a very good day.

As darkness fell, a gentle brush swept along the kitchen tiles — the last task of the evening. Nibble closed his eyes and let the warmth of the day carry him. He thought that being a cookie crumb was not so different from being anything else: you start somewhere warm and good, you find your way along the trail, and you enjoy every single bit of the journey before you become part of something larger than yourself.
