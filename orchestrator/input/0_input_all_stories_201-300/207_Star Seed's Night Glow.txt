On the very last branch of the tallest tree at the edge of the meadow, there rested a tiny star seed named Lumie. She was no bigger than a dewdrop and had a soft glow that pulsed like a heartbeat — in and out, in and out, all through the night.

Lumie had been waiting for the right moment to begin her glow journey. Every star seed dreams of the night when she will rise up from her branch and float into the sky to become a real star. But Lumie was nervous.

"What if I go out?" she whispered to the branch.

"Fireflies go out sometimes," said the branch gently, "but they always come back on."

That gave Lumie courage. On a night when the sky was very dark — darker than usual because the moon had gone behind a cloud — she let go of the branch.

At first she fell. Then, slowly, her glow grew stronger, and she began to rise. The night sky stretched around her, deep and quiet and enormous. She felt very small. But as her eyes adjusted, she saw that she was not alone. Hundreds of other tiny star seeds were rising from meadows and treetops and garden rooftops all around her, each one glowing softly.

Together, they rose higher and higher, and the sky began to fill with light — not the blinding light of day, but the kind and steady glow of many small lights working together.

A child in a house below pulled back her curtain and looked up at the night sky. "It's full of stars tonight," she said softly to her mother.

Lumie found her place between two older stars who smiled warmly. "Welcome," they said. "You're just in time."

She pulsed her light — in and out, in and out — and the night sky felt a little less dark, a little less lonesome, and a great deal more beautiful.

From that night on, every time someone looked up, Lumie was shining gently back at them.

The older stars showed Lumie how to hold her position even when the wind of space tried to nudge her sideways. She learned that being small was not the same as being weak. The steadiest lights in the night sky were often the tiniest ones — the ones that shone not to be noticed, but simply because shining was what they did. Lumie decided she would always be exactly that kind of star.
