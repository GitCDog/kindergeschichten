Chester was a cheerful yellow rubber duck who lived on the edge of the bathtub, and he took his job very seriously. Every evening when the taps turned on and the warm water began to rise, Chester slid in with a dignified splash and immediately set about exploring the bath sea.

The bath sea was different every night.

Sometimes it was calm and glassy, and Chester could see his reflection in the water below him — a round yellow captain surveying a peaceful sea. On these nights he sailed in slow circles, humming to himself.

But tonight, the foam waves were magnificent.

Mia had poured in an enormous amount of bubble bath — far more than usual — and the foam was rising in towers and castles and extraordinary shapes. Chester paddled forward and plunged straight into the middle of it.

"Uncharted territory," he announced to no one in particular.

He navigated between two foam mountains, squeezed past a bubble cliff that wobbled dangerously, and emerged into a clear blue lagoon near the soap dish. A washcloth, draped over the side of the tub, looked remarkably like a sail.

Mia arrived with a splash, and the calm waters of the bath sea became a storm of waves. Chester rocked and bobbed and dipped, spinning in the current, his squeaky voice calling out "All hands on deck!" which in practice meant only himself.

They played for a long time. Mia built foam towers and Chester demolished them by sailing through. She tried to balance a sponge on Chester's head, which worked once before he bobbed sideways and it slid off into the water with a satisfying slap.

Eventually the water cooled and Mia's eyes began to droop.

The plug was pulled, and Chester heard the great rushing sound as the bath sea disappeared. He was lifted out by a damp hand and placed back on the edge of the tub, dripping and content.

The foam waves were gone. The bath sea was an empty white tub. But Chester sat up straight and gazed out at it as though it were still full of adventure.

Because tomorrow night, it would be.
