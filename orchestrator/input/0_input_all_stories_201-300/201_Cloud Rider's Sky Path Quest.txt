High above the rooftops, where the air is cool and birds seem like tiny dots, there lived a brave cloud rider named Cirrus. He was round and fluffy, with a smile like a crescent moon, and his most treasured possession was a silver wind path — a swirling ribbon of breeze that could carry him anywhere in the great sky kingdom.

The sky kingdom stretched from the first pink glow of sunrise all the way to the deep purple curtain of night. It had cloud-castles and starlight bridges, and every corner smelled like the air right after rain. But lately, something was wrong. The wind path had grown tangled, and all across the sky kingdom, clouds were stuck in place.

"I'll fix it," said Cirrus bravely, tying a scarf of mist around his neck. He floated along the tangled path, carefully finding each knot. The first knot had caught on a weather vane atop a tall tower. Cirrus tugged gently until it came free with a soft [whoosh]. The second knot was wrapped around a flock of sleeping swallows. He tickled them lightly, and they fluttered away, untangling the path as they flew.

Higher and higher Cirrus climbed, until he reached the topmost ridge of the sky kingdom where the wind gathered its strength each morning. There sat the largest tangle of all — a swirl of cold air and warm air that had bumped into each other and refused to let go.

Cirrus thought carefully. He knew that cold air and warm air were both important. He took a slow, deep breath and began to hum — a low, steady sound that seemed to calm both winds at once. Slowly, the cold air drifted to one side and the warm air to the other. The tangle released with a gentle sigh, and the silver wind path gleamed and straightened like a ribbon caught in sunlight.

All at once, every cloud in the sky kingdom began to drift again. Stars shook and sparkled. Far below, children looked up and smiled, not knowing that a brave cloud rider had saved the night sky just for them.

Cirrus floated back to his favourite star, curled up on a soft pillow of fog, and closed his eyes. The sky kingdom was at peace, and every cloud found its way home again.
