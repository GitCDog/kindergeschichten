It began with three sofa cushions, two dining chairs, and a striped blanket — and by the end of the afternoon it was something else entirely.

The pillow fort had been constructed by a pair of siblings named Eva and Tom, and it occupied most of the living room floor. Its front entrance was a low tunnel requiring hands-and-knees navigation. Its back wall was a row of sofa cushions stacked two-high. The striped blanket formed the ceiling, draped over the chairs and held in place by a library book on each end.

The hideout game had strict rules, known only to the inhabitants.

Rule one: no adults inside without an invitation. Rule two: torches only, no overhead light. Rule three: everything brought inside became an official item of the secret base.

The secret base currently contained: two flashlights, a bag of crackers, a soft bear of uncertain age, Tom's notebook (designated as the Log), and a jar of pencils.

They sat inside in the yellow torch-light with the blanket ceiling low above them and the cushion walls soft on all sides, and the world outside the fort felt very far away.

Tom wrote in the Log. Today's entry read: Secret base active. No hostile activity detected. Crackers: sufficient.

Eva reorganized the pencils by color for no particular reason.

Outside, the afternoon went on without them. Traffic passed. The radio played quietly in the kitchen. A cat walked across the garden.

Inside the pillow fort, something else entirely was happening — a small country with two citizens, its own rules, its own economy of crackers, its own record-keeping. A temporary nation assembled from blankets and cushions and the determined imagination of two children who had decided that this afternoon, this particular square of floor, was theirs.

The torchlight made long soft shadows on the blanket ceiling.

Tom closed the Log and offered Eva a cracker.

She accepted it. The secret base was peaceful.


When it was finally time to dismantle the fort, the cushions were returned to the sofa with ceremony. The blanket was folded. The chairs were returned to the dining room. But Eva and Tom both knew — the secret base wasn't really in the blankets. It was in the agreement between them to build something together and stay inside it.
