Bloop was a bright yellow balloon, and he was absolutely certain that being a balloon was the greatest thing in the entire world. His favorite moment of every day was the one just before liftoff — that delicious, tingly feeling when the ribbon held him just barely to the ground.

Today was a very special day. A silly mix-up at the birthday party had left Bloop free. His ribbon slipped through a child's fingers, and before anyone could catch him, Bloop was sailing into the open sky on a wonderful air trip.

Up and up he floated, over the rooftops and the park and the library. The world below looked like a patchwork quilt of colors — green squares of garden, grey ribbons of road, and the shining blue oval of the town swimming pool.

"This must be what birds feel," Bloop said happily.

High up in the party sky, he discovered he was not alone. A pair of seagulls swooped past him with a friendly cry. A kite shaped like a dragon nodded politely. A little white cloud drifted over and wrapped him in cool, damp arms for just a moment.

"Where are you going?" asked the cloud.

"Wherever the wind takes me!" Bloop answered.

And the wind did take him — over a meadow dusted with wildflowers, over a farmer's wide field where a horse looked up and whinnied in surprise, and finally toward a playground where children were laughing.

One little girl with two red pigtails spotted him first. "A balloon!" she shrieked with delight, and the chase was on. Children ran across the grass with arms stretched up, jumping and giggling.

Bloop felt a rush of pure happiness. He hadn't even meant to make them laugh and play — he had simply been going on his silly air trip — and yet here was a whole playground of joy erupting beneath him.

Eventually the wind gentled, and Bloop drifted down, down, down until the little girl with red pigtails caught his trailing ribbon and held on tight.

She tied him carefully to her wrist and walked home with him bouncing above her head like a small yellow sun.

That night, Bloop floated in the corner of her bedroom, watching the moonlight come through the curtains. He had traveled far across the party sky, and tomorrow, he knew, the wind would have more adventures waiting.
