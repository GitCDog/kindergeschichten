Rocky was not just any flashlight — he was a brave flashlight with a wide yellow beam and a red plastic body that fit perfectly in a child's hand. Rocky lived on the bedside table and dreamed of one adventure above all others: exploring the dark cave at the end of Meadow Lane.

One evening, Rocky got his chance. A curious little fox named Finn came sniffing by and said, "Rocky, I have heard whispers about a dark cave filled with glittering crystals. Will you come with me?"

Rocky clicked on his light beam immediately. "Let's go!"

The two friends set off through the whispering forest. The trees grew tall and close together, and the stars above disappeared behind a canopy of leaves. Finn's paws padded softly on the mossy ground while Rocky swept his bright beam across the path ahead, chasing shadows into the corners.

At last, they stood before the entrance of the dark cave. Cold air rolled out from the darkness inside, and Finn's ears flattened a little.

"It's very dark in there," Finn whispered.

"That's what I'm here for," Rocky said cheerfully.

Step by step, they moved into the dark cave together. Rocky's brave light beam stretched ahead, and as it touched the cave walls, something astonishing happened — the crystals inside began to glow and shimmer. Blues and purples and silvers sparkled everywhere, like the inside of a fallen star.

Finn gasped. "It's beautiful!"

"All it needed was a little light," Rocky said warmly.

They wandered deeper into the cave, and Rocky made sure to illuminate every corner so Finn never felt afraid. They discovered a tiny underground stream that trickled with silver water, and a family of sleeping bats tucked high on the ceiling like small velvet clouds.

Finn pressed close to Rocky's warm glow. "Thank you for being so brave," he murmured.

"You were brave too," Rocky replied. "You were the one who wanted to find out what was inside."

When they finally turned back toward the cave entrance, the light of the moon greeted them like an old friend. Finn shook the cave dust from his tail, and Rocky dimmed his beam slowly as the world outside provided enough light to walk home.

Back on the bedside table, Rocky settled into his usual spot, his batteries warm from the journey. He had lit up the darkest place he had ever seen, and he knew that every bit of darkness — no matter how deep — was no match for even a single brave light beam.
