Deep inside a sugary hill at the edge of a quiet meadow, there lived a curious candy dragon named Fudge. Fudge had shimmering wings the colour of strawberry drops and a tail that curled like a peppermint stick. Every morning, she would fly in and out of her sugar cave, leaving little trails of sparkling sweetness behind her.

The sugar cave was a wonderful place. Its walls were lined with toffee crystals that glowed softly in the dark, and rivers of warm caramel trickled through tiny valleys on the floor. In the very deepest part of the cave sat the sweet treasure — a chest filled with the rarest flavours in the world: moonberry, dreamscotch, and the most precious of all, a single golden honey star.

One day, Fudge noticed that the honey star had disappeared from its special cushion in the chest. She sat very still and thought. She had not eaten it — she only ever looked at it. She searched every corner of the cave but found nothing. Then, very quietly, she heard a small sound — a soft munching from beneath the caramel river.

Carefully, Fudge lifted a caramel stone and found a tiny sugar mouse with wide, guilty eyes. A golden smear was on his whiskers. The honey star was mostly gone, but a tiny sliver remained in his paws.

"I was only going to taste a crumb," squeaked the mouse. "But it was so wonderful I couldn't stop."

Fudge felt a small flash of sadness — but then she smiled. She had always kept the honey star to look at, never to share. Perhaps sharing was better after all.

She placed the tiny sliver back in the chest and invited the sugar mouse to sit beside it. "We'll save this last piece," she said gently, "for a very special moment."

The mouse wiped his whiskers and nodded. From that evening on, Fudge and the sugar mouse became the best of friends. They explored every corner of the sweet treasure cave together, tasting the moonberry crystals and watching the caramel rivers gleam.

At night, curled beside the glowing toffee walls, the cave felt warmer than ever before.
