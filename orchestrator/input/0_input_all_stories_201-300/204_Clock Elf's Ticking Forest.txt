Tucked behind the grandfather clock in the hallway, there lived a little clock elf named Tick. He had a round hat with a tiny gear on top, boots that went click-click on the wooden floor, and the most important job in the whole house: keeping every clock ticking in perfect time.

One misty morning, Tick heard something unusual. The ticking forest — a magical place where every tree had a clock face and every leaf was a second hand — had gone completely silent. No tick. No tock. Nothing.

Tick grabbed his tool satchel and set off into the forest. The ticking trees stood still, their hands frozen in place. A small squirrel made of copper springs sat on a stump, worried. "They all stopped at midnight," the squirrel said. "I don't know why."

Tick walked deeper among the silent ticking trees, listening very carefully. Then he noticed something small but important — the winding path that ran through the middle of the forest had been blocked by a fallen pine cone, as big as a boulder in the elf world.

The winding path was where the forest gathered its energy each night, like a giant key turning in a lock. Without it, every clock tree had simply run down.

Tick pushed and pushed. The pine cone was far too heavy. So he went back to the copper squirrel. "Could you help me?" he asked. Together — Tick pushing from one side and the squirrel pulling from the other — they slowly moved the great pine cone off the path.

At once, a warm golden hum began to rise from the ground. One by one, the clock faces on the trees began to glow. Their hands trembled, then started moving again. Tick, tick, tick — the ticking forest came back to life with a sound like a thousand gentle heartbeats.

The copper squirrel danced in a circle. Tick sat on the pine cone, tired but very happy. He wound up his own little pocket watch and listened to the steady rhythm all around him.

The forest was full of time again, and it sounded just like home.
