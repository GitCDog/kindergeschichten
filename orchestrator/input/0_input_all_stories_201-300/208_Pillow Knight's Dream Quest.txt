In the land between sleep and waking, where the air smells of lavender and the light is always the colour of just-before-sunset, there lived a brave pillow knight named Fluff. His armour was made of the softest material imaginable — it was, in fact, a pillow, stitched together with moonlight thread and stuffed with the quietest thoughts of a thousand sleeping children.

Fluff's job was to guard the entrance to dream land, making sure that only good dreams were allowed inside. Every night, he stood at the great velvet gate and checked each dream as it floated by.

One particular evening, a strange dream arrived — a tangle of mixed-up shapes and worried colours. It was not exactly a bad dream, but it was confused and lost. It had a sad little face in the middle and kept spinning in circles.

"What happened to you?" asked Fluff kindly.

"I got mixed up," the dream admitted. "I started as a happy dream about flying, and then I bumped into a worry dream, and now I don't know what I am."

Fluff thought carefully. His soft armour glowed a little as he reached out and gently held the dream still. With patient hands, he began to smooth out its shapes, tucking the worried bits underneath and bringing the flying parts to the front.

The dream shivered, then went still, then — very slowly — a pair of bright wings appeared in the middle of it, and a smile.

"There," said Fluff. "You always were a flying dream. You just needed reminding."

The dream floated happily through the velvet gate into dream land, and Fluff could hear, from somewhere deep inside, the sound of a child laughing in their sleep.

Fluff settled back against the gate, his soft armour warm and steady. The night was long, but that was all right. There were many more dreams to welcome, and he was happy doing it.

He fluffed his armour once — just like punching a pillow into shape — and smiled into the quiet dark.

Some nights, a dream would arrive so beautiful and complete that Fluff needed to do nothing at all — just step aside and let it pass. Those were his favourite nights. He would stand at the velvet gate in his soft armour, breathing in the lavender air, watching a perfect dream float by like a lantern on a river, and feel grateful that his was the job of helping the best things find their way in.
