High in the winter sky, where the air is so cold it turns your breath to tiny clouds, a kind snowflake named Crystal was born. She appeared as if from nothing — just a tiny speck of ice that grew, slowly and perfectly, into the most beautiful shape you could imagine: six arms, each one laced with delicate patterns, no two the same as any other snowflake in the sky.

Crystal drifted on the cold air and looked down. The world below was soft and brown and waiting. It had not snowed yet this winter, and the trees stood bare against the grey sky.

She began her icy dance, swirling on the updraft of wind. First she spun clockwise, then counter-clockwise, then floated in wide lazy circles. Other snowflakes joined her — thousands of them, each one dancing their own private dance, and together they made the sky look like a snow globe that had been shaken by a giant's hand.

Down below, a small boy pressed his nose against the window and said, "It's snowing!"

Crystal landed on a branch outside that window. She balanced there, perfectly still, and looked in at the boy. He was smiling so widely his cheeks had gone pink. He pressed a finger to the glass and looked at her.

She stayed as long as she could — just long enough for the boy to get a good look at her six perfect arms and the tiny patterns she had made in the cold dark sky. Then a breeze lifted her gently and carried her on.

She settled at last on the grass below, joining the millions of other snowflakes who had come together to make the world white and soft and quiet.

The winter sky danced on above her, full of new snowflakes beginning their journey. Crystal rested, perfectly at peace, in a world that was clean and still and utterly beautiful.

In the morning, the boy came outside with his scarf and his mittens and looked at the snow covering everything. He bent down and looked closely at a patch near the garden wall, and for just a moment — in the early light — he thought he could see the outline of a perfect six-armed snowflake among all the millions. He did not know it was Crystal. But Crystal knew, and she sparkled very slightly, just for him.
