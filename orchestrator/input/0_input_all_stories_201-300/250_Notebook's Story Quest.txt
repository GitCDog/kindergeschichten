On the desk by the window, a brave notebook named Bound waited with all the patience she had — which was considerable, since notebooks have nothing to do but wait and watch and be ready.

Bound was spiral-bound, with a cover the color of sea-glass and two hundred blank pages that rustled softly when the window was open. She had been bought three weeks ago and had not yet been written in, which was a situation she held with hope rather than anxiety.

Every story quest starts with a blank page. She knew this.

The child who owned her was named Oscar, and Oscar had very many ideas and very little ability to start. He would open Bound, look at the first page, and close her again. He had done this eleven times.

On the twelfth time, something was different.

Oscar sat down with a pen — not a pencil, which could be erased, but a pen, which was a commitment — and wrote one word at the top of page one.

The word was: DRAGON.

Bound felt it appear like a small warmth on her first page. One word. It was enough.

Oscar stared at the word. Then he wrote: The dragon was not what anyone expected.

Then he couldn't stop.

Three pages later, the dragon had a name and a problem and a friend she didn't yet know was a friend. Oscar's hand moved faster as the story quest found its own momentum, and Bound felt each page fill up with a particular kind of warmth — the warmth of something being made that had not existed an hour ago.

He wrote until the light from the window turned gold and his hand cramped and he had to stop.

He closed Bound and looked at her.

Bound rested on the desk, her first three pages full, her remaining one hundred and ninety-seven still waiting. She was not worried about them. They had heard the beginning. They knew something good was coming.

Blank pages are not empty. They are full of story quests that haven't found their way out yet.

[pause] Good night my little friend
