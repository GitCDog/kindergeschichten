In the green and yellow colour box on the classroom shelf, thirty-two crayons lived together in a snug row. The curious one was Vermillion — a deep reddish-orange who had always wondered what lay outside the box lid.

Most crayons stayed inside. They were used, put back, and that was the routine. But Vermillion had seen the art land through the gaps in the box — wide white paper stretching away in every direction, waiting to become anything.

One afternoon, a child reached into the box and accidentally knocked Vermillion out. She rolled across the table and landed on the floor. Vermillion looked around. The classroom was enormous from floor level. Chair legs rose like pillars. The wastepaper basket was a tower. The art land — the paper on the table — was far above her.

But on the floor there was something she had never seen before. Someone had taped paper down here too, for a floor painting project, and it was big — the size of a swimming pool. Vermillion rolled onto it.

She had never made a mark before. She pressed her tip to the paper and dragged herself slowly forward. A warm reddish-orange line appeared behind her. She stopped and looked at it. It was the most beautiful thing she had ever done.

She made another line, then another, slowly and joyfully. She made a sun first, then rays going out in every direction. She made round oranges and pointed flames and long curving arcs that were just for the pleasure of them.

When the child who had dropped her came back, she found Vermillion in the middle of a whole glowing world of colour. "You made all this yourself?" the child asked.

Vermillion just sat quietly in the middle of her art land creation, content and a little smudged.

The child picked her up gently and gave her a small wipe. "You're my favourite," she said, and carried her back to the colour box with great care.

Back inside the box, Vermillion settled next to her neighbours — a deep blue and a sunny yellow. She told them quietly what she had made on the floor. They listened with wide, wondering eyes. The blue whispered that he had always wanted to see the art land. Vermillion smiled and told him to watch for the lid being left open. And somehow, from that moment on, they all felt just a little braver.
