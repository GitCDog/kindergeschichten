Pepper was a playful slipper — left foot, warm brown wool, with a small embroidered fox on the toe. His companion was Pippin, the right slipper, who was calmer and more cautious and always seemed to end up under the bed while Pepper ended up beside the bookshelf.

Every evening began the same way: their owner, a boy named Joe, would kick both slippers off his feet and they would land wherever gravity decided, and then the house run would begin.

Pepper loved the house run.

The first phase involved Joe's dog, a small terrier named Biscuit, who had a deep interest in all footwear. Every evening Biscuit would find Pepper first — Pepper was always closer to the middle of the room — and carry him off to the kitchen.

"Again," Pippin would say from under the bed.

Pepper didn't mind the dog's enthusiasm. It was all part of the soft chase — the nightly game of being lost and found, of turning up in unexpected places, of being reunited each morning by Joe, who would search the house saying, "Where did my slippers GO?" as though this were a great mystery.

One evening, the soft chase took an unexpected turn. Biscuit carried Pepper to the garden.

This was unprecedented. The garden was outside. Pepper had never been outside before. He sat in the cool grass and looked up at the enormous sky, which was turning pink and orange as the sun went down.

A hedgehog shuffled past and regarded him with small suspicious eyes.

"Are you lost?" asked the hedgehog.

"Only temporarily," said Pepper.

Joe found him twenty minutes later, standing in the garden in his socks and looking very confused. He lifted Pepper up and turned him over, checking for damage — all the embroidered fox was intact — and tucked him under his arm.

Back inside, Joe reunited Pepper with Pippin and placed them both neatly by the front door.

"Stay," he said sternly.

Pepper sat very still and felt the warmth of the indoors wrap around him. He was just a slipper. But he had seen the sunset from the garden, and the hedgehog, and the enormous sky.

It had been a very good evening.
