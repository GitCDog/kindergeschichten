In the long silver barrel of a flute resting on a music stand, there lived a little music note named Sol. He was shaped like a round head with a single stem, and when the flute was played, he and his friends would fly out into the air and travel along the melody path — a shimmering invisible road of sound that wound through the room.

Most notes came back when the music stopped. But Sol was curious, and one evening he decided to follow the melody path wherever it led.

He flew out on the first note of a bedtime piece — a soft, slow tune that the music teacher played for her young students at the end of the day. The sound river was warm and wide, and Sol rode it easily, sailing through the air on his single stem.

He passed the window and floated out into the street. The melody path continued even there, carrying him along rooftops and over the park. He heard other sounds joining his — a bird finishing its last evening song, a bicycle bell, the sound of someone's humming in a kitchen window.

Each sound added to the path, making it broader and richer. Sol was no longer a lone note but part of a great, flowing river of evening sound.

The melody path led him at last to the edge of a sleeping neighbourhood, where the street lights had just come on and the first stars were appearing. Here, the sound river grew still and soft, like the last echo of a lullaby.

Sol hung in the air for a long, peaceful moment, listening. Then, gently, he turned and followed the melody path back — back over the rooftops, back through the window, back into the flute where he settled with a quiet, contented [hum].

The music teacher looked at her flute. She thought she heard one last note — very soft, very sweet — that she hadn't played herself.

She smiled and put the flute away.

Sol told the other notes about his journey when they gathered in the flute the next morning. They listened with wonder. From that evening on, whenever a gentle piece was played, the notes would linger just a little longer after the music stopped — stretching the melody path as far as it would go, listening to the evening sounds join in, before coming quietly back to rest inside the silver barrel they called home.
