On the shelf above the bath, between a bottle of green shampoo and a small stack of face cloths, there was a seashell named Shell who had been there for eleven years.

She was a conch — not a large one, but a proper conch with a pointed spiral and a flared lip the color of a pale sunrise inside. She had been brought from a beach holiday when the family's oldest child was three, and she had been placed on the bath shelf and never moved.

The ocean whisper was what people heard when they held Shell up to their ear.

It was not the ocean, technically. Physicists who had looked into the matter had concluded that the sound was ambient noise resonating inside the shell's chamber. But Shell herself had come from the sea, and the resonance inside her was shaped by the exact form of her interior — a form built over years of slow growth in saltwater.

The ocean whisper carried something true, even if it wasn't literally the sea.

The three-year-old who had found Shell on the beach was fourteen now. She still picked Shell up sometimes when she was in the bath, and held her to her ear, and listened.

What she heard was the same as when she was three and what she heard was entirely different, because she was different.

A beach song has this property: it meets you where you are.

Once, she had heard in it the simple excitement of a cold wave and a shell in her hand. Now she heard something more complicated — distance, and time, and the particular comfort of things that stay in their place across many years.

Shell sat on the shelf between the shampoo and the face cloths, patient and unchanging. The bath filled and emptied. Seasons turned. The children grew.

The ocean whisper was always there, waiting for whoever needed it.


The girl pressed Shell to her ear one more time before bed, as she sometimes did. The resonance was the same as it had been on every previous occasion, which was the point. Some things were reliable. Some sounds said: the world is large and you are part of it and the sea is still there.

Shell rested on the shelf in the cool dark after the light went out.
