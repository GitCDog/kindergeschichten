In a tall glass of lemonade on the kitchen table, there lived a cheerful little ice cube named Chill. He was perfectly square, sparkled in the sunlight, and liked nothing better than bobbing gently in the cold lemonade with his friends.

But one warm summer day, the sun shone brighter than usual and the lemonade grew warmer and warmer. One by one, Chill's friends began to shrink away into the drink. Chill could feel himself getting smaller too — at his corners first, then along his edges.

"I'm going to melt," he said to himself. But instead of being frightened, he decided to use every moment of his melting race wisely.

First, he made himself spin slowly in the glass, cooling the lemonade one last time, making it taste sharp and delicious. A little girl picked up the glass and took a long, happy sip. "So refreshing!" she said, and smiled at the window.

Then Chill melted a little more, and as he did, he imagined himself becoming something new. He became part of the lemonade, then a drop of cool water on the side of the glass, then a tiny puddle on the table that the summer breeze blew gently dry.

He rose as a soft, invisible wisp of water into the warm air. Up and up he floated, past the open window, past the garden, up into the bright blue sky. Higher than the highest rooftop, he found other wisps like himself, and together they gathered into a soft white cloud.

From up there, the world looked very small and peaceful. Chill — now part of the cloud — looked down at the little girl sitting in her garden with her lemonade. He felt content. He had cooled her drink, refreshed her day, and now he was floating free.

One day, he would fall as rain, and fill a river, and perhaps become an ice cube again. But for now, drifting in the summer sky was more than enough.

The cloud grew softer, and the evening turned golden.

The other clouds drifted past, each one carrying its own piece of summer inside it. Chill discovered that from up high, every garden looked like a tiny painting. He could see swimming pools glinting blue and sunflowers turned toward the light, and he felt deeply glad that he had been part of this warm, bright day — even if only as a single melted cube of ice in a tall glass of lemonade.
