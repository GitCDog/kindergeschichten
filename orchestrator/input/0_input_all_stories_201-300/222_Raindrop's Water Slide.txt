High above the world, tucked inside a fluffy grey cloud, there lived a tiny raindrop named Drippy. Drippy spent her days gazing down at the earth far below, watching rivers glitter and gardens bloom, and wondering what it would feel like to finally fall.

One breezy morning, the cloud gave a great big shiver and Drippy felt herself tip over the edge. Down she went! The cloud fall was faster than she had imagined — the wind rushed past her ears and the world below grew rapidly bigger and bigger.

"Wheeeee!" cried Drippy.

But the very best part came next. Drippy landed on the tip-top of a smooth green leaf that curved like a perfect water slide. She zipped down the leaf's middle, following the gentle groove, and slid right off the end into a waiting puddle with a satisfying little splash.

She bobbed up to the surface and looked around in amazement. The puddle was full of other raindrops, each one giggling and spinning.

"First time?" asked an older raindrop named Marsh.

"Yes!" Drippy said breathlessly. "That was the most wonderful water slide I could have imagined!"

Marsh smiled. "There are hundreds more. Every leaf, every petal, every blade of grass is a slide if you know how to look."

Together, Drippy and Marsh floated to the edge of the puddle, caught the current of a tiny stream, and were swept away on a meandering journey through the garden. They slid down a rose petal, spun around a stone, and raced along a mossy root.

A small beetle perched on a twig watched them go by and waved his tiny antennae. A snail nodded approvingly from beneath a mushroom.

At last the stream carried Drippy to the roots of a great oak tree, where she was slowly, gently absorbed into the cool dark soil. She felt herself spreading out and sinking down, becoming part of something enormous and alive.

"Is this the end?" she wondered.

But Marsh had told her the secret of raindrops: there is no end, only a long, beautiful circle. One day, the sun would coax her back up through the leaves as a whisper of mist, and she would float skyward and join a new cloud, and wait once more for another cloud fall, another water slide, another splash.

Drippy closed her eyes and rested in the cool earth, content and full of wonder.
