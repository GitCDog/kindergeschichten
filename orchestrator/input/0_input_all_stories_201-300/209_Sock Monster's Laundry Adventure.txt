In the very back of a dark drawer, behind the folded jumpers and the balled-up scarves, there lived a silly sock monster named Tangle. He was soft and stripy, with googly button eyes and a laugh like a washing machine on spin cycle. His greatest hobby was collecting lost socks — and hiding them in his drawer cave.

Everyone in the house had noticed that their socks went missing. One red sock here, a spotted one there. The drawer cave was getting very full indeed.

One laundry day, a small girl named Mira decided to find the lost laundry once and for all. She pulled open every drawer, shook out every jumper, and peered under every bed. She was just about to give up when she heard a small giggle coming from the very back of the big bottom drawer.

She reached in carefully and found Tangle, completely surrounded by socks — polka-dotted ones, striped ones, ones with little dinosaurs on them.

"Did you take all of these?" she asked.

Tangle looked at his feet — which were, of course, wearing four different socks at once. "I only wanted one friend," he said quietly. "But then I got lonely, so I took another. And then another. I'm sorry."

Mira sat down cross-legged on the floor. She looked at the pile of lost laundry and had an idea. "What if instead of hiding them," she said, "you help me sort them?"

Tangle's googly eyes went wide. He had never sorted socks before. It turned out to be the most fun he had ever had — making pairs, matching patterns, rolling them into neat little bundles. Mira laughed when he put two wrong socks together on purpose just to see her giggle.

By the end, every sock was matched and the drawer cave was tidy. Mira left Tangle with one single striped sock of his own to keep.

He hugged it happily and tucked himself into the corner of the drawer, the most contented silly sock monster in the world.

From that day on, every laundry day became sock-sorting day, and Tangle was always ready. He knew which socks belonged to which drawer, and he could match a pair by colour, pattern, or texture faster than anyone. He still kept his one striped sock tucked safely at the back. That was his own, and it always made him smile whenever he saw it waiting for him at the end of a good day's work.
