In the shallows of the river below the bridge, there was a curious river stone named Riv who had lived at approximately the same spot for what she estimated was several thousand years, though she was not precise about it.

Riv was smooth. This was the achievement of her long life: she had arrived at this riverbed as a sharp-edged fragment broken from the cliff face upstream, and the water flow had worked her over the centuries into an oval of perfect grey smoothness, comfortable to hold in a hand, comfortable to sit in the current.

The smooth path had been made by the river itself.

Children who came to the bridge would sometimes pick her up — she was a good size for throwing — and feel the smoothness of her surface and reconsider. She was too satisfying to skip. They would hold her for a while, pressing their thumbs along her face, and then place her back in the water with a care they couldn't have explained.

One morning a girl came and sat beside the river and picked up Riv and held her for a very long time. She was thinking about something complicated, Riv sensed — the grip was tight, then loose, then tight again.

At last the girl placed Riv back in the shallow water and stood up.

"Thank you," she said.

Riv did not know what she had done, exactly. She had been a stone in a hand. She had been smooth and cool and present. She had been a thing to hold while thinking.

Perhaps that was enough. Perhaps being available to be held — being reliably there, unchanged — was its own form of usefulness.

The water flow moved around Riv as it always had, wearing her smooth with infinite patience. Above the bridge the sky moved fast. Below it, the river moved at its own pace.

And Riv sat in the shallow water and waited, smooth and grey and ready.


The girl who had held Riv that morning came back in the afternoon. She sat again by the water and picked up a different stone this time — smaller, rougher, still becoming. She turned it in her fingers and felt how much more work the river had to do.

She placed it back carefully and watched the current move over it.
