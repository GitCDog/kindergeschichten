In the toy chest at the end of the bed, among the building bricks and the stuffed giraffe, lived a curious marble named Rolo. He was a deep swirling blue with a tiny silver star floating at his centre, and he had spent many weeks wondering what lay beyond the toy chest.

One evening, the lid of the chest was left slightly open. Rolo peeked out at the wide bedroom floor. It looked enormous — smooth and wooden, with gentle valleys where the floorboards had aged. It looked like the rolling hills he had seen in picture books.

He rolled himself to the edge and — with a small, brave breath — tipped out onto the floor.

He rolled slowly at first, then faster as the floor tilted slightly toward the door. He spun in a gentle arc around a building block, detoured past a sock, and bumped softly into the leg of the bed. The impact sent him curving off in a new direction — toward the hallway.

In the hallway, the floor was a different colour and had a small rug that slowed him down. He bumped into a toy car, then a crayon, then found himself looking up at the whole house from a new angle. The ceiling seemed very high. The walls seemed very far apart.

He rolled into the living room and saw toy land — a whole city of toys the children had built that afternoon: a tower of blocks, a tunnel made of sofa cushions, roads drawn in chalk on paper. Rolo rolled through the tunnel, around the tower, and down the chalk road, admiring every bit of it.

Then, slowly, he turned around and rolled back. Back through the hallway, back into the bedroom, up the slightly tilted floor to the toy chest, and — with a gentle hop — back inside.

The stuffed giraffe looked at him. "You've been out, haven't you?"

"I have," said Rolo, settling happily against the building bricks. "It was wonderful."

The stuffed giraffe settled back against the side of the chest, smiling. Rolo caught the gleam of his own silver star in the faint light from the window and turned himself slowly, watching it move. He thought about the toy city on the floor, the tunnel of cushions, the chalk road. Tomorrow the children would build something new in toy land, and perhaps one day Rolo would roll out to see it again. For now, here was enough.
