Once upon a time, on the wide blue ocean where the waves rolled and the seabirds wheeled overhead, there sailed a little pirate named Pearl. [long pause]
Pearl was eight years old, with a captain's hat three sizes too big and a wooden ship named the Saltwind that she ran almost entirely on her own, with some help from her parrot Marco and a very opinionated seagull who hadn't been invited but refused to leave. She had a map — hand-drawn, slightly coffee-stained — that showed, in very approximate terms, the location of the glowing island. [sighs]
The glowing island was said to light up at night like a lantern — not from fire or torch, but from within, as if the whole island were made of something luminous. No one Pearl had spoken to had ever been there. That was, of course, precisely why she had to go. [beat]
She sailed for three days through ordinary ocean. She got rained on twice. Marco refused to help with the sail and instead spent most of the journey telling her, in excessive detail, about sandwiches he had eaten. The uninvited seagull turned out to be unexpectedly good at spotting weather. [softly]
On the evening of the third day, Pearl was standing at the bow watching the horizon when she saw it.
[with growing realization] A soft, golden glow. Rising from the water ahead, warm and steady as a hearth fire. The glowing island.
It grew as the Saltwind approached — larger, more golden, more extraordinary. The island's beaches shimmered. Its trees held light in their branches like lanterns. Even the sand sparkled underfoot when Pearl stepped ashore. [pacing]
She walked the whole island that night, Marco on her shoulder, the seagull at her heels, and the island's glow surrounding them like the warmest evening you could imagine.
She found no treasure chests. No gold or jewels. [beat] Just beauty. Just this astonishing, quiet, luminous place that no one knew about yet.
[softly] She took nothing. She left no mark. She just sat on the glowing beach and watched the stars above and the light below and felt, absolutely, that some quests give you exactly what you need.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
