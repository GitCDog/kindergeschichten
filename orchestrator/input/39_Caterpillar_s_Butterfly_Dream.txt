Once upon a time, on the underside of a broad green leaf in a garden full of roses and long grass, there lived a magic caterpillar named Coco. [long pause]
Coco was small and green and moved with a gentle rippling motion, like a tiny wave. She spent her days eating leaves — methodically, thoroughly, without apology — and her nights tucked under a leaf blanket, a large fallen leaf she had positioned just so, that kept the dew off and the warmth in. It was, she felt, an extremely good life. [sighs]
But sometimes, in the quiet of the evening, she had a dream. [beat]
In the dream she had wings. Not the flat, crawling life of the leaf and the stem — but wings, and air, and height, and the whole garden seen from above. Colors she didn't have names for. A kind of lightness she had never felt while carrying herself slowly along a branch. [softly]
She didn't tell anyone about the dream. It seemed too large for a caterpillar to say out loud.
One morning she woke with a strange feeling. Her leaf blanket felt too small. The branch beneath her felt too still. Something inside her — something she couldn't name — said: now. [with growing realization]
She began to spin.
She didn't decide to. Her body simply knew. Thread by thread she wrapped herself up, and the leaf blanket she tucked in around the outside for warmth, and she grew still, and dark, and quiet. [long pause]
Inside the darkness she dreamed the butterfly dream more clearly than ever. She felt the wings before she had them. She knew the light before she could see it.
[pacing] And then one morning — warm, bright, perfectly timed — the chrysalis split open.
She unfolded herself into a world that was exactly as wide and colorful and full of air as she had always dreamed.
She lifted from the branch. She rose. Below her the garden opened out — the roses, the long grass, the leaf where she had slept under her leaf blanket for so many small, good nights.
[softly] The dream, it turned out, had been a memory of something she had always been becoming.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
