Once upon a time, on a rocky beach where the ocean came in cold and the seaweed smelled of deep water, there lived a silly seal named Sammy. [long pause]
Sammy had a round body, flippers better suited for swimming than walking, whiskers that vibrated with enthusiasm, and a talent that was, among seals, considered somewhat unusual. Sammy could balance things on his nose. [sighs with the casual pride of someone naturally gifted]
Not on purpose, at first. It had simply happened — a pebble, then a shell, then a small fish that had not appreciated the experience — and Sammy had discovered that his nose was, structurally speaking, excellent for keeping things steady. [beat]
The beach ball arrived one morning, washed up on the tide. It was large and orange and striped and slightly deflated, which gave it an interesting wobble. Sammy sniffed it. He nudged it. It bounced — an ocean bounce, low and slow, the kind that salt air and waterlogged rubber produces.
Sammy balanced it on his nose. [with growing realization]
It stayed.
He walked — shuffled — toward the water. It stayed. He got in the water. It stayed, just barely, adjusted by small precise movements of his nose that he hadn't known he could make. He swam. The beach ball bobbed along, balanced impossibly, as the ocean swells moved underneath him. [pacing]
The other seals came to watch.
Then a curious cormorant came to watch.
Then a small crowd of sandpipers gathered at the waterline, tilting their heads in unison.
Sammy swam in a circle. He did a slow roll — ball leaving the nose, airborne for half a second, landing back with a soft bounce that he caught. [abruptly] A cheer went up from the sandpipers, which from sandpipers sounds like a great deal of quick piping, but the intention was unmistakable.
[softly] Sammy beached himself and let the ball roll off into the foam. He looked at his audience. His whiskers vibrated.
He had not known until today that he had a gift. He was very glad the tide had brought him something to discover it with.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
