Once upon a time, on a wide, sun-warmed beach where the ocean waves came in and out like slow breathing, there lived a curious crab named Coral. [long pause]
Coral had bright orange claws, eyes on stalks that could swivel in any direction, and a very strong opinion about sandcastles. Specifically, that they could be much better than they usually were. [sighs with architectural disappointment]
Most sandcastles, she felt, lacked ambition. A mound with a moat. A tower with a flag. Fine, if you had low standards. Coral did not have low standards. [beat]
She had been planning the sandy castle kingdom for three tides. She knew exactly where to dig — below the high tide line where the sand was damp and held its shape — and exactly what to build: a proper castle with four towers, a great hall in the middle, a drawbridge made of driftwood, and a moat connected directly to a channel that would fill with each incoming ocean wave.
She began at dawn. [pacing]
By midmorning the first tower was up. By noon the walls were standing and a passing sandpiper had stopped to admire them, which Coral found gratifying. By late afternoon the driftwood drawbridge was in place and the moat channel was dug. [softly]
The first wave came in as the sun lowered — running along the channel, filling the moat in a smooth, quiet rush, then retreating. The drawbridge held. The towers stood.
[with growing realization] Coral walked the full perimeter of her sandy castle kingdom, claws raised in satisfaction.
Then she looked at it. Really looked at it. [beat]
It was splendid. It was also empty.
She went to find the hermit crabs who lived under the rocks at the north end of the beach, and the tiny sand hoppers who gathered near the seaweed at the tideline, and the small ghost crab who was always lurking around looking for somewhere to belong.
She brought them all back.
[softly] The sandy castle kingdom, she decided, was considerably improved by having subjects.
By sunset, it was the finest, busiest, most cheerful little kingdom the beach had ever seen. The ocean waves filled the moat on schedule, and everyone agreed it was perfect.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
