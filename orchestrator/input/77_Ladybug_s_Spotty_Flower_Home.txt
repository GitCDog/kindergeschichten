Once upon a time, in a garden full of roses and lavender and the constant hum of bees, there lived a little ladybug named Dot. [long pause]
Dot had seven spots — three on each wing case and one perfectly centered near the top, which she considered the most important spot because it was hers in a way the others weren't, somehow. She was red and round and precise in her movements, and she had been looking for the right flower house for the whole of this summer. [sighs]
Finding the right home, Dot believed, was not a matter to be rushed. [beat]
She had tried the rose — too many thorns near the door, and the perfume in high summer was overwhelming. She had tried the foxglove — beautiful, but vertical, which was impractical. She had tried the sunflower — excellent view, terrible in wind. She had tried the lavender — the bees were wonderful neighbors but the traffic was relentless. [softly]
Today she was trying the hollyhock.
The hollyhock stood at the back of the garden, tall and pink, its flower cups opening from the bottom up. Dot landed on the third flower from the top and walked slowly around the inside of the cup, examining it with the thoroughness of someone who had made this mistake before. [with growing realization]
The floor was smooth and sheltered. The walls curved up to keep the wind out but left an opening to the morning light. The view from the entrance was the full length of the garden — roses to the left, lavender beyond, the old apple tree in the corner with its comfortable branch.
Dot walked around the cup a second time. A third time. [pacing]
She sat very still in the center and listened. The garden sounds — bees, birds, the distant sound of the house — came to her softly, filtered by the hollyhock's petals. The morning light fell in pink and warm. Her spotty wings caught it.
[softly] She had been looking all summer and here, at the back of the garden in the tallest flower, was exactly what she had been looking for.
Some things, she had learned, are worth the search. And they always feel right when you find them.
Dot settled in. She was home.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
