Once upon a time, on a lake so still and clear that it reflected the sky perfectly — clouds, birds, and all — there lived a sweet swan named Seren. [long pause]
Seren was white from the curve of her neck to the tips of her tail feathers, with a beak the orange-red of a late summer sunset. She moved across the graceful lake the way music moves through air — without apparent effort, the result of something precise and practiced made to look completely natural. [sighs with quiet elegance]
She shed feathers, as all swans do. [beat]
Not often — a few each week, loose ones, no longer needed. They drifted from her as she swam, settling on the surface of the graceful lake and floating there — small, white, perfectly formed — before the wind or the current carried them to the reeds at the shore.
Seren had never thought much about this until the morning she saw what the water-vole was doing.
His name was Vincent, and he lived in the bank beneath the largest willow, and he was collecting her feathers. [with growing realization]
Not keeping them — not hoarding them — but placing them. Around the lake edge, in the gaps between the reeds, along the roots of the willow, wherever there was a place that the cold wind came through and small creatures gathered. Feather by feather, he was building insulation. Lining the sheltered spots with white swan-down. [pacing]
Seren glided to the bank and watched him work for a long time. [softly]
"I should have asked," Vincent said, noticing her. "I'm sorry. They were floating already, and they're so warm —"
"You don't need to ask," said Seren.
She looked at her own feathers — the ones she wore and the ones drifting past her on the lake surface. She had always thought of shedding as simply something that happened. She had not thought of it as giving.
[softly] She shook herself gently. Three more feathers drifted free and floated toward the bank.
Vincent collected them with great care.
Seren turned and glided back toward the center of the graceful lake, feeling — surprisingly, wonderfully — lighter.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
