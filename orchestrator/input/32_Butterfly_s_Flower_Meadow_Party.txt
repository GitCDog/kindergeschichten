Once upon a time, in a wide flower meadow where the air smelled of pollen and warm sun and the grass hummed with the busy sound of a thousand tiny wings, there lived a kind butterfly named Bea. [long pause]
Bea had wings the color of a summer afternoon — pale yellow with soft orange edges — and she flew with a gentle, unhurried grace that made every flower she visited feel attended to. She was known throughout the meadow for her patience and her kindness, but most of all for a dream she had carried since she was a caterpillar. [sighs]
She wanted to throw a pollen party. A real one. The kind where everyone came and everyone danced and the whole meadow celebrated being exactly what it was. [beat]
The pollen party, she had decided, would happen at the very center of the flower meadow, on the longest day of summer. She began sending invitations two weeks early — a petal left at each creature's door, sprinkled with a dusting of golden pollen so everyone knew it was from her.
The preparations were considerable. Bea collected pollen from every flower in the meadow — rose and lavender, clover and foxglove, poppy and daisy — and arranged it in small neat piles, each one a different color, each one smelling like its flower. She strung wild grasses between the tallest stems as bunting. [pacing]
On the day of the pollen party, they came from every corner of the meadow. Bees — lots of bees, delighted, barely able to believe such a thing existed. Moths who had heard about it and traveled through the morning to arrive in time. Three beetles in their very best shell-shine. A dragonfly who hovered importantly overhead and pretended she hadn't been looking forward to it for days.
[softly] They danced in the sun. They traded pollen. The bees showed everyone a special figure-eight waggle that turned into a group activity of such infectious silliness that even the beetles joined in.
[with growing realization] Bea landed on a tall cornflower in the middle of it all and watched — and understood that the best thing you can make is not a thing at all, but a moment when everyone feels they belong.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
