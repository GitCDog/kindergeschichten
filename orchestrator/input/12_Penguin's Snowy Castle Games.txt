Once upon a time, at the very bottom of the world, in a glittering land of white and blue, there lived a playful penguin named Percy. [long pause] Percy's ice castle was the grandest building at the South Pole — every wall made of thick, clear ice that sparkled like diamonds, every tower catching the pale sunlight and throwing it around in rainbows. Percy was very proud of it. But what he loved most wasn't the castle. [beat] What Percy loved most was a good snowball fight. He spent the whole week before Saturday preparing. He rolled snowballs of every size — tiny ones for quick precise throws, medium ones for the middle of the battle, and one absolutely enormous one for the grand finale. He built snow walls and marked out a playing field with lines pressed into the snow by his flipper. He made a scoreboard, though he secretly planned not to keep proper score, because everyone needed to win. [sighs contentedly] On Saturday morning, the guests arrived. Four more penguins, waddling in a cheerful line. Two arctic foxes who had traveled a very long way, their orange tails vivid against the white landscape. And, shuffling slowly up the path — a polar bear named Gus. [softly] Gus had never played before. He had enormous paws and an extremely uncertain expression. "The rules are simple," Percy announced, hands on his hips. "Everyone plays. No one gets left out. And whoever laughs the most wins." The battle began. [pacing] Snowballs flew in every direction. Percy slid on his belly to dodge an incoming throw and knocked over two of his own snow walls entirely. The arctic foxes ran in opposite directions, accidentally throwing at each other. The other penguins formed a cheering squad and then forgot to cheer because they were too busy throwing. And then Gus wound up — slowly, carefully — and threw. [abruptly] The snowball left his paw like a comet. It sailed in a long, graceful arc. It missed everyone completely, curved gently to the right — and demolished the highest tower of the ice castle with a magnificent, glittering crash. Everyone froze. [beat] Then everyone laughed. Percy laughed so hard he fell over. The foxes howled. The penguins slid into each other with delight. "I'm so sorry!" Gus said, looking absolutely horrified. "Are you kidding?" Percy said, wiping tears off his beak. "That was the greatest throw I have ever seen in my life." They rebuilt the tower together that afternoon, laughing every single moment. The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
