Once upon a time, in a gentle meadow where the clover grew thick and the bees hummed their slow summer songs, there lived a sweet lamb named Lulu. [long pause]
Lulu had wool so soft that birds sometimes landed on her back thinking she was a particularly comfortable cloud. She didn't mind. She was, by nature, an extremely agreeable lamb. She liked warm mornings, the smell of fresh grass, and lying very still in the sun. [sighs pleasantly]
What Lulu loved most of all, though, was looking up at the clouds. [beat]
She had a gift for it. While the other lambs chased each other and practiced their baaing and generally got up to the energetic business of being young, Lulu would find her spot in the meadow, fold her legs neatly beneath her, and look up.
The clouds were never the same twice. That was the thing. Each one was a story happening once and then gone — a fluffy castle that became a rabbit that became a fish that simply dissolved into blue. [softly]
One afternoon, a particularly magnificent fluffy cloud drifted overhead — enormous, layered, its underside bright white and its top edged in the gold of the lowering sun. To Lulu it looked like a bed. The softest, most perfect bed imaginable. Wide as the meadow, deep as a dream.
[with growing realization] She closed her eyes. She imagined lying on it — the coolness of high air, the silence of being above everything, the whole gentle meadow spread below, small and green and peaceful.
She felt herself growing heavy. The sun was warm. The bees hummed. The clover smelled of summer.
[softly] She was asleep before the fluffy cloud had finished moving across the sky, and in her dream she was up there, floating, all the world quiet beneath her, the wool on her back warm as sunlight and soft as the best kind of nothing.
She slept until dinner. The other lambs found her curled in the meadow grass, smiling.
Some of the best journeys, they decided, happen with your eyes closed.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
