Once upon a time, in a great hollow beech tree at the edge of a quiet wood, there lived a cozy owl family. [long pause]
There was Father Owl, who had magnificent ear tufts and a deep, thoughtful voice. There was Mother Owl, who was considerably smarter than she let on and knew exactly where every mouse in the wood had been last Thursday. And there were three owlets — Button, Pip, and little Wren — who were at the age when sleeping felt like a waste of time and bedtime was an argument waiting to happen. [sighs]
Every night, after the evening hunt and the grooming of feathers and the arrangement of the nest, Father Owl told the star stories. [beat]
It had been his father's tradition, and his father's before that — because owls, who spend their nights awake, know the stars in a way that daytime creatures do not. Each constellation had a story. The Great Bear. The Hunter. The Swan. The little cluster that looked like a dipper full of sky-water. [softly]
"Again," Button always said, before the story was even finished.
"Again," agreed Pip, shuffling closer.
Little Wren said nothing because she was always asleep before the end, which no one mentioned because it would have embarrassed her.
[with growing realization] Tonight, Father Owl began the story of the Swan — how she had been placed in the sky as a reward for a great kindness, and how she flew across the heavens on wings made of actual starlight, never landing, never tiring, always moving through the dark.
"Does she get lonely?" Button asked.
Father Owl looked at the stars through the opening of the hollow. [pacing] "She has the whole sky," he said. "And everyone below who looks up and sees her."
"That's not the same as company," Button said.
[beat] Father Owl was quiet for a moment. "No," he said. "It isn't. That is why we are here together."
He ruffled his feathers around all three owlets, Button and Pip pressing in warm on each side, little Wren already dreaming. [softly]
Above the beech tree the star stories continued without words — the Swan flying on, the Bear turning slowly, the whole night sky wheeling with the patience of things that have been doing this for a very long time.
Inside the hollow, the owl family slept.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
