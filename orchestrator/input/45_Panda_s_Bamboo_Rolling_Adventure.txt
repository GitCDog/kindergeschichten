Once upon a time, in a bamboo forest so tall and green and cool that even the sunlight came in filtered and gentle, there lived a playful panda named Ping. [long pause]
Ping had the round, soft, black-and-white look of a creature designed specifically to be adored, and he was fully aware of this and found it somewhat embarrassing. What he preferred was not to be admired but to roll. [sighs happily]
Rolling was Ping's greatest joy. Not tumbling accidentally — proper, intentional rolling. Down the rolling hills at the edge of the bamboo forest, where the slope was long and smooth and covered in the softest grass. [beat]
He had perfected the technique over many summers: arms tucked in, chin down, a small push with one back paw, and then — away. The world becoming a blur of green and sky and green and sky, the bamboo forest rushing past on both sides, the wind in his ears, the smell of crushed grass, the particular joy of going downhill with complete commitment.
At the bottom of the rolling hills he would come to rest against the old fence post — always the same one — lie on his back, look up at the sky through the treetops, and think: that was excellent. [softly]
One afternoon he arrived at the top of the hill to find a young fox sitting there, watching the slope doubtfully.
"Have you tried rolling?" Ping asked.
"I don't think foxes roll," said the fox. [with growing realization]
Ping considered this. "Have you checked?"
The fox had not, in fact, checked.
[pacing] They rolled together. The fox was surprisingly good at it — longer-bodied, she rolled faster, and arrived at the fence post a full second before Ping, where she lay in the grass making a sound he recognized immediately as a laugh she hadn't expected to have.
They spent the whole afternoon on the rolling hills. A badger joined them. Then two rabbits. Then a hedgehog who rolled more slowly but with tremendous enthusiasm.
[softly] At the top of the last run, Ping looked at his rolling hills full of unlikely friends and thought: joy is never wasted when it's shared.
The end. Sweet dreams, little one.
